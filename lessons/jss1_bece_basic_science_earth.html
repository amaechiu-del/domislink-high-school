Introduction to Earth

The Earth is the third planet from the Sun in our solar system. It is the only known planet that supports life.

The Earth is made up of several layers, including the crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core.

  • The crust is the outermost layer of the Earth, ranging in thickness from 5-70 km.
  • The mantle is the layer beneath the crust, making up about 84% of the Earth's volume.
  • The outer core is a liquid layer of iron and nickel, about 2,250 km thick.
  • The inner core is a solid, iron-nickel alloy at the center of the Earth, with a temperature of about 5,000°C.

Examples of the Earth's features in Nigeria include:

  • The Obudu Plateau, which is a mountainous region in Cross River State.
  • The Niger Delta, which is a network of rivers, creeks, and wetlands in southern Nigeria.

Fun Fact

The Earth is not a perfect sphere, but an oblate spheroid, meaning it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator.
