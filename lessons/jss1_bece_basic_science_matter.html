Introduction to Matter

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. It can be found all around us, from the air we breathe to the chairs we sit on.

Matter can exist in three main states: solid, liquid, and gas. Here are some key points to note about matter:

  • Matter can change from one state to another through processes such as melting, freezing, and evaporation.
  • The particles that make up matter are always in motion, even in solids.
  • Matter can be classified into different types, including elements, compounds, and mixtures.

Here are two Nigerian examples of matter:

  • Petroleum, which is a major source of revenue for Nigeria, is a type of matter that exists in a liquid state.
  • The rocks found in the Jos Plateau in Nigeria are examples of solid matter.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the human body is made up of approximately 60% water, which is a type of matter? This highlights the importance of matter in our daily lives.

Now, let's test your understanding of matter with these practice questions:

  1. What are the three main states of matter?
    1. Solid, liquid, and gas
    2. Solid, liquid, and plasma
    3. Gas, liquid, and plasma
    Answer: a) Solid, liquid, and gas
  2. What is the process called when a solid changes to a liquid?
    1. Melting
    2. Freezing
    3. Evaporation
    Answer: a) Melting
  3. What type of matter is air?
    1. Solid
    2. Liquid
    3. Gas
    Answer: c) Gas
