Composition in English Language

Composition is a type of writing that involves creating an original piece of work, such as a story, poem, or essay. It is an essential skill in English language learning, as it helps students develop their writing, critical thinking, and communication skills.

The key elements of composition include:

  • Introduction: This is the beginning of the composition, where the writer introduces the topic or theme.
  • Body: This is the main part of the composition, where the writer develops the idea or theme.
  • Conclusion: This is the end of the composition, where the writer summarizes the main points and draws a conclusion.

Here are two Nigerian examples of composition:

Example 1: A student writes a story about a young boy who grows up in a rural village in Nigeria and becomes a successful doctor.

Example 2: A student writes a poem about the beauty of the Niger Delta region and the importance of preserving the environment.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the first Nigerian novel, "Things Fall Apart" by Chinua Achebe, is a classic example of composition in English language? It tells the story of Okonkwo, a Nigerian man who struggles with the changes brought about by British colonialism.

Practice Questions:

1. What is the main purpose of a composition?

Answer: The main purpose of a composition is to convey a message, tell a story, or express an idea or opinion.

2. What are the three main elements of a composition?

Answer: Introduction, body, and conclusion.

3. Who is the author of the Nigerian novel "Things Fall Apart"?

Answer: Chinua Achebe.
