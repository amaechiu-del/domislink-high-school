Introduction to Grammar

Grammar is the set of rules that govern the structure of a language. It deals with the formation of sentences, phrases, and words.

Some key aspects of grammar include:

  • Parts of speech (nouns, verbs, adjectives, etc.)
  • Sentence structure (subject-verb-object)
  • Tense and aspect (present, past, future, etc.)

Let's consider two examples from Nigeria:

In Nigerian English, we might say "I am going to the market" to indicate a present continuous action. However, in standard English, the correct sentence would be "I am going to the market" to indicate a planned future action.

Another example is the use of the phrase "how far" as a greeting in Nigerian English, which is equivalent to "how are you" in standard English.

Fun Fact

Practice Questions

1. Identify the parts of speech in the sentence "The dog is barking loudly."

Answer: The (article), dog (noun), is (verb), barking (verb), loudly (adverb)

2. Which of the following sentences is in the present continuous tense?

A) I go to school

B) I am going to school

C) I went to school

Answer: B) I am going to school

3. Rewrite the sentence "The teacher give us homework" in standard English.

Answer: The teacher gives us homework
