Introduction to Literature

Literature is the art of using language to create written works that express thoughts, feelings, and ideas. It involves the study of various forms of writing, including poetry, drama, and prose.

Some key aspects of literature include:

  • Imagery: the use of language to create vivid mental images
  • Symbolism: the use of objects, colors, or other elements to represent abstract ideas
  • Theme: the underlying message or idea of a literary work

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

The poem "The Panther" by J.P. Clark, which uses imagery to describe the animal's power and beauty, and the novel "Things Fall Apart" by Chinua Achebe, which explores the theme of cultural identity and change.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the word "literature" comes from the Latin word "littera," meaning "letter"? This refers to the written word, which is the primary medium of literary expression.

Practice Questions

1. What is the primary function of imagery in literature?

Answer: To create vivid mental images in the reader's mind.

2. Which Nigerian novel explores the theme of cultural identity and change?

Answer: "Things Fall Apart" by Chinua Achebe.

3. What is the term for the use of objects, colors, or other elements to represent abstract ideas in literature?

Answer: Symbolism.
