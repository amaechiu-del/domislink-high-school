Introduction to Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that deals with variables and their relationships. It involves the use of symbols, equations, and formulas to solve problems.

In algebra, we use variables to represent unknown values. These variables are often represented by letters such as x, y, and z.

  • Variables: These are letters or symbols that represent unknown values.
  • Constants: These are numbers that do not change value.
  • Equations: These are statements that say two things are equal.

For example, in Nigeria, a farmer has 15 bags of rice to sell at the market. If he sells x bags of rice at ₦500 each, how much money will he make in total? This can be represented by the equation: Total Money = 500x.

Another example is a taxi driver in Lagos who charges ₦5000 for a trip, plus an extra ₦500 for every hour of delay. If the trip takes x hours, the total cost can be represented by the equation: Total Cost = 5000 + 500x.

Fun Fact

Did you know that algebra was first developed by the ancient Babylonians, Egyptians, and Greeks? The word "algebra" comes from the Arabic word "al-jabr", which means "reunion of broken parts".

Practice Questions

1. Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 11

Answer: x = 3

2. Solve for y: y - 3 = 7

Answer: y = 10

3. Solve for z: z/2 = 9

Answer: z = 18
