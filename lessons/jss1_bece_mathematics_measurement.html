Measurement in Mathematics

Measurement is the process of assigning a number to a quantity, such as length, mass, or capacity, using a standard unit.

Here are some key points about measurement:

  • Length: measured in units such as meters, centimeters, or kilometers
  • Mass: measured in units such as grams, kilograms, or tons
  • Capacity: measured in units such as liters, milliliters, or cubic meters

Let's look at some examples from Nigeria:

In Lagos, a farmer measured the length of his farm to be 500 meters. He wanted to put up a fence around the farm, so he needed to calculate the perimeter.

In Abuja, a vendor at the market measured the mass of a bag of rice to be 25 kilograms. She wanted to know how much to charge her customers per kilogram.

Practice Questions

Try to answer these questions on your own, then check the answers below:

  1. A bookshelf is 2 meters long. If it is placed next to another bookshelf of the same length, what is the total length?
  2. A bucket can hold 10 liters of water. If 5 liters of water are already in the bucket, what percentage of the bucket is filled?
  3. A person weighs 50 kilograms. If they gain 10 kilograms, what is their new weight in kilograms?

Answers:

  1. 4 meters
  2. 50%
  3. 60 kilograms
