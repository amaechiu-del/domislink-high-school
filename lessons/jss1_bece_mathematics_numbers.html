Introduction to Numbers

In this lesson, we will be exploring the world of numbers. Numbers are all around us and are used to represent quantities, amounts, and measurements.

Here are some key points to note about numbers:

  • Numbers can be whole (e.g. 1, 2, 3) or decimal (e.g. 1.5, 2.7)
  • Numbers can be positive (e.g. 5, 10) or negative (e.g. -3, -9)
  • Numbers can be used to represent different units of measurement (e.g. length, weight, time)

Let's consider two examples from Nigeria:

In Lagos, a basket of tomatoes can cost ₦500. If a seller has 5 baskets, how much will they cost in total? The answer is ₦2500.

In Abuja, a taxi ride can cost ₦1000. If a passenger takes 3 rides in a day, how much will they spend in total? The answer is ₦3000.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the number zero (0) was invented in ancient India? It was a major breakthrough in mathematics and allowed for the development of more complex mathematical concepts.

Practice Questions

Try to answer these questions on your own:

  1. If a pencil costs ₦50, how much will 10 pencils cost?
  2. A bookshelf has 5 shelves, and each shelf can hold 8 books. How many books can the bookshelf hold in total?
  3. A water tank can hold 1000 liters of water. If 300 liters are already in the tank, how much more water can be added?

Answers:

  1. ₦500
  2. 40 books
  3. 700 liters
