Introduction to Law

Law is a set of rules created by a country or state to regulate the behavior of its citizens. It is designed to maintain social order, protect citizens' rights, and promote the general welfare of society.

Key Elements of Law

  • Rules and regulations
  • Enforcement by authorities
  • Consequences for breaking the law

Examples of law in Nigeria include:

  • The Nigerian Constitution, which is the supreme law of the land
  • The Road Traffic Act, which regulates the use of roads and vehicles in Nigeria

Fun Fact

Did you know that the first law school in Nigeria was established in 1962 at the University of Lagos?

Practice Questions

Test your understanding of law with these questions:

  1. What is the main purpose of law in a society?
    1. To punish criminals
    2. To maintain social order and protect citizens' rights
    3. To generate revenue for the government
    Answer: b) To maintain social order and protect citizens' rights
  2. Which of the following is an example of a law in Nigeria?
    1. The Nigerian Constitution
    2. The Bible
    3. The Quran
    Answer: a) The Nigerian Constitution
  3. What happens to someone who breaks the law?
    1. They are rewarded with a prize
    2. They are ignored by the authorities
    3. They face consequences, such as fines or imprisonment
    Answer: c) They face consequences, such as fines or imprisonment
