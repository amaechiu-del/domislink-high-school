Classification in Basic Science

Classification is the process of grouping living things into categories based on their characteristics. This helps us to understand the relationships between different organisms and to identify them more easily.

The classification of living things is based on the following categories:

  • Kingdom: The highest level of classification, which divides living things into broad groups such as animals, plants, and fungi.
  • Phylum: A level of classification that groups living things based on their body structure and development.
  • Class: A level of classification that groups living things based on their physical characteristics and behavior.

Examples of classification in Nigeria include:

  • The Baobab tree, which is classified as a plant in the kingdom Plantae, phylum Angiospermophyta, and class Magnoliopsida.
  • The African Elephant, which is classified as an animal in the kingdom Animalia, phylum Chordata, and class Mammalia.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the classification of living things is not fixed and can change over time as new discoveries are made? For example, the platypus was once classified as a mammal, but is now classified as a monotreme, a group of mammals that lay eggs.
