Earth and Space

Welcome to our lesson on Earth and Space. In this lesson, we will be exploring the different components of our universe.

The Solar System

The solar system consists of the sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and other objects. The planets in our solar system are divided into two categories: terrestrial planets and gas giants.

Components of the Universe

The universe is made up of several components, including:

  • Stars: huge balls of hot, glowing gas
  • Galaxies: massive collections of stars, gas, and dust
  • Planets: large objects that orbit around stars

Examples of galaxies include the Milky Way, which contains our solar system, and the Andromeda Galaxy, which is the closest major galaxy to the Milky Way. In Nigeria, we can see the stars and constellations in the night sky from places like the Obudu Plateau and the Jos Plateau. The National Space Research and Development Agency (NASRDA) in Abuja is also involved in space research and development.

Importance of Space Exploration

Space exploration is important for several reasons, including:

  • Improving our understanding of the universe
  • Developing new technologies
  • Expanding our knowledge of the Earth's place in the universe

For example, the Nigerian government has launched several satellites into space, including the NigeriaSat-1 and the NigeriaSat-X, which provide important data for agriculture, forestry, and disaster management.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the International Space Station orbits the Earth at an altitude of around 250 miles (400 kilometers)? It takes about 90 minutes to complete one orbit around the Earth.
