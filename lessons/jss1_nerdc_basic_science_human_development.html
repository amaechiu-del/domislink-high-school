Human Development

Human development refers to the process by which humans grow, develop, and mature from conception to old age. It involves physical, emotional, and psychological changes that occur throughout an individual's life.

The stages of human development include:

  • Prenatal development: from conception to birth
  • Infancy: from birth to 1 year
  • Childhood: from 1 to 12 years
  • Adolescence: from 13 to 19 years
  • Adulthood: from 20 to 64 years
  • Old age: from 65 years and above

Examples of human development in Nigeria include:

  • The National Population Commission's efforts to reduce infant mortality rates through improved healthcare services
  • The Federal Government's introduction of free education for all children, aimed at promoting cognitive development and empowering future generations

Fun Fact

Did you know that the human brain continues to develop until the age of 25? This is why many experts consider young adulthood a critical period for cognitive and emotional development.
