Living and Non-Living Things

Basic Science - JSS1 (NERDC Syllabus)

Key Differences

  • Living things grow, move, breathe, reproduce, and respond to changes around them.
  • Non-living things do not grow, breathe, reproduce, or respond to their environment.
  • Living things need food, water, and air to survive. Non-living things do not need any of these.
  • Living things have cells and can die. Non-living things do not have cells and cannot die.
  • Examples of living things: plants, animals, humans, insects.
  • Examples of non-living things: rocks, water, plastic, sand, air.

Nigerian Examples

1. Iroko Tree (Living): The Iroko tree is a living plant found in Nigeria. It grows tall, produces leaves, and reproduces by seeds. It needs sunlight, water, and nutrients from the soil.

2. Granite Rock (Non-Living): Granite rocks are common in places like Jos, Plateau State. They do not grow, breathe, or reproduce. They remain the same for many years unless broken by weather or humans.

🧠 Fun Fact: Did you know that a mimosa pudica (touch-me-not plant) is a living thing that moves its leaves when you touch it? Some non-living things like robots can also move, but they don't grow or reproduce!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which of the following is a living thing? (a) Stone (b) Yam (c) Plastic cup (d) Sand
    Answer: (b) Yam — because it grows and can sprout.
  2. Question 2: Name one non-living thing found in your classroom and explain why it is non-living.
    Answer: A desk — it does not grow, breathe, or reproduce. It stays the same unless damaged.
  3. Question 3: Why is a goat considered a living thing while a bicycle is not?
    Answer: A goat grows, eats, breathes, and can have babies (reproduces). A bicycle has none of these characteristics.
