Introduction to Matter

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. It can be found all around us, from the air we breathe to the chairs we sit on.

States of Matter

The three main states of matter are:

  • Solid: Has a fixed shape and volume, like a rock or a book.
  • Liquid: Takes the shape of its container, like water or oil.
  • Gas: Has neither a fixed shape nor a fixed volume, like air or helium.

Examples in Nigeria

Matter is all around us in Nigeria. For example:

  • The water in the Niger Delta is an example of a liquid.
  • The stones used to build the National Mosque in Abuja are examples of solids.

Fun Fact

Did you know that matter can change from one state to another? For example, when you boil water, it changes from a liquid to a gas. This process is called phase change.

Practice Questions

Test your understanding of matter with these questions:

  1. What is the definition of matter?
    1. Anything that has mass and takes up space
    2. Anything that has no mass and takes up no space
    3. Anything that has mass but takes up no space
    4. Anything that has no mass but takes up space
    Answer: a) Anything that has mass and takes up space
  2. What are the three main states of matter?
    1. Solid, liquid, and gas
    2. Solid, liquid, and plasma
    3. Gas, liquid, and plasma
    4. Solid, plasma, and energy
    Answer: a) Solid, liquid, and gas
  3. What is an example of a solid in Nigeria?
    1. The water in the Niger Delta
    2. The stones used to build the National Mosque in Abuja
    3. The air in the Sahara Desert
    4. The oil in the Niger Delta
    Answer: b) The stones used to build the National Mosque in Abuja
