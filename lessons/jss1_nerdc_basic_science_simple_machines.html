Simple Machines

Simple machines are devices that make work easier by changing the direction or magnitude of the force applied. There are six types of simple machines: lever, pulley, wheel and axle, inclined plane, wedge, and screw.

The following are explanations of simple machines in bullet points:

  • Lever: A lever is a simple machine that consists of a rigid bar which is free to move or pivot around a fixed point called the fulcrum.
  • Pulley: A pulley is a simple machine that consists of a wheel with a grooved rim and a rope, cable, or chain wrapped around it.
  • Wheel and axle: A wheel and axle is a simple machine that consists of a circular wheel attached to a central axle.
  • Inclined plane: An inclined plane is a simple machine that is a flat surface whose angle relative to the horizontal plane is not zero.
  • Wedge: A wedge is a simple machine that is used to split or separate objects.
  • Screw: A screw is a simple machine that consists of a cylindrical body with a helical ridge.

Examples of simple machines in Nigeria include:

  • The use of a wheelbarrow to carry loads, which is an example of a wheel and axle.
  • The use of a hoe to lift and throw soil, which is an example of a lever.

Fun Fact: The first simple machine to be discovered was the lever, which was discovered by the ancient Greek mathematician Archimedes.
