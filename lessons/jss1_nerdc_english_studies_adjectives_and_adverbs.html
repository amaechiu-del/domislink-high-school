English Studies: Adjectives and Adverbs

Diagram: How Adjectives and Adverbs function in a sentence.

1. Adjectives

An adjective is a word that describes, identifies, or quantifies a noun or a pronoun.

  • They tell us more about the size, color, shape, or quality of a person or thing.
  • They usually come before the noun they describe.
  • Example 1: The diligent student won a scholarship to King's College.
  • Example 2: Lagos is a noisy city with many tall buildings.

2. Adverbs

An adverb is a word that modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.

  • They tell us how, when, where, or to what extent an action happens.
  • Many adverbs end in -ly (though not all do).
  • Example 1: Amina danced gracefully during the Durbar festival in Kano.
  • Example 2: The Eyo masquerade moved very quickly through the streets.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "Good" is an adjective, but "Well" is usually an adverb. You don't say "He plays football good"; you say "He plays football well!"

Practice Questions

1. Identify the adjective in this sentence: "The delicious jollof rice was served at the wedding."

2. Identify the adverb in this sentence: "Tunde finished his homework quietly in the library."

3. Choose the correct word: "The eagle flies ________ (high / highly) in the sky."

Answers:
1. Delicious (describes the rice).
2. Quietly (tells us how he finished).
3. High (Adverb of place/manner).
