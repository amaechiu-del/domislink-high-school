English Studies: Understanding Conjunctions

In English Studies, we use special words to glue our thoughts together. These "glue words" are called Conjunctions.

The "Conjunction Bridge" connecting two separate ideas.

What is a Conjunction?

  • A conjunction is a part of speech used to connect words, phrases, or clauses.
  • Coordinating Conjunctions: These join words or groups of words that are equal in importance (Example: and, but, or, nor, for, yet, so).
  • Subordinating Conjunctions: These connect a dependent clause to an independent clause (Example: because, although, if, since, while).

Nigerian Context Examples:

1. "Chidi bought a loaf of bread and a bottle of minerals at the kiosk."

2. "Amaka did not go to the market because it was raining heavily in Enugu."

Fun Fact: You can remember the seven most common coordinating conjunctions using the acronym FANBOYS: For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So!

Practice Questions

  1. Identify the conjunction in this sentence: "Tunde wanted to play football, but he had to do his homework."
  2. Choose the correct word to fill the gap: "Would you like to eat pounded yam ____ amala for dinner?" (and / or)
  3. True or False: The word 'because' is a conjunction used to show a reason.

Answers: 1. but | 2. or | 3. True
