Direct and Indirect Speech

Understanding the Difference

In English, there are two main ways to tell someone what another person said:

  • Direct Speech: This repeats the exact words spoken by a person. We use quotation marks ("...") to enclose the words.
  • Indirect Speech (Reported Speech): This reports the meaning of what someone said without using their exact words. We usually change the tense and pronouns.

Key Rules for Changing to Indirect Speech

  • Remove Quotation Marks: We drop the " " and often add the word "that".
  • Change Pronouns: "I" might become "he" or "she" depending on who is speaking.
  • Backshift Tenses: Usually, the present tense changes to the past tense (e.g., is becomes was).
  • Change Time Markers: "Now" becomes "then," and "tomorrow" becomes "the next day."

Nigerian Examples

Example 1:
Direct: "I am going to Balogun Market," said Auntie Ifeoma.
Indirect: Auntie Ifeoma said that she was going to Balogun Market.

Example 2:
Direct: "We have finished our Jollof rice," the children shouted.
Indirect: The children shouted that they had finished their Jollof rice.

💡 Fun Fact: Direct speech is like a "replay" of a video, while indirect speech is like a "summary" of the movie! In many Nigerian languages, we use special particles to show we are reporting someone else's words, just like we use "that" in English.
