Essay Writing

English Studies (NERDC Syllabus for JSS1)

What is an Essay?

An essay is a piece of continuous writing where you express your thoughts, ideas, or experiences on a specific topic in an organized way.

Types of Essays

  • Narrative Essay: This tells a story or recounts an event.
  • Descriptive Essay: This describes a person, place, or object in detail.
  • Expository Essay: This explains a process or provides information on a topic.
  • Argumentative Essay: This aims to persuade the reader to agree with a specific point of view.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (Narrative): "My First Day at Secondary School in Lagos." (Focuses on telling the story of your arrival and feelings.)

Example 2 (Descriptive): "A Traditional Wedding Ceremony in my Village." (Focuses on describing the sights, sounds, and vibrant colors of the event.)

💡 Fun Fact: The word "Essay" comes from the French word "essayer", which means "to try" or "to attempt." When you write an essay, you are "trying" to explain your point!

Practice Questions

1. Which part of an essay captures the reader's attention and introduces the topic?

2. If you are asked to write about "How to prepare Jollof Rice," what type of essay are you writing?

3. Where should you summarize your main points in an essay?

Answers:
1. The Introduction.
2. An Expository Essay (because it explains a process).
3. The Conclusion.
