Letter Writing

JSS1 English Studies (NERDC Curriculum)

Letter writing is a way of communicating our thoughts, feelings, or information to someone who is far away. Even in the age of emails and WhatsApp, knowing how to write a proper letter is a vital skill for every student.

Letters travel across distances to deliver your message!

Types of Letters

  • Informal Letters: These are personal letters written to people we know very well, such as parents, siblings, cousins, and friends.
  • Formal Letters: These are official letters written to people in positions of authority, such as the School Principal, a Local Government Chairman, or a Bank Manager.

Features of an Informal Letter

  • Writer's Address: Written at the top right-hand corner.
  • Date: Written immediately below the address.
  • Salutation: For example: Dear Tunde, or Dearest Mum,
  • The Body: Divided into the Introduction, Content, and Conclusion.
  • Closing: Usually Yours sincerely, followed by your first name.
💡 Fun Fact: Before postage stamps were invented in 1840, the person receiving the letter had to pay for the delivery, not the person sending it!

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Informal Letter (To a Friend)

12 Ajayi Street,
Ikeja, Lagos State.
14th October, 2023.

Dear Chidi,

How are you and your family? I am writing to tell you about our Inter-House Sports competition held last week. My house, Blue House, came first! I won a silver medal in the 100m race. I wish you were there to see it.

Extend my greetings to your parents. I hope to see you during the Christmas holidays.

Yours sincerely,
Bisi

Example 2: Formal Letter (To a Principal)

JSS 1B,
Government Secondary School,
Wuse, Abuja.
20th October, 2023.

The Principal,
Government Secondary School,
Wuse, Abuja.

Dear Sir,

APPLICATION FOR SICK LEAVE

I am writing to request permission to be absent from school for two days, starting from tomorrow, 21st October. This is because I have a severe bout of malaria and my doctor has advised me to rest.

I promise to catch up on all missed lessons as soon as I return.

Yours faithfully,
(Signature)
Emeka Okoro

Practice Questions

  1. Where is the writer's address located in a letter?
  2. What is the difference between the closing of a formal letter and an informal letter?
  3. Name one person you would write a formal letter to.

  1. The writer's address is located at the top right-hand corner of the page.
  2. Informal letters usually end with "Yours sincerely" and your first name, while formal letters end with "Yours faithfully," a signature, and your full name.
  3. The School Principal (or any official like a Governor, Manager, or Teacher).
