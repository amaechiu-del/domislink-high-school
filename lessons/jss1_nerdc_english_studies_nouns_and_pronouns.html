English Studies: Nouns and Pronouns

Wait for the animation to see the noun change into a pronoun!

1. What is a Noun?

A Noun is a naming word. It names people, places, animals, things, ideas, or qualities.

  • Common Nouns: General names like boy, city, school.
  • Proper Nouns: Specific names (always start with capital letters) like Nigeria, Monday, Emeka.
  • Abstract Nouns: Things you cannot touch like honesty, love, wisdom.

2. What is a Pronoun?

A Pronoun is a word used in place of a noun to avoid repeating the noun over and over again.

  • Personal Pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they.
  • Possessive Pronouns: Mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1:
Sentence: Obinna is a fast runner. Obinna won the race.
Better: Obinna is a fast runner. He won the race.
(Here, "Obinna" is the Noun, and "He" is the Pronoun.)
Example 2:
Sentence: Lagos is very crowded because Lagos is a commercial hub.
Better: Lagos is very crowded because it is a commercial hub.
(Here, "Lagos" is the Noun, and "it" is the Pronoun.)
💡 Fun Fact: The word "I" is the shortest and oldest word in the English language, and it is also the most commonly used pronoun!
