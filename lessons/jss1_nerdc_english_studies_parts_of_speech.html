The Eight Parts of Speech Working Together

What are Parts of Speech?

In English, every word we speak or write belongs to a specific category based on the job it does in a sentence. There are eight main parts:

  • Noun: Names of persons, places, animals, or things. (e.g., Lagos, table, student)
  • Pronoun: Words used instead of nouns to avoid repetition. (e.g., he, she, they, it)
  • Verb: Words that show action or a state of being. (e.g., run, eat, is, dance)
  • Adjective: Words that describe or give more information about a noun. (e.g., beautiful, tall, blue)
  • Adverb: Words that describe a verb, adjective, or another adverb. (e.g., quickly, very, loudly)
  • Preposition: Words that show the relationship or position of a noun. (e.g., on, under, beside)
  • Conjunction: Joining words that connect sentences or phrases. (e.g., and, but, because)
  • Interjection: Words used to express sudden feelings or emotions. (e.g., Wow! Ouch! Oh!)

Nigerian Examples

  1. Obinna bought a delicious suya and greedily ate it.
    (Noun: Obinna/suya, Adjective: delicious, Adverb: greedily)
  2. Hey! Tunde is under the mango tree.
    (Interjection: Hey, Noun: Tunde/mango, Preposition: under)

💡 Fun Fact

The most commonly used noun in the English language is "time", while the most commonly used word overall is "the"!
