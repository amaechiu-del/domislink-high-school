Reading Comprehension

Reading Comprehension is the ability to read a text, process it, and understand its meaning. It is not just about pronouncing words correctly; it is about knowing what the author is trying to say.

Key Skills for Effective Reading

  • Skimming: Running your eyes quickly over a text to get the "gist" or the main idea.
  • Scanning: Looking for specific information, such as a date, a name, or a place.
  • Context Clues: Using the words around an unknown word to guess its meaning.
  • Literal Understanding: Identifying information that is clearly stated in the text.
  • Inference: "Reading between the lines" to understand things the author didn't say directly.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The New Yam Festival
"During the New Yam Festival in Ogidi, the villagers gather at the village square. The oldest man in the community offers the first slice of yam to the ancestors before anyone else eats. This signifies gratitude for a harvest."
Comprehension Question: Why does the oldest man eat first? Answer: To show gratitude to the ancestors.

Example 2: A Trip to Balogun Market
"Tunde gripped his mother’s hand tightly. The shouts of 'Fine fabric!' and the heavy smell of fried akara filled the air. He realized that if he let go for even a second, the sea of people would swallow him up."
Inference Question: How does Tunde feel? Answer: He feels anxious or overwhelmed by the crowd.

💡 Fun Fact: Research shows that reading for just 15 minutes a day can expose you to over 1 million words in a year!

Practice Exercises

Read the short passage below and answer the questions:

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world. In Northern Nigeria, the harmattan season brings dust from this desert, covering everything in a fine brown layer. During this time, the air is dry, and people wear sweaters to protect themselves from the cold morning breeze.

  1. What brings the dust to Northern Nigeria?
  2. Based on the passage, what is the weather like during the harmattan?
  3. Find a word in the passage that means the same as "provide cover/safety."

Answers

  • 1. The harmattan season (bringing dust from the Sahara Desert).
  • 2. The weather is dry and has a cold morning breeze.
  • 3. Protect.
