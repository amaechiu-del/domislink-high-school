Verbs and Tenses

JSS1 English Studies (NERDC Syllabus)

A verb is an "action word." It tells us what a person, animal, or thing is doing. However, verbs also tell us when an action happens. This is called Tense.

The movement shows the flow of time from yesterday to tomorrow.

Key Tenses to Know:

  • Present Tense: Used for actions happening right now or habits.
    Example: "He walks to school."
  • Past Tense: Used for actions that are finished.
    Example: "He walked to school yesterday."
  • Future Tense: Used for actions that will happen later.
    Example: "He will walk to school tomorrow."

Nigerian Context Examples:

1. Present Tense: Chidi eats Jollof rice every Sunday.

2. Future Tense: Our teacher will visit the Zuma Rock in Niger State next month.

Fun Fact: The verb "set" is one of the most complicated words in English! It has over 400 different meanings, but its past tense is still just "set."
