Understanding Algebraic Processes

In Mathematics, Algebra is a way of using letters or symbols to represent numbers. These letters are called variables because their values can change.

Animation: "x" represents a hidden number. If x = 3, then x + 5 = 8.

Key Concepts

  • Variable: A letter (like \(x, y, a, b\)) that stands for a number we do not know yet.
  • Constant: A fixed number that does not change (e.g., \(5, 10, 100\)).
  • Expression: A mathematical phrase combining numbers and variables (e.g., \(2x + 7\)).
  • Coefficient: The number placed in front of a variable (e.g., in \(4y\), 4 is the coefficient).
Fun Fact: The word "Algebra" comes from the Arabic word "Al-Jabr", which means "reunion of broken parts." It was popularized by the Persian mathematician Al-Khwarizmi!

Nigerian Context Examples

Example 1: The Market Trip

Tunde went to Mile 12 Market to buy tubers of yam. If one tuber of yam costs ₦\(y\), how much will Tunde pay for 5 tubers?

Solution:
Cost of 1 tuber = \(y\)
Cost of 5 tubers = \(y + y + y + y + y\) or \(5 \times y\)
Algebraic Expression: \(5y\)

Example 2: Age Calculation

Chidi is \(n\) years old. His elder sister, Amaka, is 4 years older than him. How old is Amaka?

Solution:
Chidi's age = \(n\)
Amaka's age = Chidi's age + 4 years
Algebraic Expression: \(n + 4\)

Practice Questions

  1. Simplify the expression: \(3a + 5a - 2a\)
  2. If \(x = 10\), find the value of \(2x - 4\).
  3. A basket contains \(p\) mangoes. If 3 mangoes are eaten, how many mangoes are left?

Answers:

1. 6a (Process: 3 + 5 - 2 = 6)

2. 16 (Process: 2(10) - 4 = 20 - 4 = 16)

3. p - 3
