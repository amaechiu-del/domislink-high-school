Understanding Integers

In Mathematics, Integers are a special set of numbers that include all whole numbers and their negative opposites, including zero.

The green dot shows moving between positive and negative integers.

Key Points to Remember:

  • Positive Integers: Numbers greater than zero (1, 2, 3, ...). They represent gains or increases.
  • Negative Integers: Numbers less than zero (-1, -2, -3, ...). They represent losses or decreases.
  • Zero (0): It is an integer, but it is neither positive nor negative.
  • The Number Line: As you move to the right, the numbers get larger. As you move to the left, the numbers get smaller.

Nigerian Real-Life Examples

1. Business in Onitsha Market: If Chidi buys goods for ₦5,000 but sells them for ₦7,000, he has a profit of +2,000 (Positive). If he sells them for ₦4,000, he has a loss of -1,000 (Negative).

2. Temperature in Jos: During the cold harmattan season, if the temperature is 5 degrees above zero, it is +5°C. If it were to drop 2 degrees below zero (very rare!), it would be -2°C.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "Integer" comes from the Latin word "integer", which means "whole" or "untouched." This is why we don't include fractions or decimals in the group of integers!
