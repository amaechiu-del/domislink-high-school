What is a Percentage?

  • The word Percent means "per hundred" or "out of 100".
  • We use the symbol % to represent percentages.
  • A percentage is a fraction with a denominator of 100. For example, 20% = 20/100.
  • To convert any fraction to a percentage, multiply it by 100.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: School Scores
Tunde scored 18 out of 20 in his Mathematics test at a secondary school in Lagos. What is his score as a percentage?
Solution:
(18 ÷ 20) × 100 = 0.9 × 100 = 90%.
Example 2: Market Discount
A fabric seller in Kano gives a 10% discount on a lace material that costs ₦50,000. How much is the discount?
Solution:
10% of ₦50,000 = (10/100) × 50,000 = 0.1 × 50,000 = ₦5,000.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "cent" in percentage comes from the Latin word centum, which means hundred. This is the same root used for "Century" (100 years) and "Centimeter" (1/100th of a meter)!

Practice Questions

  1. Express 2/5 as a percentage.
  2. Find 25% of 120 tubers of yam.
  3. In a class of 50 students, 30 are girls. What percentage of the class are boys?

  1. (2/5) × 100 = 40%
  2. (25/100) × 120 = 30 tubers
  3. Total students = 50. Boys = 50 - 30 = 20 boys.
    Percentage of boys = (20/50) × 100 = 40%
