Perimeter and Area of Plane Shapes

Orange line = Perimeter | Blue space = Area

1. What is Perimeter?

  • Definition: Perimeter is the total distance around the edge of a closed shape.
  • Units: It is measured in linear units like centimeters (cm), meters (m), or kilometers (km).
  • Formula (Rectangle): $P = 2 \times (Length + Width)$
  • Formula (Square): $P = 4 \times Side$

2. What is Area?

  • Definition: Area is the total amount of surface space inside a shape.
  • Units: It is measured in square units like $cm^2$, $m^2$, or $km^2$.
  • Formula (Rectangle): $Area = Length \times Width$
  • Formula (Square): $Area = Side \times Side$ (or $S^2$)

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Fencing in Lagos
Chief Okeke wants to build a wire fence around his rectangular land in Lekki. If the land is 30m long and 20m wide, the length of the wire he needs is the Perimeter.
$P = 2 \times (30 + 20) = 2 \times 50 = 100m$.

Example 2: Tiling in Kano
Mallam Musa wants to cover the floor of his square prayer room with rugs. If one side of the room is 5 meters, the amount of rug needed is the Area.
$Area = 5m \times 5m = 25m^2$.

💡 Fun Fact!

The word "Perimeter" comes from two Greek words: 'peri' (around) and 'metron' (measure). Ancient Egyptians used knotted ropes to measure the perimeter of fields after the Nile River flooded!
