Plane Shapes (2D Shapes)

In Mathematics, plane shapes are flat, two-dimensional (2D) figures. This means they have length and breadth (width) but no thickness or height.

Observe how a square can change while remaining a flat plane shape.

Key Properties of Common Plane Shapes

  • Square: Has four equal sides and four right angles (90°).
  • Rectangle: Has four sides where opposite sides are equal and parallel.
  • Triangle: A three-sided polygon with three angles that always sum up to 180°.
  • Circle: A perfectly round shape with no corners or edges; every point on the boundary is the same distance from the center.

Nigerian Examples

1. The Nigerian Flag: This is a perfect example of a Rectangle. It consists of three vertical bands of equal size (green, white, green).
2. The 50 Kobo Coin: If you look at the face of a 50 Kobo coin, it is a perfect example of a Circle.
💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that the word "Polygon" comes from Greek words? "Poly" means many and "gon" means angle. So, a polygon is a shape with many angles!

Practice Questions

  1. How many sides does a rectangle have, and how many are equal?
  2. Which plane shape has three sides and three corners?
  3. A square has four sides of different lengths. True or False?
Answers:

1. A rectangle has 4 sides; only opposite sides are equal.
2. A Triangle.
3. False (A square must have all four sides equal).
