Understanding Prime Factors

In Mathematics, specifically the JSS1 NERDC curriculum, we learn that numbers can be broken down into smaller building blocks. These building blocks are called Prime Factors.

What are Prime Factors?

  • Factors: These are numbers that divide another number exactly without leaving a remainder.
  • Prime Numbers: These are numbers greater than 1 that have only two factors: 1 and themselves (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13...).
  • Prime Factors: These are factors of a number that are also prime numbers.
Key Concept: Every composite number can be expressed as a product of prime numbers. This is like the "DNA" of the number!

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Basket of Oranges

Obinna has 30 oranges from the Benue market. He wants to find the prime factors of 30.

  • Is 30 divisible by 2? Yes. 30 ÷ 2 = 15. (2 is prime)
  • Is 15 divisible by 2? No. Try 3. 15 ÷ 3 = 5. (3 is prime)
  • 5 is already a prime number.
  • Answer: The prime factors of 30 are 2, 3, and 5. (Written as 2 × 3 × 5).
Example 2: School Desks

A school in Enugu just received 28 new desks. Express 28 as a product of its prime factors.

  • 28 ÷ 2 = 14
  • 14 ÷ 2 = 7
  • 7 is a prime number.
  • Answer: 28 = 2 × 2 × 7 or 2² × 7.
💡 Fun Fact: The number 2 is the only even prime number in existence! Every other prime number is odd.

Practice Questions

  1. Express 24 as a product of prime factors.
  2. Find the prime factors of 50.
  3. What are the prime factors of 45?

Answers

1. 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 (or 2³ × 3)
2. 2 × 5 × 5 (or 2 × 5²)
3. 3 × 3 × 5 (or 3² × 5)
