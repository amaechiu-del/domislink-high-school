Ratio and Proportion

JSS1 Mathematics (NERDC Curriculum)

What is Ratio?

A Ratio is a way of comparing two or more quantities of the same kind to show how many times one number contains another.

The ratio of red circles to blue squares is 2:3 (Two to Three).

Key Points to Remember:

  • The symbol used for ratio is the colon (:).
  • Ratios have no units (e.g., the ratio of 5kg to 10kg is 1:2, not 1kg:2kg).
  • The order of numbers matters! 2:3 is not the same as 3:2.
  • A Proportion is a statement that two ratios are equal (e.g., 1:2 = 2:4).

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Mixing Zobo Drink

Aunty Shade makes Zobo by mixing 5 litres of water with 2 cups of dried hibiscus leaves. Express the relationship between water and leaves as a ratio.

Solution: The ratio of water to leaves is 5 : 2.

Example 2: School Population

In a JSS1 class in Lagos, there are 20 boys and 30 girls. Simplify the ratio of boys to girls.

Solution:
Ratio = 20 : 30
Divide both sides by 10 (Highest Common Factor):
20 ÷ 10 : 30 ÷ 10 = 2 : 3.

Fun Fact: The "Golden Ratio" (approx 1:1.61) is often used by Nigerian architects and artists because it creates shapes that are naturally pleasing to the eye!
