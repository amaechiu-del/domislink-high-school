Simple Linear Equations

JSS 1 Mathematics (NERDC Curriculum)

What is a Linear Equation?

An equation is a mathematical statement showing that two expressions are equal. Think of it like a weighing balance: for the scale to stay level, both sides must have the same value.

  • Variables: These are letters (like \(x\), \(y\), or \(a\)) that represent unknown numbers.
  • Equality Sign (=): This shows that the left-hand side (LHS) is exactly the same as the right-hand side (RHS).
  • Solving: To solve an equation means to find the value of the letter that makes the statement true.

The equation stays balanced as long as we do the same thing to both sides!

Rules for Solving Equations

  • If you add a number to one side, you must add it to the other side.
  • If you subtract from one side, you must subtract from the other.
  • The goal is to get the letter (variable) by itself on one side.
Example 1: The Gala Snack

Tunde went to a kiosk in Lagos. He bought one Gala and a bottle of water for ₦250. If the water costs ₦100, how much is the Gala?

Solution:
Let \(g\) be the price of Gala.
\(g + 100 = 250\)
Subtract 100 from both sides:
\(g = 250 - 100\)
\(g = 150\). So, the Gala is ₦150.

Example 2: School Bus Seats

In a school in Abuja, a small bus has \(x\) seats. After 5 students get off the bus, there are 12 students left. How many seats were occupied at the start?

Solution:
Equation: \(x - 5 = 12\)
Add 5 to both sides to isolate \(x\):
\(x = 12 + 5\)
\(x = 17\). There were 17 students initially.

Fun Fact: The word "Algebra" comes from the Arabic word "al-jabr", which means "reunion of broken parts." It was popularized by the mathematician Al-Khwarizmi over 1,000 years ago!

Test Your Knowledge!

  1. Solve for \(y\): \(y + 15 = 40\)
  2. Solve for \(m\): \(m - 8 = 22\)
  3. If \(3x = 15\), what is the value of \(x\)? (Hint: Divide both sides by 3)
Answers:
1. \(y = 25\) | 2. \(m = 30\) | 3. \(x = 5\)
