Solid Shapes (3D Shapes)

In Mathematics, Solid Shapes are objects that have three dimensions: length, width, and height. Unlike flat shapes (like a drawing of a square on paper), solid shapes take up space.

Key Properties of Solid Shapes

  • Faces: The flat or curved surfaces that make up the outside of the shape.
  • Edges: The lines where two faces meet.
  • Vertices (Corners): The points where three or more edges meet.
  • Volume: Because they are 3D, solid shapes can hold things inside them (capacity).

Common Examples in Nigeria

1. A Tin of Peak Milk
This is a Cylinder. It has two flat circular faces (top and bottom) and one curved surface.
2. A Dangote Cement Block
This is a Cuboid. It has 6 rectangular faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices.
💡 Fun Fact: A Sphere (like a football) is a unique solid shape because it has only one curved face, zero edges, and zero vertices!
