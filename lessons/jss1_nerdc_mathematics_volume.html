Understanding Volume

In Mathematics, Volume is the amount of three-dimensional (3D) space that an object occupies. While area measures the surface, volume measures what is inside the object.

Interactive Cuboid: Visualizing Length, Width, and Height.

Key Points about Volume:

  • Volume is measured in cubic units, such as cubic centimeters (cm³) or cubic meters (m³).
  • For a regular box shape (a cuboid), the formula is:
    Volume = Length × Width × Height
  • If all sides are equal (a cube), the formula is: Volume = Side × Side × Side (S³).
  • Liquid volume is often measured in Litres (L). (1,000cm³ = 1 Litre).

Nigerian Examples

1. The Dangote Cement Block: A standard 9-inch building block used in Lagos construction is a cuboid. If a block is 45cm long, 22.5cm wide, and 22.5cm high, its volume helps a builder know how much concrete was used to make it.
2. GeePee Water Tank: Imagine a rectangular GeePee overhead tank in your school. If the tank is 2m long, 2m wide, and 2m high, it is a cube. Its volume is 2m × 2m × 2m = 8m³, which tells us how much water it can store for students to use.
💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that the average human body has a volume of about 66,000 cubic centimeters (cm³)? That's about the same volume as 66 large "Eva" water bottles!

Practice Questions

  1. Calculate the volume of a shoebox that is 30cm long, 15cm wide, and 10cm high.
  2. A cubic sugar box has a side length of 4cm. What is its volume?
  3. A rectangular water reservoir in a village in Kano is 5m long, 4m wide, and 2m deep. How many cubic meters of water can it hold?
Answers:
1. 4,500cm³ (30 × 15 × 10)
2. 64cm³ (4 × 4 × 4)
3. 40m³ (5 × 4 × 2)
