Physical Environment

The physical environment refers to the natural surroundings in which we live, including land, water, air, plants, and animals.

The components of the physical environment include:

  • Land: This includes mountains, hills, valleys, and plains.
  • Water: This includes rivers, lakes, oceans, and wetlands.
  • Air: This refers to the atmosphere that surrounds the earth.

Examples of physical environments in Nigeria include:

  • The Niger Delta region, which is a wetland area with many rivers and creeks.
  • The Jos Plateau, which is a mountainous region with many hills and valleys.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the physical environment plays a crucial role in shaping the culture and lifestyle of a community? For example, communities living in coastal areas tend to rely on fishing and other marine activities for their livelihood.

Practice Questions

1. What are the three main components of the physical environment?

Answer: Land, Water, and Air.

2. Give an example of a physical environment in Nigeria.

Answer: The Niger Delta region.

3. Why is the physical environment important to humans?

Answer: The physical environment provides us with the necessary resources for survival, such as food, water, and shelter, and also influences our culture and lifestyle.
