Social Issues in Nigeria

Social issues are problems that affect a large number of people in a society. They can be related to economics, politics, culture, or the environment.

Some common social issues include:

  • Poverty: a state of being extremely poor
  • Unemployment: a state of being without a job
  • Corruption: the abuse of power for personal gain

Examples of social issues in Nigeria include:

  • Boko Haram insurgency in the North East: a terrorist group that has caused displacement and violence
  • Oil pollution in the Niger Delta: the pollution of water and soil due to oil spills, affecting the health and livelihood of local communities

Fun Fact

The United Nations has 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to address social issues worldwide, including poverty, inequality, and climate change.

Practice Questions:

  1. What is the main cause of poverty in Nigeria?
  2. What is the effect of corruption on the economy?
  3. How can we address the issue of oil pollution in the Niger Delta?

Answers:

  1. Lack of employment opportunities and poor education
  2. Corruption leads to a decrease in economic growth and an increase in inequality
  3. We can address the issue of oil pollution by implementing stricter regulations on oil companies and providing support to affected communities
