The Family

The family is a basic social unit that consists of people related by blood, marriage, or adoption. It is the smallest unit of society and plays a crucial role in the socialization of individuals.

The family performs several functions, including:

  • Providing shelter and protection for its members
  • Offering emotional support and care
  • Teaching social values and norms
  • Providing economic support

In Nigeria, for example, the family plays a significant role in the socialization of children. Two notable examples of family-oriented cultures in Nigeria are:

  • The Igbo people, who place a strong emphasis on extended family ties and communal living
  • The Yoruba people, who have a strong tradition of respect for elders and ancestral heritage

Fun Fact

Did you know that the concept of family is not limited to humans? Many animal species, such as wolves and elephants, also live in family groups and exhibit complex social behaviors.

Practice Questions

1. What is the primary function of the family in society?

Answer: The primary function of the family is to provide shelter, protection, emotional support, and economic care for its members.

2. Which Nigerian ethnic group is known for its strong emphasis on extended family ties?

Answer: The Igbo people.

3. What is one way in which animals, such as wolves and elephants, exhibit family-like behavior?

Answer: They live in groups and exhibit complex social behaviors, such as cooperation and mutual care.
