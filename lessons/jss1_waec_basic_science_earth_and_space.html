Earth and Space

Introduction to Earth and Space, a fundamental topic in Basic Science. In this lesson, we will explore the structure of the Earth and its relationship with the surrounding space.

Structure of the Earth

The Earth is composed of several layers: the crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core.

The above diagram illustrates the different layers of the Earth. The crust is the outermost layer, followed by the mantle, outer core, and inner core.

Features of the Earth's Surface

The Earth's surface has several features, including:

  • Mountains: the highest points on the Earth's surface, e.g. the Obudu Plateau in Nigeria
  • Valleys: low areas of the Earth's surface, e.g. the Niger Delta in Nigeria
  • Oceans: large bodies of saltwater that cover most of the Earth's surface

Fun Fact

The Earth is not a perfect sphere, but an oblate spheroid, meaning it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator.
