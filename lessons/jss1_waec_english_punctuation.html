Punctuation in English

Punctuation refers to the use of special marks or symbols to separate words, phrases, and clauses in written language. It helps to clarify the meaning of text, make it easier to read, and convey the tone and emotion of the writer.

Some common punctuation marks include:

  • Full stop (.)
  • Comma (,)
  • Colon (:)
  • Semi-colon (;)
  • Question mark (?)
  • Exclamation mark (!)

Here are two Nigerian examples of punctuation in use:

The capital of Nigeria is Abuja. It is located in the Federal Capital Territory.

The popular Nigerian dish, jollof rice, is often served at parties: it is made with rice, tomatoes, and spices.

Fun Fact

The use of punctuation dates back to ancient civilizations, with evidence of punctuation marks found in ancient Greek and Roman texts.

Practice Questions

Choose the correct punctuation mark to use in each sentence:

  1. What _______ your name?
  2. The city of Lagos _______ the former capital of Nigeria;
  3. I love reading books _______ my favourite subject is English.

Answers:

  1. ?
  2. was
  3. and
