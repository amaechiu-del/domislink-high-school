Reading Comprehension

Reading comprehension is the ability to understand and interpret written text. It involves identifying the main idea, making inferences, and drawing conclusions.

Here are some key points to note about reading comprehension:

  • Identify the main idea of the text
  • Make inferences based on the information provided
  • Draw conclusions based on the evidence in the text
  • Use context clues to understand unfamiliar words

For example, in Nigeria, reading comprehension is essential for students to understand and analyze texts in various subjects, such as English, History, and Science. Two examples of texts that require reading comprehension in Nigeria are:

  • The Nigerian Constitution, which requires citizens to understand and interpret the laws and principles that govern the country
  • The works of Nigerian authors, such as Chinua Achebe's "Things Fall Apart", which require readers to understand and analyze the themes, characters, and plot

Fun Fact: Did you know that reading comprehension is a skill that can be improved with practice? The more you read and practice active reading strategies, the better you will become at understanding and interpreting written text.
