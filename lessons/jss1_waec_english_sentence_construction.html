Sentence Construction

Sentence construction is the process of building sentences using words, phrases, and clauses. A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.

Here are the key points to note about sentence construction:

  • A sentence must have a subject and a predicate.
  • The subject is the person, place, or thing that the sentence is about.
  • The predicate is the action or state of being of the subject.

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: "Lagos is a busy city." In this sentence, "Lagos" is the subject and "is a busy city" is the predicate.

Example 2: "The Nigerian football team won the match." In this sentence, "The Nigerian football team" is the subject and "won the match" is the predicate.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the longest sentence in the English language has over 100 words? It was written by Jonathan Coe in his novel "The Rotters' Club" and contains 126 words!

Now, let's practice what we've learned with some questions:

Question 1: Identify the subject and predicate in the sentence "The teacher is writing on the board."

Answer: Subject = The teacher, Predicate = is writing on the board

Question 2: Construct a sentence using the subject "The student" and the predicate "is reading a book."

Answer: The student is reading a book.

Question 3: Identify the error in the sentence "The dog run quickly."

Answer: The error is the verb "run" which should be "runs" to agree with the subject "The dog". The correct sentence is "The dog runs quickly."
