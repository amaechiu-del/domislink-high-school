Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions are mathematical expressions that consist of variables, constants, and algebraic operations.

Here are some key points about algebraic expressions:

  • Variables are letters or symbols that represent unknown values.
  • Constants are numbers that do not change value.
  • Algebraic operations include addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Let's consider some examples from Nigeria:

In Lagos, a trader sells 5 baskets of tomatoes per day. If each basket contains 10 tomatoes, the total number of tomatoes sold per day can be represented by the algebraic expression 5x, where x is the number of tomatoes in each basket. However, since we know that x = 10, the expression simplifies to 5 * 10 = 50 tomatoes per day.

In Abuja, a student has 2n + 5 pencils in her pencil case, where n is the number of pencils she bought yesterday. If she bought 3 pencils yesterday, the total number of pencils she has today is 2 * 3 + 5 = 11 pencils.

Fun Fact

Did you know that algebraic expressions are used in many real-world applications, including science, engineering, and economics? They are a fundamental tool for solving problems and modeling complex systems.

Practice Questions

Solve the following problems:

  1. Simplify the expression 2x + 3, where x = 4.
  2. Evaluate the expression x - 2, where x = 7.
  3. Factor out the common term from the expression 3x + 6.

Answers:

  1. 2 * 4 + 3 = 11
  2. 7 - 2 = 5
  3. 3(x + 2)
