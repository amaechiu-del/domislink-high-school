Factors in Mathematics

Factors are numbers that are multiplied together to get another number. For example, the factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12.

The following are key points to note about factors:

  • Every number has at least two factors: 1 and itself.
  • A number can have more than two factors.
  • Factors can be prime or composite numbers.

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

In a Nigerian market, a trader has 12 crates of oranges to pack into boxes. If each box can hold 2, 3, 4, or 6 crates, how many boxes will the trader need? The answer depends on the factors of 12, which are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12.

In another example, a Nigerian farmer has 15 bags of maize to store in a warehouse. If each bag can be stored in a room with 1, 3, or 5 other bags, how many rooms will the farmer need? The answer depends on the factors of 15, which are 1, 3, 5, and 15.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the number 1 is a factor of every number? This means that every number can be divided by 1 without leaving a remainder.

Now, let's practice what we have learned:

Question 1: What are the factors of 18?

Answer: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, and 18.

Question 2: What are the factors of 25?

Answer: 1, 5, and 25.

Question 3: What are the factors of 36?

Answer: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, and 36.
