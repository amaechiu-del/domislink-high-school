Fractions

Fractions are a way of representing part of a whole. They consist of a numerator (the top number) and a denominator (the bottom number).

The above diagram shows a circle divided into 4 equal parts, with 1 part shaded. This can be represented as the fraction 1/4.

Here are some key points about fractions:

  • A fraction can be proper (numerator less than denominator), improper (numerator greater than or equal to denominator), or mixed (a combination of a whole number and a proper fraction).
  • Fractions can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided, just like whole numbers.
  • To compare fractions, we need to find a common denominator.

Let's look at some examples from Nigeria:

In a Nigerian market, a seller has 1/2 of a bag of rice left to sell. If the bag originally contained 20kg of rice, how many kilograms of rice are left? The answer is 10kg (1/2 x 20 = 10).

In a Nigerian school, 3/4 of the students are girls. If there are 24 students in the class, how many are girls? The answer is 18 (3/4 x 24 = 18).

Fun Fact: The ancient Egyptians used fractions to build the pyramids! They used fractions to calculate the area of triangles and the volume of pyramids.

Now, let's practice what we've learned. Here are three questions:

1. What is 1/2 + 1/4? Answer: 3/4

2. A Nigerian farmer has 3/4 of a hectare of land to plant crops. If 1 hectare is equal to 10000 square meters, how many square meters of land does the farmer have? Answer: 7500 square meters

3. What is 2/3 x 9? Answer: 6
