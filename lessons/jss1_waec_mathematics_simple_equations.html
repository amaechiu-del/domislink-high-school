Simple Equations

A simple equation is a statement that expresses the equality of two mathematical expressions, often containing variables.

Here are key points about simple equations:

  • They contain one or more variables (letters or symbols that represent unknown values).
  • They can be solved using basic arithmetic operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
  • The goal is to isolate the variable (get it by itself on one side of the equation).

Let's look at some examples from Nigeria:

Example 1: A farmer in Kano has 15 baskets of tomatoes. He gives 3 baskets to his friend. If he wants to put the remaining baskets into crates that hold 2 baskets each, how many crates will he need? This can be represented by the equation: 15 - 3 = 2x, where x is the number of crates.

Example 2: A student in Lagos has ₦120 to spend on lunch. She buys a meal for ₦40 and wants to share the remaining money equally among her 4 friends. This situation can be represented by the equation: 120 - 40 = 4x, where x is the amount each friend will get.

Fun Fact

Simple equations have many real-life applications in various fields like science, engineering, economics, and more. They help us solve problems, make informed decisions, and understand the world around us.

Practice Questions

Try solving these simple equations:

  1. 2x + 5 = 11
  2. x - 3 = 7
  3. 4x = 28

Answers:

  1. x = 3
  2. x = 10
  3. x = 7
