Types of Numbers

In mathematics, numbers can be classified into different types based on their properties. These types include natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, and irrational numbers.

  • Natural numbers: 1, 2, 3, ...
  • Whole numbers: 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
  • Integers: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
  • Rational numbers: numbers that can be expressed as a fraction, e.g. 1/2, 3/4
  • Irrational numbers: numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction, e.g. π, √2

For example, in Nigeria, the population of a city can be represented as a whole number, while the ratio of boys to girls in a school can be represented as a rational number. The cost of a plots of land in Lagos can be expressed as a decimal, which is a type of rational number.

Another example is the measurement of the length of a room in a house in Abuja, which can be expressed as a decimal number, making it a rational number.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the ancient Greeks discovered irrational numbers while trying to calculate the length of the diagonal of a square?

Practice Questions

1. Classify the number 0.5 as a type of number.

Answer: Rational number

2. Give an example of an irrational number in real life.

Answer: The length of the diagonal of a square with a side length of 1 meter, which is √2 meters.

3. Determine whether the number π is a rational or irrational number.

Answer: Irrational number
