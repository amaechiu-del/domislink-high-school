Social Issues in Nigeria

Social issues are problems that affect a large number of people in a society. They can be related to the environment, economy, politics, or social relationships.

Some common social issues in Nigeria include:

  • Poverty: a state of being extremely poor, lacking basic needs like food, water, and shelter.
  • Unemployment: a state of being without a job, often leading to financial difficulties.
  • Corruption: the abuse of power for personal gain, often leading to social and economic problems.

Examples of social issues in Nigeria include:

  • The Boko Haram insurgency in the North-East, which has led to displacement, poverty, and lack of access to education.
  • The Niger Delta crisis, which has led to environmental degradation, poverty, and lack of access to basic amenities like clean water and healthcare.

Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria has one of the largest youth populations in the world, with over 60% of its population under the age of 25? This demographic can be a powerful force for positive change in addressing social issues.

Practice Questions:

  1. What is the main cause of poverty in Nigeria?
    1. Lack of education
    2. Lack of job opportunities
    3. Corruption
    Answer: b) Lack of job opportunities
  2. Which of the following is an example of a social issue in Nigeria?
    1. Boko Haram insurgency
    2. Niger Delta crisis
    3. Both a and b
    Answer: c) Both a and b
  3. What can be done to address social issues in Nigeria?
    1. Increased government funding
    2. Community-based initiatives
    3. Both a and b
    Answer: c) Both a and b
