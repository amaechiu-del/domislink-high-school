The Family

The family is a basic social unit that consists of people who are related by blood, marriage, or adoption. It is an important institution in every society, providing a sense of belonging, support, and identity to its members.

The family performs several functions, including:

  • Providing emotional support and care to its members
  • Teaching social values and norms to children
  • Providing economic support and security to its members

In Nigeria, for example, the family is highly valued and plays a significant role in the socialization of children. Two examples of this are:

The Yoruba people of southwestern Nigeria, who place a strong emphasis on family and community ties, and the Igbo people of southeastern Nigeria, who have a strong tradition of extended family systems.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the concept of family is not limited to humans? Many animals, such as wolves and elephants, also live in family groups and have complex social structures.
