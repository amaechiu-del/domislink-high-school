Acids and Bases

Acids and bases are two types of chemical substances that play important roles in our daily lives. In this lesson, we will explore the properties and examples of acids and bases.

Properties of Acids and Bases

  • Acids have a sour taste and turn blue litmus paper red.
  • Bases have a bitter taste and turn red litmus paper blue.
  • Acids and bases react with each other to form salts and water.

Examples of Acids and Bases in Nigeria

In Nigeria, we can find many examples of acids and bases. For instance:

  • Lemon juice, which is commonly used in Nigerian cuisine, is an example of an acid.
  • The soap made from the ash of palm trees, which is used for laundry, is an example of a base.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the human body has a natural pH balance? The pH of human blood is slightly alkaline, ranging from 7.35 to 7.45. This is why our bodies can maintain homeostasis and function properly.

Practice Questions

Test your understanding of acids and bases with these practice questions:

  1. What is the characteristic taste of an acid?
    1. Sour
    2. Bitter
    3. Sweet
    Answer: a) Sour
  2. What happens when an acid and a base react with each other?
    1. They form a new acid
    2. They form a new base
    3. They form a salt and water
    Answer: c) They form a salt and water
  3. What is an example of a base in Nigerian culture?
    1. Lemon juice
    2. Palm tree ash soap
    3. Groundnut oil
    Answer: b) Palm tree ash soap
