Growth in Living Organisms

Growth refers to the permanent increase in size and weight of an organism. It is an irreversible change that occurs as a result of the production of new cells.

The process of growth involves cell division, which is the process by which a cell divides into two or more daughter cells.

  • Increase in cell number: Growth occurs as a result of an increase in the number of cells in an organism.
  • Increase in cell size: Growth can also occur as a result of an increase in the size of cells.
  • Differentiation: As cells divide and grow, they also differentiate into different types of cells that perform specific functions.

Examples of growth in Nigeria include:

  • The growth of a mango tree from a seedling to a mature tree in a village in Kano State.
  • The growth of a child from birth to adulthood in a family in Lagos State.

Answer: Increase in cell number

Answer: The growth of a cocoa tree from a seedling to a mature tree in a farm in Ondo State.

3. What is the term for the process by which cells become specialized to perform specific functions?

Answer: Differentiation
