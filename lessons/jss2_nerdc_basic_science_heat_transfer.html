Heat Transfer

Heat transfer is the process by which heat energy is transferred from one body to another due to a temperature difference. There are three main methods of heat transfer: conduction, convection, and radiation.

  • Conduction: This is the transfer of heat energy through direct contact between particles or molecules. It occurs in solids, liquids, and gases.
  • Convection: This is the transfer of heat energy through the movement of fluids. It occurs in liquids and gases.
  • Radiation: This is the transfer of heat energy through electromagnetic waves. It can occur in a vacuum and does not require a medium.

Examples of heat transfer in Nigeria include:

  • The use of heat transfer in the production of palm oil, where heat is transferred from the steam to the palm fruits to extract the oil.
  • The use of heat transfer in the cooling systems of cars, where heat is transferred from the engine to the surrounding air through the radiator.

Fun Fact: The Earth's climate is regulated by the balance between the amount of heat energy absorbed from the sun and the amount of heat energy lost to space through radiation.
