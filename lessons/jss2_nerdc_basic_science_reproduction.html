Reproduction in Basic Science

Reproduction is the process by which living organisms produce offspring. It is essential for the survival of species.

There are two main types of reproduction: sexual and asexual reproduction. The main difference between them is the involvement of gametes (sex cells) in sexual reproduction.

  • Sexual reproduction involves the fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.
  • Asexual reproduction involves the production of offspring without the fusion of gametes.

Examples of reproduction in Nigeria include:

  • The reproduction of mango trees in the savannas of Nigeria, where the trees produce fruits that contain seeds which can grow into new trees.
  • The reproduction of tilapia fish in Nigerian rivers, where the female fish lays eggs that hatch into fry.

Fun Fact

Did you know that some organisms, such as certain types of bacteria, can reproduce by a process called binary fission, where the cell divides into two identical cells?

Practice Questions

1. What is the main difference between sexual and asexual reproduction?

Answer: The main difference is the involvement of gametes (sex cells) in sexual reproduction.

2. Give an example of asexual reproduction in Nigeria.

Answer: The reproduction of bacteria in Nigerian soil, where the bacteria divide into two identical cells.

3. What is the term for the cell formed by the fusion of male and female gametes?

Answer: Zygote.
