Sense Organs

Sense organs are specialized structures that help us perceive and interpret our environment. They play a crucial role in our daily lives, enabling us to interact with the world around us.

The five sense organs are:

  • Eye (sight)
  • Ear (hearing)
  • Nose (smell)
  • Tongue (taste)
  • Skin (touch)

For example, in Nigeria, the sense of sight is used to appreciate the beauty of the Osun Osogbo Sacred Grove, while the sense of hearing is used to enjoy the sounds of Afrobeat music.

In another example, the sense of smell is used to savor the aroma of jollof rice, a popular Nigerian dish, while the sense of taste is used to enjoy its flavor.

Fun Fact

Butterflies taste with their feet! They have tiny sensors on their feet that help them detect the sweetness or bitterness of a substance.

Practice Questions

1. Which sense organ is responsible for hearing?

Answer: Ear

2. What is the function of the nose?

Answer: Smell

3. Which sense organ is used to feel touch?

Answer: Skin
