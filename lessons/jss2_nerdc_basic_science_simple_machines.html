Simple Machines

Simple machines are devices that make work easier by providing a mechanical advantage. They are the basic components of more complex machines.

There are six types of simple machines:

  • Levers
  • Inclined planes
  • Wedges
  • Screws
  • Pulleys
  • Wheels and axles

Examples of simple machines in Nigeria include:

  • Borehole drilling machines, which use inclined planes and wheels and axles to lift water from deep beneath the ground.
  • Okada (motorcycle) kick-starters, which use levers to make it easier to start the engine.

Fun Fact: The first simple machine, the lever, was used by ancient Egyptians to build the pyramids over 4,500 years ago!

Practice Questions:

  1. What is the purpose of a simple machine?
  2. Which simple machine is used to lift heavy loads with less effort?
  3. Give an example of a simple machine used in Nigerian agriculture.

Answers:

  1. To make work easier by providing a mechanical advantage.
  2. A pulley.
  3. A tractor, which uses wheels and axles to make it easier to plow and harvest fields.
