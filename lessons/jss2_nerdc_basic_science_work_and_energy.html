Work and Energy

Work and energy are closely related concepts in physics. When a force is applied to an object, it can cause the object to move or change its state, and this is known as work. The energy expended to do this work is called the energy transferred.

The following are key points to note about work and energy:

  • Work is done when a force is applied to an object and the object moves in the direction of the force.
  • The unit of work is the joule (J).
  • Energy is the ability to do work.
  • There are different types of energy, including kinetic energy, potential energy, thermal energy, and electrical energy.

Here are two Nigerian examples of work and energy:

In Lagos, a car driver applies a force to the accelerator pedal, causing the car to move from rest to a high speed. This is an example of work being done, as the force applied to the pedal causes the car to change its state from rest to motion.

In Kano, a farmer uses a tractor to plough his farm. The tractor does work on the soil as it moves, breaking up the soil and preparing it for planting. The energy expended by the tractor is an example of mechanical energy being transferred to the soil.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the human body is capable of producing a significant amount of energy? When you exercise, your body converts chemical energy from the food you eat into kinetic energy, which is then used to power your movements.

Now, let's practice what we've learned with some questions:

1. A boy pushes a box up a slope. If the force applied is 50 N and the distance moved is 10 m, how much work is done?

Answer: 500 J

2. What type of energy is associated with an object's motion?

Answer: Kinetic energy

3. A girl lifts a bucket of water from the ground to a height of 2 m. If the mass of the water is 10 kg, how much work is done?

Answer: 196 J
