Active and Passive Voice

In English, voice refers to the way in which the subject of a sentence performs or receives the action described by the verb. There are two main types of voice: active and passive.

The key differences between active and passive voice are:

  • In active voice, the subject performs the action described by the verb.
  • In passive voice, the subject receives the action described by the verb.

Here are two examples from Nigeria:

Active: The Lagos State Government built the Eko Atlantic City. (The subject, Lagos State Government, performed the action of building.)

Passive: The Eko Atlantic City was built by the Lagos State Government. (The subject, Eko Atlantic City, received the action of being built.)

Fun Fact

Did you know that using passive voice can sometimes make your writing seem more formal or objective? However, overusing it can make your writing seem unclear or wordy.

Practice Questions:

1. Identify the voice used in the sentence: "The Federal Government of Nigeria has launched a new education policy."

Answer: Active

2. Rewrite the sentence in passive voice: "The Nigerian team won the African Cup of Nations."

Answer: The African Cup of Nations was won by the Nigerian team.

3. Identify the voice used in the sentence: "The new airport in Abuja was commissioned by the President."

Answer: Passive
