Reported Speech

Reported speech is a way of expressing what someone else has said, without using their exact words. It is often used in conversations, writing, and storytelling to convey information or dialogue that has taken place earlier.

The following are key points to note about reported speech:

  • The verb tense of the reported clause is often changed to match the time of reporting.
  • Pronouns are also changed to match the context of the reporting.
  • Adverbs of time and place may be changed or omitted.

Here are two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: "I will come to the party," said Tolani. (Direct speech) -> Tolani said that she would come to the party. (Reported speech)

Example 2: "I have never been to Lagos," said Chinedu. (Direct speech) -> Chinedu said that he had never been to Lagos. (Reported speech)

Fun Fact: The use of reported speech can add variety and interest to writing and conversation, and can help to avoid repetition and monotony.

Practice Questions:

1. "I am going to the market," said Nneoma. (Direct speech) -> _______________________

Answer: Nneoma said that she was going to the market.

2. "We will finish the project by Friday," said the team leader. (Direct speech) -> _______________________

Answer: The team leader said that they would finish the project by Friday.

3. "I have never eaten pounded yam," said Bola. (Direct speech) -> _______________________

Answer: Bola said that he had never eaten pounded yam.
