Tenses in English

Tenses are used to express time in English. There are three main types of tenses: past, present, and future.

The three main types of tenses are:

  • Past tense: used to describe actions that happened in the past
  • Present tense: used to describe actions that are happening now
  • Future tense: used to describe actions that will happen in the future

Here are two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: "I ate jollof rice yesterday" (past tense)

Example 2: "I will visit Lagos next month" (future tense)

Fun Fact: The concept of tenses is not unique to English. Many languages, including Yoruba and Igbo, have their own systems of expressing time.

Practice Questions:

1. Which tense is used to describe actions that are happening now?

Answer: Present tense

2. Which of the following sentences is in the past tense?

A) I will eat breakfast

B) I ate breakfast

Answer: B) I ate breakfast

3. Which of the following sentences is in the future tense?

A) I am studying for my exams

B) I will study for my exams

Answer: B) I will study for my exams
