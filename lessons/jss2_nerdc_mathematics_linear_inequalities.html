Linear Inequalities

Linear inequalities are mathematical statements that compare two expressions using inequality signs such as <, >, ≤, or ≥. They can be used to represent real-world situations where a quantity is constrained by a certain limit.

The above diagram illustrates the concept of linear inequalities, where the line y = 2x - 1 is greater than the line y = x + 1.

Here are some key points to note about linear inequalities:

  • Linear inequalities can be written in the form ax + b < c or ax + b > c, where a, b, and c are constants.
  • Linear inequalities can be graphed on a coordinate plane, where the solution set is the region above or below the line.
  • Linear inequalities can be solved using algebraic methods, such as adding or subtracting the same value from both sides of the inequality.

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: A farmer in Kano State wants to buy fertilizer for his farm. The cost of the fertilizer is ₦500 per bag, and he has a budget of ₦10,000. If he wants to buy at least 15 bags of fertilizer, what is the maximum amount he can spend on each bag?

Example 2: A student in Lagos State wants to score at least 80% on her math exam. If she has scored 60% on her previous exams, and she wants to improve her score by at least 10%, what is the minimum score she needs to achieve on her next exam?

Fun Fact: Did you know that linear inequalities are used in many real-world applications, such as finance, engineering, and economics? They are used to model complex systems and make predictions about future outcomes.

Practice Questions:

1. Solve the inequality 2x + 5 > 11.

Answer: x > 3

2. Graph the inequality y < 2x - 1 on a coordinate plane.

Answer: The solution set is the region below the line y = 2x - 1.

3. A company in Abuja wants to produce at least 500 units of a product per day. If the production cost is ₦100 per unit, and the company has a budget of ₦50,000 per day, what is the maximum number of units the company can produce?

Answer: 500 units
