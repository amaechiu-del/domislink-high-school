Polygons

A polygon is a 2D shape with at least three sides. It can be classified as a regular or irregular polygon, depending on whether all its sides and angles are equal.

Some key points about polygons include:

  • All polygons have at least three sides.
  • The sum of the interior angles of a polygon can be calculated using the formula (n-2)*180, where n is the number of sides.
  • Polygons can be regular (all sides and angles are equal) or irregular (all sides and angles are not equal).

Fun Fact

The word "polygon" comes from the Greek words "poly" meaning "many" and "gon" meaning "angle" or "corner".

Answer: (6-2)*180 = 720 degrees

2. What is the difference between a regular and irregular polygon?

Answer: A regular polygon has all sides and angles equal, while an irregular polygon has all sides and angles not equal.
