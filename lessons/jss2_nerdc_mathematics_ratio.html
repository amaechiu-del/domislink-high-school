Ratio

Ratio is a way of comparing two quantities by division. It can be written in the form a:b or a/b, where a and b are the quantities being compared.

Here are some key points about ratio:

  • Ratio can be used to compare quantities of the same unit or different units.
  • Ratio can be simplified by dividing both quantities by their greatest common divisor.
  • Ratio can be used to solve problems involving proportions and scaling.

Here are two examples of ratio in Nigeria:

In a school in Lagos, the ratio of boys to girls is 3:5. If there are 120 boys, how many girls are there?

In a market in Kano, the ratio of yams to plantains is 2:3. If there are 240 yams, how many plantains are there?

Fun Fact: The ratio of the Nigerian flag's green white-green stripes is 1:2:1.

Practice Questions:

1. A bag contains 12 red balls and 8 blue balls. What is the ratio of red balls to blue balls?

Answer: 3:2

2. A bakery sells 15 loaves of bread for every 20 loaves of cake. What is the ratio of bread to cake?

Answer: 3:4

3. A farmer has 24 chickens and 16 goats. What is the ratio of chickens to goats?

Answer: 3:2
