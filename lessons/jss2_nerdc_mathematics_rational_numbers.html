Rational Numbers

Rational numbers are a fundamental concept in mathematics, and they play a crucial role in various mathematical operations. A rational number is a number that can be expressed as the ratio of two integers, where the denominator is non-zero.

Here are the key points to note about rational numbers:

  • A rational number can be positive, negative, or zero.
  • A rational number can be expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.
  • Rational numbers can be added, subtracted, multiplied, and divided, just like integers.

Let's consider some examples from Nigeria:

In Nigeria, the price of a bag of rice can be expressed as a rational number. For instance, if a bag of rice costs ₦2,500, and you buy 1/4 of a bag, the cost can be calculated as ₦2,500 x 1/4 = ₦625.

Another example is the measurement of land in Nigeria. If a plot of land measures 3/4 of a hectare, and 1 hectare is equal to 10,000 square meters, then the area of the plot can be calculated as 3/4 x 10,000 = 7,500 square meters.

Fun Fact

Did you know that rational numbers are used in music? The rhythm and melody of music can be represented using rational numbers, where the numerator represents the number of beats, and the denominator represents the time signature.
