Simple Interest

Simple interest is a type of interest that is calculated as a percentage of the principal amount borrowed or invested.

The formula for calculating simple interest is:

  • I = PRT
  • Where I is the interest, P is the principal amount, R is the rate of interest, and T is the time period.

Here are two examples:

Example 1: Mr. Johnson borrows ₦10,000 from a bank at an interest rate of 5% per annum. If he repays the loan after 2 years, how much will he pay in total?

Solution: I = PRT = 10000 x 0.05 x 2 = ₦1000. Total amount = ₦10,000 + ₦1000 = ₦11,000.

Example 2: A savings account at a Nigerian bank pays an interest rate of 2% per annum. If you deposit ₦50,000 into the account, how much interest will you earn after 5 years?

Solution: I = PRT = 50000 x 0.02 x 5 = ₦5000.

Fun Fact: Did you know that simple interest is used in many real-life situations, such as calculating the interest on a savings account or the cost of borrowing money from a bank?

Practice Questions:

1. If a borrower takes a loan of ₦20,000 at an interest rate of 4% per annum, how much will he pay in total after 3 years?

Answer: I = PRT = 20000 x 0.04 x 3 = ₦2400. Total amount = ₦20,000 + ₦2400 = ₦22,400.

2. A student invests ₦8000 in a savings account that pays an interest rate of 3% per annum. How much interest will she earn after 2 years?

Answer: I = PRT = 8000 x 0.03 x 2 = ₦480.

3. If a lender lends ₦15,000 to a borrower at an interest rate of 6% per annum, how much will the borrower pay in total after 4 years?

Answer: I = PRT = 15000 x 0.06 x 4 = ₦3600. Total amount = ₦15,000 + ₦3600 = ₦18,600.
