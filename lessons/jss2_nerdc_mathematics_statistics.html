Statistics for JSS2

Statistics is the study of the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data. It involves the use of various methods to summarize and describe data, such as measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and measures of variability (range, variance, standard deviation).

Here are the key points to note about statistics:

  • It involves the collection of data from a sample or population.
  • It uses various methods to summarize and describe data, such as tables, graphs, and charts.
  • It helps to identify patterns and trends in data.
  • It is used in various fields, such as business, medicine, and social sciences.

Here are two Nigerian examples of statistics in action:

In Lagos State, the government uses statistics to track the number of people living in the state, the number of schools, and the number of hospitals. This helps them to plan and allocate resources effectively.

In Nigeria, the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) releases data on the country's inflation rate, unemployment rate, and GDP. This helps businesses, investors, and policymakers to make informed decisions.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the word "statistics" comes from the German word "Statistik", which means "study of the state"? It was first used in the 18th century to describe the collection and analysis of data about states and governments.

Now, let's practice what we've learned. Here are three questions:

1. What is the main purpose of statistics?

Answer: The main purpose of statistics is to collect, analyze, and interpret data to understand patterns and trends.

2. What is the difference between a sample and a population?

Answer: A sample is a subset of data collected from a larger group, while a population is the entire group of people or items being studied.

3. What is the mean of the numbers 2, 4, 6, 8, 10?

Answer: To find the mean, we add up all the numbers and divide by the total number of values. So, (2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10) / 5 = 30 / 5 = 6.
