Human Trafficking

Human trafficking is the recruitment, transportation, transfer, harbouring or receipt of people through force, coercion or other means, with the aim of exploiting them for profit.

The process of human trafficking involves:

  • Recruitment: The process of finding and recruiting victims
  • Transportation: The process of moving victims from one place to another
  • Exploitation: The process of using victims for profit, such as forced labor or prostitution

Examples of human trafficking in Nigeria include:

  • The trafficking of young girls from Nigeria to Italy for forced prostitution
  • The use of child labor in Nigerian farms and factories, where children are forced to work long hours in hazardous conditions

Fun Fact: The International Labor Organization (ILO) estimates that there are over 40 million victims of modern slavery worldwide, with human trafficking being a major contributor to this number.

Practice Questions:

  1. What is the main aim of human trafficking?
    1. To provide employment opportunities
    2. To exploit people for profit
    3. To provide education and training
    Answer: b) To exploit people for profit
  2. What is the process of finding and recruiting victims called?
    1. Transportation
    2. Exploitation
    3. Recruitment
    Answer: c) Recruitment
  3. What is the estimated number of victims of modern slavery worldwide?
    1. 10 million
    2. 20 million
    3. 40 million
    Answer: c) 40 million
