Digestive System

The digestive system is a complex process by which the body breaks down food into nutrients that can be absorbed and utilized for energy, growth, and repair.

The digestive system consists of the following organs:

  • Mouth: where food is broken down by teeth and mixed with saliva
  • Esophagus: a muscular tube that carries food from the mouth to the stomach
  • Stomach: where food is mixed with digestive enzymes and acids
  • Small intestine: where nutrients are absorbed into the bloodstream
  • Large intestine: where water and electrolytes are absorbed

In Nigeria, examples of digestive system disorders include:

  • Cholera outbreaks in Lagos State, which can cause severe diarrhea and dehydration
  • Malnutrition in children in Kano State, which can lead to stunted growth and development

Fun Fact

The human digestive system is home to over 1,000 different species of bacteria, which play a crucial role in breaking down food and maintaining overall health.

Practice Questions

1. What is the main function of the stomach in the digestive system?

Answer: To mix food with digestive enzymes and acids.

2. What is the name of the muscular tube that carries food from the mouth to the stomach?

Answer: Esophagus.

3. What is the name of the intestine where nutrients are absorbed into the bloodstream?

Answer: Small intestine.
