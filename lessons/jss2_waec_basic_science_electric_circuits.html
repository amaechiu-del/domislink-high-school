Electric Circuits

An electric circuit is a path through which electric current flows. It consists of a power source, conductors, and loads.

Key components of an electric circuit include:

  • Power source (e.g. battery or generator)
  • Conductors (e.g. wires)
  • Loads (e.g. light bulbs or motors)

Examples of electric circuits in Nigeria include:

  • The streetlights in Lagos, which are powered by a central generator and connected by a network of conductors.
  • The electrical system in a typical Nigerian home, which consists of a power source (e.g. a generator or the national grid), conductors (e.g. wires), and loads (e.g. light bulbs and appliances).

Fun Fact: The first electric circuit was invented by Alessandro Volta in 1800, using a stack of alternating copper and zinc discs separated by cardboard soaked in saltwater.

Practice Questions:

  1. What is the purpose of a conductor in an electric circuit?
  2. What is the difference between a power source and a load in an electric circuit?
  3. Give an example of an electric circuit in your daily life.

Answers:

  1. The purpose of a conductor is to carry electric current from the power source to the load.
  2. A power source generates electric energy, while a load uses electric energy to perform a specific function.
  3. Example: A flashlight, which consists of a power source (battery), a conductor (wire), and a load (light bulb).
