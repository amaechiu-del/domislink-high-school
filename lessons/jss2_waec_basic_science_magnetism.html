Magnetism

Magnetism is a physical phenomenon resulting from the interaction between magnetic fields. It is a class of physical phenomena that are mediated by magnetic fields.

The following are key points to note about magnetism:

  • A magnet has two poles: north and south.
  • Like poles repel each other, while unlike poles attract each other.
  • Magnets can attract certain metals like iron, nickel, and cobalt.

Examples of magnetism in Nigeria include:

  • The use of magnets in the Ajaokuta Steel Company to separate iron from other metals.
  • The use of magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) machines in Nigerian hospitals to diagnose medical conditions.

Fun Fact

The Earth itself is a giant magnet, with its magnetic field protecting us from harmful solar and cosmic radiation.

Practice Questions

  1. What are the two poles of a magnet?
  2. What happens when two like poles are brought together?
  3. Give an example of a metal that is attracted to magnets.

Answers:

  1. North and south poles.
  2. They repel each other.
  3. Iron.
