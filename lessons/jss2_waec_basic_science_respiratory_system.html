Respiratory System

The respiratory system is a vital system in the human body that brings oxygen into the body and removes carbon dioxide. It consists of the nose, mouth, throat, trachea, bronchi, and lungs.

The process of breathing involves the following steps:

  • Inhalation: Air enters the body through the nose or mouth and passes through the throat and trachea into the bronchi and lungs.
  • Exchange of gases: Oxygen from the air is absorbed into the bloodstream, while carbon dioxide is removed.
  • Exhalation: The diaphragm and intercostal muscles relax, and air is pushed out of the lungs and back out of the body.

For example, in Nigeria, the harmattan season can affect the respiratory system, causing dryness and irritation in the nose and throat. Similarly, the high levels of air pollution in cities like Lagos and Abuja can also have negative impacts on the respiratory system.

In the town of Jos, the cool and dry climate can help to relieve respiratory issues such as asthma, while in the city of Port Harcourt, the high humidity can exacerbate respiratory problems.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the human nose can detect over 1 trillion different scents? The nose contains specialized cells called olfactory receptors that are responsible for detecting odor molecules in the air.

Practice Questions

1. What is the main function of the respiratory system?

Answer: The main function of the respiratory system is to bring oxygen into the body and remove carbon dioxide.

2. What is the name of the muscle that separates the chest cavity from the abdominal cavity and plays a crucial role in breathing?

Answer: The diaphragm.

3. What is the term for the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide between the bloodstream and the lungs?

Answer: Gas exchange.
