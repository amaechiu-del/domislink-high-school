Prefixes and Suffixes in English

Prefixes and suffixes are affixes that are added to the beginning or end of a root word to change its meaning. Understanding prefixes and suffixes can help you decipher unfamiliar words and expand your vocabulary.

Here are some key points to note about prefixes and suffixes:

  • Prefixes are added to the beginning of a root word, while suffixes are added to the end.
  • Prefixes and suffixes can change the meaning of a word, but they do not change the root word itself.
  • Many prefixes and suffixes have Greek or Latin origins, and are used in many English words.

Here are two Nigerian examples of words that use prefixes and suffixes:

  • The word "Nigerian" has the suffix "-ian", which means "belonging to" or "from".
  • The word "anti-corruption" has the prefix "anti-", which means "against".

Fun Fact

Did you know that the word "quarantine" comes from the Italian word "quaranta", which means "40"? This is because ships arriving in Venice during the Black Death were required to anchor offshore for 40 days before landing, to ensure they did not bring the disease ashore.

Practice Questions

Choose the correct answer for each question:

  1. What is the prefix in the word "unhappy"?
    • A) un-
    • B) happy
    • C) -y
    • D) none of the above
    Answer: A) un-
  2. What is the suffix in the word "happiness"?
    • A) happy
    • B) -ness
    • C) -y
    • D) none of the above
    Answer: B) -ness
  3. What is the meaning of the prefix "re-" in the word "restart"?
    • A) against
    • B) again
    • C) from
    • D) none of the above
    Answer: B) again
