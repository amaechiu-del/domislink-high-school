Summary in English

A summary is a brief statement that presents the main points of a text, leaving out unnecessary details. It is an essential skill in English language that helps readers to understand the main idea of a text without having to read the entire text.

To write a good summary, you need to:

  • Read the text carefully to identify the main points
  • Leave out unnecessary details and examples
  • Use your own words to paraphrase the text
  • Keep your summary brief and concise

Here are two Nigerian examples:

The story of Moremi, a heroine from the Yoruba tribe, can be summarized as a bravery tale of a woman who saved her people from the Igbo tribe. Another example is the story of Efunsetan Aniwura, a powerful and wealthy woman from the Yoruba tribe, which can be summarized as a story of a woman who rose to power and became a leader in her community.

Fun Fact

Did you know that summarizing a text can help improve your reading comprehension and retention? It forces you to focus on the main ideas and understand the author's purpose.

Practice Questions

1. What is the main purpose of a summary?

Answer: The main purpose of a summary is to present the main points of a text briefly and concisely.

2. What should you leave out when writing a summary?

Answer: You should leave out unnecessary details and examples.

3. Why is summarizing a text important?

Answer: Summarizing a text is important because it helps improve your reading comprehension and retention, and forces you to focus on the main ideas and understand the author's purpose.
