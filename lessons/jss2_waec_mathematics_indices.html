Indices

Indices, also known as exponents, are used to show that a number is being multiplied by itself a certain number of times. For example, 2^3 means 2 multiplied by itself 3 times, or 2 * 2 * 2.

The rules of indices are as follows:

  • a^m * a^n = a^(m+n)
  • a^m / a^n = a^(m-n)
  • (a^m)^n = a^(m*n)

Let's look at some examples from Nigeria:

In Lagos, a baker is making loaves of bread. If she needs to package 2^3 loaves of bread into boxes, how many loaves will she have in total? The answer is 8 loaves.

In Abuja, a farmer is growing crops. If he plants 3^2 seeds, how many seeds will he have planted? The answer is 9 seeds.

Fun Fact: The concept of indices was first developed by the ancient Greeks, who used it to solve problems in geometry and algebra.
