Introduction to Probability

Probability is a measure of the likelihood of an event occurring. It is a number between 0 and 1, where 0 represents an impossible event and 1 represents a certain event.

The concept of probability can be explained using the following points:

  • Experiment: An action or situation that can produce a set of outcomes.
  • Outcome: A result of an experiment.
  • Sample Space: The set of all possible outcomes of an experiment.
  • Event: A set of one or more outcomes of an experiment.

Let's consider two examples from Nigeria:

Example 1: If a coin is tossed, what is the probability of getting a head? Since there are two possible outcomes (head or tail), the probability of getting a head is 1/2 or 0.5.

Example 2: If a die is rolled, what is the probability of getting a 6? Since there are six possible outcomes (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6), the probability of getting a 6 is 1/6 or approximately 0.17.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the concept of probability was first introduced by the French mathematician Pierre-Simon Laplace in the 18th century? He used probability to study the movements of celestial bodies and to predict the likelihood of certain events.

Practice Questions

1. If a bag contains 5 red balls and 3 blue balls, what is the probability of drawing a red ball?

Answer: 5/8 or 0.625

2. If a spinner has 8 equal sections, what is the probability of landing on a particular section?

Answer: 1/8 or 0.125

3. If it rains on 3 out of 5 days in a week, what is the probability that it will rain on a given day?

Answer: 3/5 or 0.6
