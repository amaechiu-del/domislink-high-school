Introduction to Statistics

Statistics is the study of the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data. It involves the use of mathematical techniques to summarize and describe data.

In statistics, we use various methods to analyze and interpret data. These methods include:

  • Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Measures of dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation)
  • Data presentation (tables, charts, graphs)

For example, in Nigeria, statistics can be used to:

1. Analyze the population growth rate in Lagos State. The National Population Commission of Nigeria (NPC) uses statistical methods to estimate the population of Lagos State and project its future growth.

2. Compare the performance of different states in the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE). The West African Examinations Council (WAEC) uses statistical methods to analyze the results and provide insights on the performance of each state.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the word "statistics" comes from the German word "Statistik", which means "study of the state"? The term was first used in the 18th century to describe the collection and analysis of data about the state and its citizens.

Practice Questions

1. What is the mean of the numbers 2, 4, 6, 8, 10?

Answer: 6

2. A survey of 20 students found that 12 students like mathematics, 8 students like science, and 5 students like both mathematics and science. How many students like either mathematics or science?

Answer: 15

3. A company produces 100 units of a product per day. The average number of units sold per day is 80. What is the average number of units left unsold per day?

Answer: 20
