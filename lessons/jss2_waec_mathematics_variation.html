Variation in Mathematics

Variation is a fundamental concept in mathematics that deals with the relationship between two or more quantities. It is used to describe how one quantity changes when another quantity changes.

There are two types of variation:

  • Direct Variation: This occurs when one quantity increases or decreases in proportion to the increase or decrease of another quantity. For example, if the cost of producing a product is directly proportional to the number of units produced.
  • Inverse Variation: This occurs when one quantity increases or decreases in proportion to the decrease or increase of another quantity. For example, if the time taken to travel a certain distance is inversely proportional to the speed of the vehicle.

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

The cost of transportation from Lagos to Abuja is directly proportional to the number of passengers. If the cost of transportation for 5 passengers is N50,000, what will be the cost for 10 passengers?

The time taken to fill a tank with water is inversely proportional to the rate at which water flows into the tank. If a tank is filled in 2 hours with a flow rate of 5 liters per minute, how long will it take to fill the tank with a flow rate of 10 liters per minute?

Fun Fact

Did you know that the concept of variation is used in many real-life situations, such as determining the cost of production, predicting population growth, and understanding the relationship between variables in scientific experiments?

Practice Questions

1. If the cost of producing a product is directly proportional to the number of units produced, and the cost of producing 10 units is N10,000, what will be the cost of producing 20 units?

Answer: N20,000

2. If the time taken to travel a certain distance is inversely proportional to the speed of the vehicle, and a vehicle travels 200km in 4 hours at a speed of 50km/h, how long will it take to travel the same distance at a speed of 100km/h?

Answer: 2 hours

3. If the area of a rectangle is directly proportional to the product of its length and width, and the area of a rectangle with length 5cm and width 3cm is 15cm², what will be the area of a rectangle with length 10cm and width 6cm?

Answer: 60cm²
