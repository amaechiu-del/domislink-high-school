Economic Activities

Economic activities refer to the various ways in which people produce, distribute, and exchange goods and services. These activities can be broadly categorized into three main sectors: primary, secondary, and tertiary.

The primary sector involves the extraction and production of raw materials, such as agriculture, mining, and forestry. The secondary sector involves the processing and manufacturing of these raw materials into finished goods, such as food processing, textile production, and construction. The tertiary sector involves the provision of services, such as transportation, communication, and finance.

  • The primary sector is the foundation of a country's economy, as it provides the raw materials needed for production.
  • The secondary sector is responsible for transforming these raw materials into finished goods, which can be sold domestically or internationally.
  • The tertiary sector is the fastest-growing sector in many countries, as it provides essential services that support economic activity.

Examples of economic activities in Nigeria include:

  • Agriculture in the Niger Delta region, where crops such as cassava, yams, and maize are produced.
  • Manufacturing in the city of Lagos, where companies produce goods such as textiles, food products, and construction materials.

3. Which sector is the fastest-growing in many countries?

Answer: Tertiary sector
