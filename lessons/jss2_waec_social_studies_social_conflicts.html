Social Conflicts

Social conflicts refer to the struggles and disagreements that arise between individuals, groups, or societies due to differences in interests, values, or beliefs.

The following are some explanations of social conflicts:

  • They can arise from economic, political, or social differences.
  • They can be resolved through negotiation, mediation, or compromise.
  • They can have both positive and negative effects on individuals and societies.

Examples of social conflicts in Nigeria include:

  • The Biafran War (1967-1970), which was a conflict between the Nigerian government and the secessionist state of Biafra.
  • The Niger Delta conflict, which is an ongoing conflict between the Nigerian government and militant groups in the Niger Delta region over issues of resource control and environmental degradation.

Fun Fact: The term "conflict" comes from the Latin word "conflictus," which means "a striking together."
