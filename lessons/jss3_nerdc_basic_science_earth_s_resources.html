What Are Earth’s Resources?

Earth provides everything we need to survive — from the air we breathe to the food we eat. These are called natural resources. They are divided into two main types:

  • Renewable Resources — Can be replaced naturally over time. Examples: sunlight, wind, water, trees (if replanted).
  • Non-Renewable Resources — Exist in limited amounts and take millions of years to form. Examples: crude oil, coal, natural gas, minerals.
  • Biotic Resources — Come from living things. Examples: wood, food crops, animals.
  • Abiotic Resources — Come from non-living things. Examples: water, air, soil, sunlight, metals.

🌍 Two Nigerian Examples

1. Crude Oil (Non-Renewable): Nigeria is one of Africa’s largest oil producers. Crude oil is drilled in the Niger Delta and refined into petrol, diesel, kerosene, and plastics. It is a major source of income for the country, but it is limited and causes pollution.

2. Cassava (Renewable Biotic Resource): Grown widely across Nigeria, cassava is a renewable food resource. It is processed into garri, fufu, and starch. Farmers replant cassava stems each season, making it a sustainable resource when managed well.

⭐ Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria has over 34 different solid minerals spread across all 36 states? These include limestone (used for cement), tin, coal, gold, and even gemstones like sapphire! Yet, most of Nigeria’s energy still comes from crude oil.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: State two differences between renewable and non-renewable resources. Give one Nigerian example of each.
  2. Question 2: Explain why crude oil is considered a non-renewable resource even though it is found in large quantities in the Niger Delta.
  3. Question 3: List three abiotic resources and explain how each one is used in everyday life.

✅ Answers:

Answer 1: Renewable resources can be replaced naturally (e.g., sunlight, cassava), while non-renewable resources cannot be replaced once used up (e.g., crude oil in Nigeria). Renewable resources are unlimited or quickly replenished; non-renewable take millions of years to form.

Answer 2: Crude oil is non-renewable because it takes millions of years to form from dead organisms under heat and pressure. We are using it much faster than nature can replace it. Once Nigeria’s oil reserves are exhausted, they cannot be renewed within human lifetimes.

Answer 3: (i) Water — used for drinking, cooking, washing, and irrigation. (ii) Air — used for breathing, wind energy, and burning fuel. (iii) Soil — used for growing food crops like yam, maize, and vegetables.
