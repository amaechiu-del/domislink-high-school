Ecology: The Web of Life

Ecology is the study of how living things (organisms) interact with each other and with their non-living environment. It helps us understand the balance of nature and our role within it.

  • Habitat: The natural home or environment of an organism (e.g., a pond, forest, or savanna).
  • Population: All the organisms of the same species living in a habitat (e.g., all the tilapia fish in a pond).
  • Community: All the different populations living and interacting in a habitat.
  • Ecosystem: The community of living things plus the non-living (abiotic) factors like sunlight, water, and soil.
  • Food Chain: A sequence showing who eats whom (e.g., Grass → Grasshopper → Lizard → Hawk).
  • Food Web: A network of interconnected food chains showing many feeding relationships.
  • Producers: Organisms that make their own food using sunlight (e.g., green plants).
  • Consumers: Organisms that eat other organisms (herbivores, carnivores, omnivores).
  • Decomposers: Organisms that break down dead matter (e.g., bacteria, fungi).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Example 1: The Niger Delta Mangrove Ecosystem

Mangrove trees (producers) grow in the swampy creeks. Crabs and fish (primary consumers) feed on mangrove leaves. Larger fish and birds (secondary consumers) eat the crabs. Decomposers like bacteria break down dead leaves, recycling nutrients. This ecosystem is vital for local fishing communities.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Example 2: The Guinea Savanna Food Web

Grasses (producers) are eaten by zebras and antelopes (primary consumers). Lions and hyenas (secondary/tertiary consumers) hunt these herbivores. Vultures and termites (decomposers) clean up carcasses. This balance is essential for the savanna's health.

⭐ Fun Fact

Did you know? In Nigeria, the Lake Chad Basin once supported millions of people and wildlife. Due to climate change and human activities, the lake has shrunk by 90% since the 1960s, showing how fragile an ecosystem can be!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What is the main difference between a food chain and a food web?
  2. Question 2: In a Nigerian pond ecosystem, name one producer and one primary consumer.
  3. Question 3: Why are decomposers important in an ecosystem? Give one example from Nigeria.

✅ Answers

Answer 1: A food chain shows a single path of energy flow (e.g., grass → zebra → lion), while a food web shows multiple interconnected food chains, representing real-life feeding relationships.

Answer 2: Producer: Water lettuce or algae. Primary consumer: Tilapia fish or tadpoles.

Answer 3: Decomposers break down dead organisms and waste, returning nutrients to the soil for producers. Example: Fungi in a Nigerian forest decompose fallen leaves.
