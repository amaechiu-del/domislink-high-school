Key Concepts in Heredity

  • Heredity is the passing of traits from parents to offspring through genes.
  • Genes are segments of DNA found on chromosomes — they code for specific traits (e.g., eye colour, height).
  • Chromosomes come in pairs; humans have 23 pairs (46 total). One set comes from the mother, one from the father.
  • Dominant traits appear when at least one dominant allele is present (e.g., dark skin in some crosses).
  • Recessive traits only appear when both alleles are recessive (e.g., albinism).
  • Genotype is the genetic make-up (e.g., AA, Aa, aa); phenotype is the observable trait.

Nigerian Examples

🌍 Example 1 – Sickle Cell Trait (Nigeria)
In many Nigerian families, the sickle cell gene (HbS) is inherited. If both parents carry one copy (AS), there is a 25% chance their child will have sickle cell disease (SS). This is a classic example of recessive inheritance.
🌍 Example 2 – Skin Colour Variation (Nigeria)
Among the Yoruba, Igbo, and Hausa peoples, skin colour ranges from dark to light. Multiple genes control this trait. A child may have lighter skin than both parents if recessive alleles combine — showing how heredity can produce variety.
💡 Fun Fact: Did you know? The Nigerian dwarf goat passes its small size to kids through a dominant gene! Farmers use this knowledge to breed smaller, easier-to-manage livestock.

Practice Questions

  1. What is the difference between genotype and phenotype? Give one example from the lesson.
  2. If two parents with genotype AS (sickle cell trait) have a child, what is the probability the child will have genotype SS?
  3. Why do children often look like a mix of both parents? Explain using the concept of chromosomes.

✅ Answers

  1. Genotype is the genetic makeup (e.g., AS), while phenotype is the observable trait (e.g., having sickle cell trait or disease).
  2. 25% (or 1 in 4) — from the cross AS × AS, the possible genotypes are AA, AS, AS, SS.
  3. Each parent contributes one chromosome from every pair. The child receives half their chromosomes from mother and half from father, so they inherit a blend of both parents’ genes.
