Advanced Grammar: The Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, uncertainty, or possibility about a situation. It is often used in clauses beginning with "if," "wish," or "it is necessary that."

Here are some key points about the subjunctive mood:

  • It is used to express doubt or uncertainty about a situation.
  • It is often used in clauses beginning with "if," "wish," or "it is necessary that."
  • It can be used to express possibility or potentiality.

Here are two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: "It is necessary that the students study hard for their exams." (Here, the subjunctive mood is used to express necessity or importance.)

Example 2: "I wish it were possible to travel to the United States." (Here, the subjunctive mood is used to express a desire or wish.)

Fun Fact: The subjunctive mood is not as commonly used in modern English as it was in the past. However, it is still an important part of the language and can be used to add nuance and complexity to writing and speech.

Practice Questions:

1. Which of the following sentences uses the subjunctive mood?

A) I go to school every day.

B) It is necessary that I go to school every day.

C) I wish I could go to school every day.

Answer: B) It is necessary that I go to school every day.

2. Complete the sentence: "I suggest that he _______ more time studying for his exams."

A) study

B) studies

C) shall study

Answer: A) study

3. Which of the following sentences uses the subjunctive mood to express a wish?

A) I hope it will rain tomorrow.

B) I wish it would rain tomorrow.

C) It is necessary that it rain tomorrow.

Answer: B) I wish it would rain tomorrow.
