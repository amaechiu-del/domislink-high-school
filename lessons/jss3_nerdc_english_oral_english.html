Introduction to Oral English

Oral English is a crucial aspect of the English language that involves the effective use of spoken language to communicate ideas, thoughts, and opinions. It encompasses various skills, including speaking, listening, and pronunciation.

Key aspects of Oral English include:

  • Fluency: The ability to speak smoothly and continuously.
  • Accuracy: The ability to use correct grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation.
  • Appropriacy: The ability to use language suitable for the context and audience.

Let's consider two Nigerian examples:

In a Nigerian market, a vendor might say, "How much you buy?" which is an example of informal oral English. On the other hand, in a formal setting like a conference, a speaker might say, "I would like to discuss the current economic trends in Nigeria," which is an example of formal oral English.

Fun Fact

Did you know that Nigeria has over 250 ethnic groups, each with its own language and dialect? This diversity has contributed to the country's rich oral tradition, with storytelling and oral poetry being essential parts of Nigerian culture.
