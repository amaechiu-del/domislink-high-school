Summary in English

A summary is a brief statement that presents the main points of a text, leaving out unnecessary details. It helps readers understand the main idea of the text without having to read the entire content.

The following are key points to note when writing a summary:

  • Identify the main idea of the text
  • Leave out unnecessary details
  • Use your own words to paraphrase the text
  • Keep it brief and concise

Here are two Nigerian examples:

In Chinua Achebe's novel "Things Fall Apart", a summary could be: The novel tells the story of Okonkwo, a strong and proud Igbo man, and his struggles with the changes brought by British colonialism.

In Wole Soyinka's play "The Lion and the Jewel", a summary could be: The play is a comedy that tells the story of a village's struggle to maintain its cultural heritage in the face of modernization.

Fun Fact

Did you know that summarizing a text can help improve your understanding and retention of the material? It's like creating a mental map of the main ideas!

Practice Questions

1. What is the main purpose of a summary?

Answer: To present the main points of a text briefly and concisely.

2. What should you leave out when writing a summary?

Answer: Unnecessary details.

3. Why is it important to use your own words when writing a summary?

Answer: To avoid plagiarism and to show that you understand the material.
