Algebraic Processes

Algebraic processes involve the use of variables, constants, and mathematical operations to solve equations and inequalities. In this lesson, we will explore the basics of algebraic processes and how they are used in real-life situations.

The following are key points to note about algebraic processes:

  • Variables are letters or symbols that represent unknown values.
  • Constants are numbers that do not change value.
  • Algebraic expressions are combinations of variables, constants, and mathematical operations.

Let's consider two examples from Nigeria:

Example 1: A farmer in Kano State has 15 bags of rice to sell at the market. If he sells each bag for ₦5,000, how much money will he make in total? This can be represented algebraically as 15x, where x is the price per bag.

Example 2: A student in Lagos State scores 80 marks in a mathematics test. If she scores 20 marks in each of her 4 subjects, what is her total score? This can be represented algebraically as 4x, where x is the score per subject.

Fun Fact: The word "algebra" comes from the Arabic word "al-jabr", which means "reunion of broken parts". This refers to the process of solving equations by combining like terms.

Now, let's practice what we have learned. Try to solve the following questions:

1. If a shopkeeper in Abuja has 20 boxes of goods to sell, and each box contains 12 items, how many items does he have in total?

Answer: 20 x 12 = 240 items

2. A car travels from Lagos to Ibadan at an average speed of 60 km/h. If the distance between the two cities is 120 km, how long does the journey take?

Answer: Time = Distance / Speed = 120 km / 60 km/h = 2 hours

3. A student in Port Harcourt has ₦500 to spend on books. If each book costs ₦50, how many books can he buy?

Answer: Number of books = Total amount / Cost per book = ₦500 / ₦50 = 10 books
