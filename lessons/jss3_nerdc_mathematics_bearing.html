Bearing in Mathematics

Bearing is a measure of direction, and it is used to describe the position of one point relative to another. It is measured in degrees, with 0° being north, 90° being east, 180° being south, and 270° being west.

Here are some key points about bearing:

  • Bearing is measured clockwise from north.
  • It is used to describe the position of one point relative to another.
  • Bearing can be measured in degrees, with 0° being north, 90° being east, 180° being south, and 270° being west.

Here are two Nigerian examples:

Example 1: A ship is sailing from Lagos to Port Harcourt. If the ship is sailing on a bearing of 270°, it means that it is sailing west.

Example 2: A plane is flying from Abuja to Kano. If the plane is flying on a bearing of 45°, it means that it is flying northeast.

Fun Fact

Did you know that bearing is used in navigation to determine the direction of travel? It is an essential concept in mathematics and is used in many real-world applications, including aviation, maritime, and land surveying.

Practice Questions:

1. A person is walking on a bearing of 120°. What direction is the person walking?

Answer: Southeast

2. A plane is flying on a bearing of 225°. What direction is the plane flying?

Answer: Southwest

3. A ship is sailing on a bearing of 315°. What direction is the ship sailing?

Answer: Northwest
