Statistics

Statistics is a branch of mathematics that deals with the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data.

The process of statistics involves:

  • Collecting data from a sample or population
  • Organizing and summarizing the data
  • Analyzing the data to draw conclusions
  • Presenting the results in a clear and meaningful way

Examples of statistics in Nigeria include:

  • The National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) conducts surveys to collect data on the country's population, economy, and infrastructure.
  • The Nigerian Football Federation uses statistics to analyze the performance of football teams and players in the Nigerian Professional Football League.

Fun Fact

Did you know that the word "statistics" comes from the German word "Statistik", which means "state affairs" or "political science"?

Practice Questions

1. What is the main purpose of statistics?

Answer: The main purpose of statistics is to collect, analyze, and interpret data to draw conclusions and make informed decisions.

2. What is the difference between a sample and a population in statistics?

Answer: A sample is a subset of individuals or data points selected from a larger population, while a population is the entire group of individuals or data points that one is interested in understanding or describing.

3. What is the role of the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) in Nigeria?

Answer: The National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) is responsible for collecting, analyzing, and disseminating statistical data on various aspects of the Nigerian economy and society.
