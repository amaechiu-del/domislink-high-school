What Are Global Issues?

Global issues are problems that affect people all over the world. No single country can solve them alone — they require international cooperation. They cross borders and impact our environment, economy, health, and security.

Key Global Issues Explained

  • Climate Change – Rising temperatures, extreme weather, and melting ice caps caused by greenhouse gas emissions. It threatens food security and displaces communities.
  • Poverty – Over 700 million people live on less than $1.90 per day. Poverty limits access to education, healthcare, and clean water.
  • Armed Conflict – Wars and civil unrest destroy infrastructure, create refugees, and disrupt economies. Many conflicts are fueled by competition for resources.
  • Pandemics & Disease – Outbreaks like COVID-19 and Ebola spread rapidly across borders, overwhelming healthcare systems and disrupting global trade.
  • Inequality – The gap between rich and poor continues to widen, both within countries and between nations, limiting opportunities for millions.

Nigerian Examples

  • Climate Change: The shrinking of Lake Chad has reduced water supply for farming and fishing in northeastern Nigeria, forcing many families to migrate and contributing to regional instability.
  • Poverty & Inequality: In Lagos, extreme wealth exists alongside sprawling slums like Makoko, where residents lack basic sanitation and electricity — a vivid example of the global inequality crisis.
🌟 Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria has the highest rate of deforestation in Africa? We lose about 3.7% of our forest cover every year — that's roughly 400,000 hectares — due to logging, agriculture, and fuelwood collection. This contributes to global climate change!

Practice Questions

  2. Name two ways that climate change affects people in Nigeria.
  3. What is one example of inequality mentioned in this lesson?

Answers

  1. Because these problems cross national borders (e.g., pollution, disease, economic effects) and require cooperation between many countries to address effectively.
  2. It reduces water in Lake Chad (affecting farmers and fishermen) and causes deforestation that worsens weather patterns and food production.
  3. The gap between rich and poor in cities like Lagos, where wealthy neighborhoods exist next to slums with no basic services.
