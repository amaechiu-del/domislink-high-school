Human Rights (JSS3 Social Studies - NERDC Syllabus)

Key Points About Human Rights

  • What are Human Rights? They are basic rights and freedoms that every person is entitled to, simply because they are human. They are universal, inalienable, and equal for all.
  • Why are they important? Human rights protect our dignity, ensure we are treated fairly, and allow us to live freely and safely. They form the foundation of justice and peace in any society.
  • Key examples: Right to life, right to education, freedom of speech, right to a fair trial, freedom from torture, and right to healthcare.
  • Who protects them? Governments, international bodies like the United Nations, and organisations like the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) in Nigeria work to uphold these rights.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In 2020, the Nigerian government passed the Child Rights Act (already adopted by many states) to protect children from child labour, early marriage, and abuse. This ensures the right to education and safety for all Nigerian children.

Example 2: During the #EndSARS protests in 2020, many young Nigerians exercised their right to peaceful assembly and freedom of speech to demand an end to police brutality. The protests highlighted the importance of these rights in holding authorities accountable.

Fun Fact 🎉

Did you know? Nigeria’s Constitution (1999, as amended) guarantees human rights in Chapter IV (Sections 33–46). It includes the right to life, dignity of the human person, personal liberty, fair hearing, and freedom of thought, conscience, and religion—all enforceable in a court of law!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Name any two human rights guaranteed by the Nigerian Constitution.
  2. Question 2: Why is the right to education important for Nigerian children?
  3. Question 3: What is the role of the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) in Nigeria?

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Right to life and freedom of speech (or any other valid rights like right to fair hearing, right to dignity, etc.).
  2. Answer 2: The right to education helps children gain knowledge and skills to become productive citizens, break the cycle of poverty, and contribute to national development.
  3. Answer 3: The NHRC investigates complaints of human rights violations, educates the public about their rights, and recommends actions to the government to protect citizens' rights.
