International Organizations: Bringing the World Together

Key Points

  • International Organizations are groups of countries that work together to solve common problems.
  • United Nations (UN) – founded in 1945, aims to maintain peace and security worldwide.
  • African Union (AU) – promotes unity and development among African nations.
  • World Health Organization (WHO) – coordinates global health responses and sets health standards.
  • UNICEF – helps children around the world with education, healthcare, and protection.
  • These organizations hold regular meetings, pass resolutions, and provide aid to countries in need.

🇳🇬 Nigeria's Involvement

Example 1: Nigeria is a founding member of the African Union (AU) and actively participates in peacekeeping missions in other African countries, such as in Liberia and Sierra Leone.

Example 2: Nigeria works closely with the World Health Organization (WHO) to fight diseases like polio and malaria. In 2020, Nigeria was declared polio-free thanks to joint efforts with WHO and UNICEF.

🌟 Fun Fact

Did you know? The United Nations has 193 member countries – that's almost every country in the world! The only UN member that is not a fully recognized country is the Holy See (Vatican City), which is a permanent observer.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What is the main purpose of the United Nations (UN)?
    Answer: To maintain international peace and security, and to promote cooperation among nations.
  2. Name one Nigerian contribution to an international organization.
    Answer: Nigeria sends troops for peacekeeping missions in African countries under the African Union (e.g., Liberia, Sierra Leone).
  3. Which international organization focuses on children's welfare worldwide?
    Answer: UNICEF (United Nations Children's Fund).
