Energy and Society

Energy is the ability to do work. In society, energy powers our homes, industries, transportation, and technology. Understanding energy sources and their impact on the environment is crucial for sustainable development.

Key Points

  • Energy Sources: Energy comes from renewable (solar, wind, hydro) and non-renewable (coal, oil, gas) sources.
  • Energy Conversion: Energy changes form—solar panels convert sunlight to electricity; turbines convert wind to electrical energy.
  • Energy in Society: Every sector—agriculture, transport, manufacturing, communication—depends on affordable energy.
  • Sustainability: Over-reliance on fossil fuels causes pollution and climate change; renewable energy is cleaner and sustainable.
  • Energy Conservation: Simple actions like turning off lights, using efficient appliances, and reducing waste save energy and money.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Kainji Dam on the Niger River generates hydroelectric power, supplying electricity to many Nigerian homes and industries. This is a renewable energy source using water flow.

Example 2: In northern Nigeria, solar-powered boreholes provide clean water for communities. Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity to pump water, reducing reliance on diesel generators.

⭐ Fun Fact

Nigeria receives abundant sunshine all year round—enough to generate over 400,000 MW of solar power! However, we currently use less than 5% of that potential. Imagine the possibilities!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Name two renewable energy sources suitable for Nigeria and explain why they are important for society.
  2. Question 2: What is the main difference between renewable and non-renewable energy? Give one example of each.
  3. Question 3: Describe one way energy conservation can benefit a Nigerian household.

Answers

  • Answer 1: Solar energy (from sunlight) and hydroelectric energy (from rivers) are renewable. They reduce pollution, lower electricity costs, and provide power to remote areas where the national grid does not reach.
  • Answer 2: Renewable energy can be replenished naturally (e.g., sunlight, wind, water), while non-renewable energy is finite and takes millions of years to form (e.g., crude oil, coal).
  • Answer 3: Turning off lights and fans when not in use reduces electricity bills, saves money for the family, and decreases the demand on the national grid, helping to prevent blackouts.
