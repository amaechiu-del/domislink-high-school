Pollution Control – Basic Science (JSS3)

Key Points on Pollution Control

  • Pollution control means reducing or eliminating harmful substances from the environment.
  • It involves prevention (stopping pollution before it happens) and remediation (cleaning up existing pollution).
  • Common methods include: using filters on factory chimneys, treating sewage before releasing it into rivers, and recycling waste materials.
  • Individuals can help by reducing waste, reusing items, and recycling plastics, metals, and paper.
  • Laws such as the National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency (NESREA) Act help control pollution in Nigeria.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In the Niger Delta, oil spills have polluted rivers and farmlands. Control measures include using booms and skimmers to collect spilled oil, and planting mangroves to restore ecosystems.

Example 2: In Lagos, the government has introduced waste collection and recycling programs (like the "Lagos Waste Management Authority") to reduce plastic and household waste clogging drainage channels and polluting the lagoon.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that one recycled aluminum can saves enough energy to power a TV for three hours? In Nigeria, recycling just 10% more cans could save enough electricity to light thousands of homes for a day!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main purpose of installing filters in factory chimneys?
    Answer: To trap harmful particles and gases before they are released into the air, reducing air pollution.
  2. Give one way individuals can help control pollution in their community.
    Answer: Sorting waste for recycling (e.g., separating plastics, glass, and paper) or using reusable bags instead of plastic bags.
  3. Name one Nigerian law or agency responsible for pollution control.
    Answer: The National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency (NESREA) or the NESREA Act.
