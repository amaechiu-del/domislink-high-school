Space Exploration: JSS3 Basic Science (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Space Exploration is the use of astronomy and space technology to study outer space.
  • Artificial Satellites are human-made objects placed in orbit around Earth for communication, weather monitoring, and scientific research.
  • Rockets provide the thrust needed to overcome Earth's gravity and launch spacecraft into orbit.
  • Orbit is the curved path a celestial object or satellite follows around a larger body due to gravity.
  • Benefits: Improved communication (e.g., mobile phones, TV), weather forecasting, GPS navigation, and discovery of new planets and stars.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • NigeriaSat-1: Launched in 2003, Nigeria's first Earth observation satellite. It helped monitor environmental changes, agriculture, and disaster management across Nigeria.
  • Nigeria Communications Satellite (NigComSat-1R): Launched in 2011, this satellite provides communication services including internet, television, and telephone connectivity to rural areas in Nigeria and West Africa.

⭐ Fun Fact

Did you know? The International Space Station (ISS) orbits Earth at about 28,000 km/h! That means it goes around the entire planet in just 90 minutes. Astronauts on the ISS see 16 sunrises and 16 sunsets every day!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What is the name of the force that keeps satellites in orbit around the Earth?
  2. List two benefits of space exploration to Nigeria.
  3. Name one Nigerian satellite and its primary function.

✅ Answers

  1. Gravity (Earth's gravitational pull keeps satellites in orbit).
  2. Any two: Improved communication (e.g., internet/TV), weather forecasting, environmental monitoring, GPS navigation, or agricultural planning.
  3. NigeriaSat-1 (Earth observation for environmental monitoring) OR NigComSat-1R (communication services like internet and TV).
