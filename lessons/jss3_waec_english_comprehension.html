Comprehension: Unlocking Meaning in Texts

📌 Key Points for Comprehension

  • Read the passage carefully — at least twice. The first time for general meaning, the second for details.
  • Identify the main idea — what is the passage mostly about? Look at the topic sentence (often the first sentence).
  • Note supporting details — facts, examples, or explanations that back up the main idea.
  • Use context clues — if you don't know a word, read the sentences around it to guess its meaning.
  • Understand the author's purpose — is the passage informing, persuading, entertaining, or describing?
  • Answer in your own words — don't copy directly from the passage unless the question asks for a quote.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (from a WAEC passage):
"The harmattan season in Northern Nigeria brings with it a dry, dusty wind that reduces visibility. Farmers often prepare by storing water and covering their crops."

Question: Why do farmers cover their crops during harmattan?
Answer: To protect them from the dry, dusty wind that could damage them.

Example 2 (from a Nigerian folktale):
"Tortoise begged the birds to lend him feathers so he could fly to the feast in the sky. The birds agreed, but Tortoise's greed made him keep all the food for himself."

Question: What character flaw did Tortoise show?
Answer: Greed — he kept all the food instead of sharing with the birds who helped him.

🎉 Fun Fact!

Did you know that the word "comprehension" comes from the Latin comprehendere, meaning "to grasp or seize"? In the WAEC English exam, comprehension passages often include topics like Nigerian culture, science, and current affairs — so reading newspapers and magazines can really boost your skill!

✏️ Practice Questions

Read this short passage and answer the questions below:

In many Nigerian villages, the town crier plays an important role. He walks through the streets beating a wooden gong and announcing news from the village head. This tradition has existed for centuries, but today, some villages use loudspeakers to spread information. However, the town crier remains a respected figure because he connects the people to their heritage.

  1. What instrument does the town crier use to attract attention?
    Answer: He beats a wooden gong.
  2. Why is the town crier still respected today?
    Answer: Because he connects the people to their heritage.
  3. What modern tool is sometimes used instead of the traditional method?
    Answer: Loudspeakers.
