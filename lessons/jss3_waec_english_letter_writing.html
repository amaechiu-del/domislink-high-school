Letter Writing (WAEC Syllabus - JSS3 English)

Key Elements of a Formal Letter

  • Sender's Address — written at the top right corner (no name).
  • Date — placed below the sender's address.
  • Recipient's Address — written on the left, below the date.
  • Salutation — e.g., "Dear Sir," or "Dear Madam,".
  • Subject Line — a short phrase summarizing the letter's purpose (optional but recommended).
  • Body — the main message, divided into clear paragraphs.
  • Closing — "Yours faithfully," (if salutation used "Dear Sir/Madam") or "Yours sincerely," (if name used).
  • Signature & Name — sign above your printed full name.

Two Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Application for a School Prefect Position

Sender: 23, Oyo Street, Ibadan, Oyo State.
Date: 15th March 2025
Recipient: The Principal, St. John's Secondary School, Ibadan.
Salutation: Dear Sir,
Subject: Application for the Post of Head Boy
Body: I am writing to express my interest in the position of Head Boy for the 2024/2025 academic session. I have demonstrated leadership as class captain for three years and maintained an excellent academic record. I promise to uphold discipline and represent the school with integrity.
Closing: Yours faithfully,
Signature: (signed)
Name: Chukwudi Okonkwo

Example 2: Complaint Letter to a Local Government Chairman

Sender: 7, Market Road, Enugu, Enugu State.
Date: 22nd June 2025
Recipient: The Chairman, Enugu South Local Government, Enugu.
Salutation: Dear Sir,
Subject: Complaint About Poor Road Condition in Abakpa
Body: I write on behalf of the residents of Abakpa to draw your attention to the deplorable state of Abakpa Road. The road has become impassable during rains, causing accidents and hindering commercial activities. We humbly request urgent rehabilitation.
Closing: Yours faithfully,
Signature: (signed)
Name: Ngozi Eze

Fun Fact

Did you know? In Nigeria, the first postage stamp was issued in 1914, and letters were carried by bicycle or on foot before the railway expanded! Today, formal letters are still required for official applications, bank requests, and school admissions.

Practice Questions

  1. What is the correct closing for a letter that begins with "Dear Sir"?
  2. List three items that must appear in the sender's address block of a formal letter.
  3. Why is a subject line important in a formal letter? Give one reason.

Answers

  1. "Yours faithfully,"
  2. House number and street, town/city, state (and optionally, country).
  3. It helps the reader immediately know the purpose of the letter, making it easier to process and respond.
