🔤 What is Phonetics?

Phonetics is the study of speech sounds — how they are produced, transmitted, and heard. In the WAEC English syllabus, you must understand the 44 sounds of English (24 consonants + 20 vowels) and how to transcribe them using the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA).

📌 Key Concepts

  • Consonants — sounds produced with obstruction in the vocal tract (e.g., /p/, /b/, /t/, /d/, /k/, /g/)
  • Vowels — sounds produced with no obstruction; they can be short (/ɪ/, /e/, /æ/, /ɒ/, /ʌ/, /ʊ/) or long (/iː/, /uː/, /ɔː/, /ɜː/, /ɑː/)
  • Diphthongs — two vowel sounds gliding together (e.g., /aɪ/ in "price", /ɔɪ/ in "boy", /eə/ in "hair")
  • Voiced vs Voiceless — feel your throat: /b/ (voiced) vs /p/ (voiceless); /d/ vs /t/; /g/ vs /k/
  • Stress — syllables pronounced with more force; e.g., PREsent (noun) vs preSENT (verb)

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The word "water" — Nigerian English often pronounces it /ˈwɔːtə/ instead of /ˈwɔːtə/ (standard). The /t/ is often pronounced as a flap [ɾ] in fast speech.

Example 2: The word "bag" — many Nigerians use /bæg/ with a short /æ/ sound, while standard British English uses /bæɡ/. Also, the final /g/ may be devoiced to /k/ in some Nigerian accents.

🎉 Fun Fact!

The English language has only 26 letters but 44 sounds! That's why spelling can be tricky — the letter 'a' can sound different in cat (/æ/), car (/ɑː/), care (/eə/), and cake (/eɪ/).

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which of the following words contains a voiceless consonant at the beginning?
    A) dog B) cat C) goat D) van
  2. Question 2: Write the IPA symbol for the vowel sound in the Nigerian English pronunciation of "bed" (as commonly spoken in Lagos).
  3. Question 3: Identify the diphthong in the word "voice" and give its IPA symbol.

✅ Answers

  • Answer 1: B) cat — /k/ is voiceless (vocal cords do not vibrate).
  • Answer 2: /ɛ/ — the short 'e' sound (as in "bed" /bɛd/). Many Nigerians use /ɛ/ rather than /e/.
  • Answer 3: /ɔɪ/ — the diphthong in "voice" /vɔɪs/.
