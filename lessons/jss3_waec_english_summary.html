Summary Writing for JSS3 (WAEC Syllabus)

What is Summary?

A summary is a shortened version of a text that captures only the main points in your own words. It must be brief but complete.

Key Rules for Good Summary

  • Read carefully – Understand the passage before writing.
  • Identify key ideas – Ignore examples, repetitions, and minor details.
  • Use your own words – Do not copy sentences from the original.
  • Keep it short – Usually one-third of the original length.
  • Do not add opinions – Only include what the author said.
  • Check for completeness – Ensure all main ideas are included.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1:

Original: "The harmattan season in Northern Nigeria brings cold, dry winds and dusty air. Many people wear thick clothes and use moisturizers to protect their skin. Schools sometimes close early because of poor visibility."

Summary: "Harmattan in Northern Nigeria causes cold, dusty conditions, leading people to wear warm clothes and use skin protection, and sometimes causes school closures."

Example 2:

Original: "The Aso Rock in Abuja is a famous 400-metre rock formation. It is a popular tourist attraction and also houses the official residence of Nigeria's President. Many visitors come to see its unique shape and learn about its history."

Summary: "Aso Rock in Abuja is a 400-metre landmark that serves as both a tourist site and the location of the Nigerian President's official residence."

🎉 Fun Fact: The longest summary ever written was for a 1,500-page book, but it was still only 10 pages long! A good summary saves time while keeping all the important information.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Read this sentence: "The Niger River flows through Nigeria and provides water for farming, fishing, and transportation." Write a one-sentence summary.
    Answer: "The Niger River supports farming, fishing, and transportation in Nigeria."
  2. Question 2: What is the first and most important step in summary writing?
    Answer: Reading the passage carefully to understand it fully.
  3. Question 3: True or False: You should include your own opinions in a summary. Explain why.
    Answer: False. A summary must only contain the author's main ideas, not personal opinions or interpretations.
