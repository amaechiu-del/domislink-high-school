Vocabulary Development for JSS3 (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Vocabulary refers to the set of words a person knows and uses in communication.
  • Building strong vocabulary helps in reading comprehension, writing, and speaking effectively.
  • Learn words in context — understand how they are used in sentences.
  • Use a dictionary and thesaurus to discover meanings, synonyms, and antonyms.
  • Practice using new words in daily conversations and essays.
  • Pay attention to word families (e.g., govern, government, governor, governmental).

Nigerian Examples in Context

Example 1: The governor promised to ameliorate the suffering of the people by providing clean water in every village.

(Ameliorate = to make something bad become better)

Example 2: During the harmattan season, many people procure warm clothing and moisturizers to protect their skin.

(Procure = to obtain or get something with effort)

🎉 Fun Fact! The English language has over 170,000 words in current use, but the average Nigerian JSS3 student uses only about 5,000–10,000 words actively. Learning just 10 new words each week can double your vocabulary in one year!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What does the word “benevolent” mean?
    a) Mean and selfish
    b) Kind and generous
    c) Angry and aggressive
    d) Lazy and slow
  2. Question 2: Choose the correct synonym for “abundant”:
    a) Scarce
    b) Plentiful
    c) Tiny
    d) Weak
  3. Question 3: Use the word “initiative” in a sentence about a Nigerian student.

Answers

  • Answer 1: b) Kind and generous
  • Answer 2: b) Plentiful
  • Answer 3: (Sample answer) The student showed great initiative by organizing a reading club in her community.
