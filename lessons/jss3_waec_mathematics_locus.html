Locus in Mathematics (JSS3 WAEC)

What is a Locus?

A locus (plural: loci) is the set of all points that satisfy a given condition or rule. Think of it as the path traced by a moving point that follows a specific rule.

Animated SVG: Locus of Points Equidistant from a Fixed Point

The green point moves around the red fixed point, always staying 80 units away. The dashed blue circle is its locus.

Key Rules for Common Loci

  • Equidistant from a fixed point: The locus is a circle with the fixed point as centre.
  • Equidistant from two fixed points: The locus is the perpendicular bisector of the line joining the two points.
  • Equidistant from two intersecting lines: The locus is the angle bisector of the angle between the lines.
  • At a fixed distance from a line: The locus is a pair of parallel lines on either side of the given line.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A goat is tethered to a peg in the ground with a rope of length 5 metres. What is the locus of all positions the goat can reach?

Solution: The goat can move in any direction but is limited by the rope. The locus is a circle of radius 5 metres, with the peg as centre.

Example 2: In a school football field, two goal posts are 8 metres apart. A player wants to stand such that he is exactly the same distance from both posts. Describe his possible positions.

Solution: The set of points equidistant from the two goal posts is the perpendicular bisector of the line joining the posts. The player must stand anywhere on that straight line.

💡 Fun Fact: In navigation, the locus of points with the same distance from a lighthouse is a circle. Ships use these "circles of position" to determine their location at sea!

Practice Questions

  1. A point moves so that it is always 3 cm from a fixed point X. Draw and describe its locus.
  2. Two villages A and B are 10 km apart. A new well is to be built such that it is the same distance from both villages. On a map, where should the well be located?
  3. A ladder 6 m long leans against a vertical wall. As the ladder slides down, its foot moves away from the wall. Describe the locus of the midpoint of the ladder.

Answers

  1. A circle of radius 3 cm with centre at X.
  2. On the perpendicular bisector of the line joining A and B.
  3. The midpoint traces a quarter-circle of radius 3 m (half the ladder length) with centre at the corner where wall meets ground.
