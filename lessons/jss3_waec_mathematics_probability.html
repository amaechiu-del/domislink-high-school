What is Probability?

Probability tells us how likely something is to happen. It is always a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).

  • Formula: Probability = (Number of favourable outcomes) ÷ (Total number of possible outcomes)
  • Probability can be written as a fraction, decimal, or percentage.
  • If an event has probability p, the chance it does not happen is 1 – p.
  • All possible outcomes together add up to 1.

🎯 Nigerian Example 1 — School Quiz

In a JSS3 class of 40 students, 15 are boys and 25 are girls. If a student is chosen at random, what is the probability that it is a girl?

Solution: Favourable outcomes = 25 girls. Total outcomes = 40 students. Probability = 25/40 = 5/8.

🎯 Nigerian Example 2 — Market Bags

A trader has 10 bags of rice, 6 bags of beans, and 4 bags of garri. If a bag is picked at random, what is the probability it is not rice?

Solution: Total bags = 10+6+4 = 20. Not rice means beans or garri = 6+4 = 10. Probability = 10/20 = 1/2.

💡 Fun Fact: The word “probability” comes from the Latin probabilitas, meaning “provable”. The modern theory of probability began in the 1600s when French mathematicians Pascal and Fermat exchanged letters about gambling problems!

✏️ Practice Questions

  1. A bag contains 3 red, 5 blue, and 2 green marbles. One marble is picked at random. What is the probability of picking a blue marble?
  2. In a school of 200 students, 80 are in JSS1, 70 in JSS2, and 50 in JSS3. If one student is chosen at random, what is the probability they are in JSS3?
  3. A fair coin is tossed once. What is the probability of getting a tail?

✅ Answers

  1. Total marbles = 3+5+2 = 10. Blue = 5. Probability = 5/10 = 1/2.
  2. JSS3 students = 50. Total = 200. Probability = 50/200 = 1/4.
  3. A fair coin has 2 sides (head and tail). Probability of tail = 1/2.
