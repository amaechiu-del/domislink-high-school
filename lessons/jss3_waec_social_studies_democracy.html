Democracy in Social Studies (JSS3 WAEC Syllabus)

Key Principles of Democracy

  • Rule of Law: Every citizen, including leaders, must obey the law. No one is above the law.
  • Free and Fair Elections: Regular elections allow citizens to choose their leaders without intimidation or fraud.
  • Separation of Powers: Government is divided into three branches (Executive, Legislature, Judiciary) to prevent abuse of power.
  • Fundamental Rights: Citizens enjoy rights such as freedom of speech, assembly, and religion.
  • Majority Rule with Minority Rights: Decisions are made by majority vote, but the rights of minority groups are protected.
  • Citizen Participation: People have the right to vote, run for office, and engage in public debate.

Nigerian Examples of Democracy in Action

Example 1: 2023 General Elections – Nigeria held presidential and National Assembly elections in February 2023. Despite challenges, millions of Nigerians voted to elect President Bola Tinubu, demonstrating the democratic process of choosing leaders through the ballot box.

Example 2: Freedom of the Press – In Nigeria, independent newspapers and TV stations (like Channels TV and The Guardian) regularly criticise government policies. This freedom, protected by the constitution, allows journalists to hold leaders accountable, a key feature of democracy.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria is often called the "Giant of Africa" because it has the largest population of any African country? Since returning to civilian rule in 1999, Nigeria has had six peaceful transfers of power between political parties – a sign of a maturing democracy!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What does "Separation of Powers" mean in a democracy?
    Answer: It means dividing government into three branches (Executive, Legislature, Judiciary) to prevent any one branch from becoming too powerful.
  2. Question 2: Give one Nigerian example of how citizens exercise their democratic rights.
    Answer: Nigerians vote in elections (e.g., 2023 presidential election) to choose their leaders. Another example is citizens protesting peacefully or expressing opinions in the media.
  3. Question 3: Why is "Rule of Law" important for democracy?
    Answer: It ensures that everyone, including government officials, is subject to the same laws, preventing dictatorship and protecting citizens' rights.
