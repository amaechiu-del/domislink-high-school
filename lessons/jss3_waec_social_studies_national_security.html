National Security in Social Studies

Key Points about National Security

  • Definition: National security is the protection of a country's citizens, economy, and institutions from internal and external threats.
  • Elements: It includes military defense, law enforcement, economic stability, cyber security, and disaster management.
  • Importance: Ensures peace, order, and development; protects sovereignty and territorial integrity.
  • Threats: Terrorism, armed robbery, kidnapping, cyber attacks, human trafficking, and environmental disasters.
  • Responsibility: Every citizen has a role — reporting suspicious activities, obeying laws, and supporting security agencies.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Boko Haram Insurgency
Since 2009, Nigeria has faced the Boko Haram terrorist group in the Northeast. The government deployed the military and formed the Multi-National Joint Task Force (MNJTF) with neighboring countries to combat the threat. This shows how national security involves both military action and international cooperation.

Example 2: Cyber Security and Financial Fraud
Nigeria's Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) works to protect citizens from internet fraud (e.g., "Yahoo Yahoo" scams). The Cybercrime Act of 2015 was passed to prosecute offenders, safeguarding the nation's digital space and economic reputation.

🎉 Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria's National Security Adviser (NSA) coordinates all security agencies, including the Army, Navy, Air Force, Police, DSS, and NIA? The NSA reports directly to the President!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define national security and list two elements that make it up.
    Answer: National security is the protection of a country's citizens, economy, and institutions from threats. Two elements are military defense and law enforcement.
  2. Question 2: Mention one internal threat to Nigeria's national security and one agency that tackles it.
    Answer: Internal threat: Armed robbery/kidnapping. Agency: Nigeria Police Force or EFCC (depending on the crime).
  3. Question 3: How can ordinary citizens contribute to national security? Give two ways.
    Answer: Citizens can contribute by: (i) reporting suspicious activities to security agencies, and (ii) obeying the laws of the land.
