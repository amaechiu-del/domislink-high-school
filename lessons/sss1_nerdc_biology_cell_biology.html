Cell Biology: The Building Blocks of Life

Key Parts of a Cell

  • Cell Membrane – Controls what enters and leaves the cell.
  • Nucleus – Contains DNA; controls all cell activities.
  • Mitochondrion – Powerhouse of the cell; releases energy.
  • Vacuole – Stores water, food, and waste.
  • Cell Wall – Provides support and shape (only in plants).

Nigerian Examples

  • Cassava Plant Cells: The cell walls in cassava leaves help support the plant as it grows in Nigerian farms, while vacuoles store water during dry seasons.
  • Yam Tuber Cells: Yam cells have large vacuoles that store starch, providing energy for sprouting when planted in the rainy season.
🎉 Fun Fact! The longest cell in the human body is the neuron (nerve cell) – some can be over 1 metre long! In Nigeria, the giant African snail also has very long nerve cells to help it sense its environment.

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main function of the mitochondrion in a cell?
  2. Name one difference between a plant cell and an animal cell.
  3. If a cell loses its nucleus, what important process will it not be able to control?

Answers

  • 1. The mitochondrion releases energy for the cell through respiration.
  • 2. Plant cells have a cell wall, but animal cells do not. (Other correct answers: plant cells have chloroplasts, or a large central vacuole.)
  • 3. It will not be able to control its activities or reproduce, because the nucleus contains the genetic instructions (DNA).
