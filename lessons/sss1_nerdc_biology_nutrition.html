Key Nutrients & Their Functions

  • Carbohydrates – Provide energy for daily activities (e.g., yam, rice, garri).
  • Proteins – Build and repair body tissues (e.g., beans, fish, eggs).
  • Fats & Oils – Store energy and protect organs (e.g., palm oil, groundnut oil).
  • Vitamins – Help fight diseases and keep the body healthy (e.g., vitamin C from oranges).
  • Minerals – Strengthen bones and teeth (e.g., calcium from milk, iron from spinach).
  • Water – Essential for digestion, circulation, and temperature control.
  • Dietary Fibre – Aids digestion and prevents constipation (e.g., vegetables, fruits).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • Example 1 – Local Swallow Meal: A plate of pounded yam (carbohydrates) with egusi soup (proteins from melon seeds, vitamins from vegetables like ugu leaves). This combination provides energy, growth, and disease protection.
  • Example 2 – Student Snack: Boiled groundnuts (proteins and healthy fats) with ripe plantain (carbohydrates and vitamin A). A common roadside snack that gives quick energy and helps body repair.
🧠 Fun Fact: Did you know that ukwa (African breadfruit seed) is sometimes called "the Nigerian superfood"? It contains more protein than most grains and is rich in healthy fats – perfect for growing SSS1 students!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A student eats only garri (cassava flakes) for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Which major nutrient is missing from their diet, and what health problem could develop?
  2. Question 2: Name two Nigerian foods that are rich in proteins and explain why proteins are important for teenagers.
  3. Question 3: Why is it important to include vegetables like ugu or bitter leaf in your soup, even if you already eat enough carbohydrates?

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: Proteins and vitamins are missing. The student may suffer from slow growth, weak muscles, frequent illness, or poor wound healing.
  2. Answer 2: Examples: beans (e.g., moin moin, akara) and fish (e.g., smoked mackerel). Proteins are vital for teenagers because they support rapid growth, muscle development, and repair of body tissues during adolescence.
  3. Answer 3: Vegetables provide vitamins (like vitamin A and C) and minerals (like iron and calcium) that carbohydrates alone cannot supply. They help prevent diseases, improve vision, strengthen bones, and boost the immune system.
