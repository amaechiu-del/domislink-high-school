Tissues: The Building Blocks of Life

Biology – SSS1 (NERDC Syllabus)

Key Points

  • Tissue is a group of similar cells that work together to perform a specific function.
  • There are four main types in animals: epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissue.
  • Epithelial tissue covers body surfaces and lines internal organs (e.g., skin, mouth lining).
  • Connective tissue supports and binds other tissues (e.g., bone, blood, cartilage).
  • Muscle tissue enables movement (e.g., skeletal muscles, heart muscle).
  • Nervous tissue transmits electrical signals and controls body activities (e.g., brain, spinal cord).
  • In plants, tissues are classified as meristematic (dividing) and permanent (mature) tissues.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • Cassava (Manihot esculenta): The storage root contains parenchyma tissue (a type of plant permanent tissue) that stores starch, a staple food in Nigeria.
  • Goat meat (chevon): The muscle tissue in goat meat is rich in protein and commonly consumed in Nigerian dishes like pepper soup and suya.

🎉 Fun Fact: The human body has about 200 different cell types that organize into just four primary tissues! Your heart beats because of specialized muscle tissue that never gets tired – it contracts about 100,000 times a day.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which type of tissue lines the inside of your mouth and protects underlying layers?
    Answer: Epithelial tissue.
  2. Question 2: Name one example of connective tissue found in the human body and state its function.
    Answer: Blood (connective tissue) – transports oxygen, nutrients, and waste products around the body. (Also accept: bone – provides support and protection.)
  3. Question 3: In the cassava plant, which tissue stores the starch we eat as garri or fufu?
    Answer: Parenchyma tissue (a type of permanent plant tissue).
