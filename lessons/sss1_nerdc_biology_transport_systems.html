Transport Systems in Biology

Topic: Transport Systems in Living Organisms
Class: SSS1 (Nerdc Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Transport system moves substances (oxygen, nutrients, waste) throughout the body.
  • In mammals, the circulatory system includes the heart, blood vessels, and blood.
  • Arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart (except pulmonary artery).
  • Veins carry deoxygenated blood back to the heart (except pulmonary vein).
  • Capillaries are tiny vessels where exchange of gases and nutrients occurs.
  • Plants have a vascular system (xylem and phloem) for water and food transport.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Malaria and Blood Transport: The Plasmodium parasite (causing malaria) is transported in the blood of infected humans. Mosquitoes pick up the parasite from the blood and spread it to others.
  2. Cassava Plant Transport: Cassava (Manihot esculenta) stores starch in its roots. The phloem transports sugars from leaves to the roots for storage, while xylem carries water from the soil to the leaves.

🌟 Fun Fact: The human heart beats about 100,000 times per day! In Nigeria, the traditional "agbada" fabric pattern sometimes includes spiral designs that resemble the double circulation of blood.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What is the main difference between arteries and veins in terms of blood flow direction?
  2. Question 2: Name the two types of transport tissues in plants and their functions.
  3. Question 3: In the Nigerian context, how does the transport system of the body help in the spread of malaria?

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Arteries carry blood away from the heart; veins carry blood back to the heart.
  2. Answer 2: Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves; phloem transports food (sugars) from leaves to other parts of the plant.
  3. Answer 3: The Plasmodium parasite is carried in the blood (transport system). When a mosquito bites an infected person, it picks up the parasite from the blood and can transmit it to another person.
