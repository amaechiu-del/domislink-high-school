🔬 Key Concepts of Atomic Structure

  • Atom – the smallest unit of an element that retains its chemical properties.
  • Nucleus – dense central core containing protons (positive) and neutrons (neutral).
  • Proton – positively charged particle; number = atomic number (Z).
  • Neutron – neutral particle; mass ≈ proton.
  • Electron – negatively charged particle orbiting the nucleus in shells/energy levels.
  • Atomic number (Z) = number of protons → identifies the element.
  • Mass number (A) = protons + neutrons.
  • Isotopes – same number of protons, different number of neutrons (e.g., Carbon-12, Carbon-14).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Tin (Sn) – Nigeria is a significant tin producer (Plateau State). Tin has atomic number 50, meaning 50 protons and 50 electrons. Its most common isotope has 70 neutrons (mass number 120).

2. Crude oil (Hydrogen & Carbon) – Nigeria’s oil wealth is based on hydrocarbons. Hydrogen (Z=1) has one proton; Carbon (Z=6) has six protons. Understanding isotopes like Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 helps date ancient organic materials in the Niger Delta.

🎉 Fun Fact

If you could line up 100 million atoms side by side, they would stretch only about 1 centimetre! The nucleus is 100,000 times smaller than the whole atom – like a pea in the middle of a football stadium.

✏️ Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: An atom has 11 protons and 12 neutrons. What is its atomic number and mass number? Name the element.
  2. Question 2: How many electrons does a neutral atom of oxygen (atomic number 8) have? Explain why.
  3. Question 3: Two atoms have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons. What are these atoms called? Give one example.

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: Atomic number = 11 (number of protons). Mass number = 11 + 12 = 23. The element is Sodium (Na).
  2. Answer 2: A neutral oxygen atom has 8 electrons because the number of electrons equals the number of protons (atomic number = 8) to balance charge.
  3. Answer 3: They are called isotopes. Example: Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 (both have 6 protons, but 6 and 8 neutrons respectively).
