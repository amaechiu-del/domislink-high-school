Introduction to Chemistry

SSS1 Chemistry – NERDC Syllabus

What is Chemistry?

  • Chemistry is the branch of science that studies the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter.
  • It explains how substances interact, combine, and transform to form new materials.
  • Chemistry is central to understanding everyday materials like food, medicine, soap, and fuel.
  • It is divided into branches: organic, inorganic, physical, analytical, and biochemistry.
  • Studying chemistry helps develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and laboratory skills.

Importance of Chemistry

  • Helps in the production of medicines (e.g., malaria drugs like Artemether).
  • Used in agriculture to make fertilizers and pesticides.
  • Essential in manufacturing: plastics, paints, detergents, and batteries.
  • Explains natural processes like rusting, cooking, and digestion.
  • Supports other sciences like biology, physics, and environmental science.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The production of palm oil involves chemical processes such as boiling, fermentation, and separation of oil from water. Chemistry explains why palm oil solidifies at room temperature (saturated fats).

Example 2: Local black soap (Ose Dudu) is made by mixing plantain peel ash with coconut oil. This is a chemical reaction called saponification — a key concept in chemistry.

🧪 Fun Fact: Did you know? The human body contains about 60% water (H₂O). Chemistry helps us understand how water dissolves nutrients and carries them to cells — essential for life!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main focus of chemistry?
    Answer: The study of matter, its composition, properties, and changes.
  2. Name one branch of chemistry and give an example of what it studies.
    Answer: Organic chemistry – studies carbon-containing compounds (e.g., fuels, plastics).
  3. Explain how chemistry is used in making a common Nigerian product.
    Answer: In making local black soap, ash reacts with oil (saponification) to produce soap.
