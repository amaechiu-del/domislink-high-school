Periodic Table: Structure & Patterns

Key Concepts

  • Periods are horizontal rows (1–7). Elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells.
  • Groups are vertical columns (1–18). Elements in the same group have the same number of valence electrons and similar chemical properties.
  • Alkali metals (Group 1) are highly reactive, soft metals (e.g., Lithium, Sodium).
  • Noble gases (Group 18) are unreactive because they have full outer electron shells (e.g., Helium, Neon).
  • The table is arranged by increasing atomic number (number of protons).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Tin (Sn) in Nigeria: Tin is found in Plateau State (e.g., Jos). It is a metal in Group 14, Period 5. Tin is used in soldering and food packaging.

2. Limestone (Calcium, Ca): Calcium is a Group 2 alkaline earth metal. Limestone deposits in Ogun State and other parts of Nigeria are used to produce cement for building.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The element Potassium (K) was discovered by Sir Humphry Davy in 1807. In Nigeria, potassium is found in many local foods like plantains and yams – it's essential for your nerves and muscles!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: How many periods and groups are in the modern periodic table?
    Answer: 7 periods and 18 groups.
  2. Question 2: Which group contains the most reactive metals? Give one example found in Nigeria.
    Answer: Group 1 (Alkali metals). Example: Sodium (Na) – found in table salt (common in Nigerian kitchens).
  3. Question 3: Why are noble gases like Helium and Neon unreactive?
    Answer: They have a full outer electron shell (8 electrons, except Helium which has 2), so they do not easily gain, lose, or share electrons.
