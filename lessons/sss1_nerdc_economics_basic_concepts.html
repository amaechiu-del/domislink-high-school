Basic Concepts in Economics

Economics is the study of how people make choices to satisfy their unlimited wants with limited resources. For SSS1 students in Nigeria, understanding these core ideas helps you see the world—from the market in your neighbourhood to national budgets—more clearly.

Key Concepts Explained

  • Scarcity – The basic economic problem: human wants are unlimited, but resources (land, labour, capital) are limited.
  • Choice – Because of scarcity, we must decide how to use resources. Every choice involves trade-offs.
  • Opportunity Cost – The cost of the next best alternative that is given up when a choice is made. It is not always money—it can be time or satisfaction.
  • Scale of Preference – A list of wants arranged in order of importance. It helps individuals and governments make rational choices.
  • Production – The process of combining resources to create goods and services that satisfy wants.
  • Consumption – The use of goods and services to satisfy human wants.

Two Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A Farmer in Kaduna
A farmer has a plot of land that can either grow maize or groundnuts. He chooses to plant maize. The opportunity cost is the groundnuts he could have harvested. This shows scarcity (limited land) and choice.

Example 2: A Student in Lagos
A student has ₦500 and can either buy a textbook or a movie ticket. She buys the textbook. The opportunity cost is the enjoyment from the movie. This illustrates opportunity cost and scale of preference.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "Economics" comes from the Greek oikonomia, meaning "household management." In Nigeria, the National Bureau of Statistics uses economic concepts like scarcity and opportunity cost to decide how to allocate the national budget among education, health, and infrastructure!

Practice Questions

  1. Define opportunity cost and give one example from your daily life.
  2. Why is scarcity considered the basic economic problem?
  3. If a government spends money building a new airport instead of a hospital, what is the opportunity cost? Explain.

Answers

Answer 1: Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative given up when a choice is made. Example: If you spend your Saturday studying instead of going to a party, the opportunity cost is the fun and social time you missed.

Answer 2: Scarcity is the basic problem because human wants are unlimited, but resources (like time, money, and materials) are limited. This forces individuals and societies to make choices about how to use resources efficiently.

Answer 3: The opportunity cost of building a new airport is the hospital that could have been built with the same money. The government gave up better healthcare services for the citizens in order to improve air transport.
