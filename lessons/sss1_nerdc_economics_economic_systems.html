Economic Systems

Key Features of Economic Systems

  • Traditional Economy: Based on customs, rituals, and barter. Little technology or specialization.
  • Command Economy: Government makes all decisions about production and distribution.
  • Market Economy: Individuals and businesses decide based on supply, demand, and price signals.
  • Mixed Economy: Combines private enterprise with government regulation (most common today).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Traditional: The Igbo Osu system and Hausa farming communities where land is shared based on lineage and customs.

2. Mixed: Nigeria's oil and gas sector – private companies (e.g., Shell, Chevron) operate alongside NNPC (government-owned).

⚡ Fun Fact

Did you know? Nigeria is the largest economy in Africa, but it operates as a mixed economy – the government controls oil (about 10% of GDP), while agriculture and services are mostly market-driven!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What is the main difference between a command economy and a market economy?
  2. Give one example of a traditional economic practice still found in rural Nigeria.
  3. Why is Nigeria considered a mixed economy?

✅ Answers

  1. In a command economy, the government controls production and prices; in a market economy, private individuals and businesses decide based on supply and demand.
  2. Barter trade in village markets (e.g., exchanging yams for fish).
  3. Because it has both private sector industries (e.g., telecoms, banking) and government-controlled sectors (e.g., oil, electricity).
