🌍 Factors of Production (SSS1 Economics – NERDC)

In Economics, the factors of production are the resources used to produce goods and services. They are the building blocks of every economy — from a small farm in Kano to a tech hub in Lagos.

📌 Bullet-Point Explanations

  • Land – all natural resources (soil, water, minerals, forests). Reward: Rent.
  • Labour – human effort (physical & mental) used in production. Reward: Wages/Salary.
  • Capital – man-made goods used to produce other goods (machines, tools, factories). Reward: Interest.
  • Entrepreneurship – the ability to combine land, labour, and capital to create goods/services and take risks. Reward: Profit.

🇳🇬 Two Nigerian Examples

✅ Example 1 – Cassava Farming in Oyo State:
A farmer uses land (fertile soil), labour (farm workers), capital (tractors, cutlasses, processing machines), and entrepreneurship (the farmer who organises and sells garri/floor) to produce cassava products.
✅ Example 2 – Nollywood Movie Production:
A producer uses land (location/scenery), labour (actors, camera crew, directors), capital (cameras, lighting, editing software), and entrepreneurship (the producer who funds and markets the film) to create a movie.
Did you know? Nigeria’s first television station (WNTV, 1959) used capital equipment imported from the UK, local labour (Nigerian presenters and engineers), and entrepreneurial vision from Chief Obafemi Awolowo — all four factors in action!

📝 Practice Questions (with Answers)

Q1: Which factor of production is rewarded with interest?
Answer: Capital (e.g., money used to buy machinery earns interest for the lender).
Q2: In a Lagos tailoring business, the sewing machine is an example of which factor?
Answer: Capital (it is a man-made tool used to produce clothes).
Q3: Give one reason why the entrepreneur is sometimes called the “risk-bearer”.
Answer: The entrepreneur invests their own money and time, and if the business fails, they bear the financial loss — unlike workers who still earn wages.
