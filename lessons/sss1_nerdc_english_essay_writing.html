Essay Writing: The Art of Structured Expression

Key Points for Essay Writing

  • Structure: Every essay needs an introduction, body paragraphs, and a conclusion.
  • Introduction: Start with a hook (interesting fact, question, or quote) and state your thesis (main argument).
  • Body Paragraphs: Each paragraph should have one main point, supported by evidence or examples.
  • Conclusion: Summarise your main points and restate your thesis in a new way.
  • Coherence: Use linking words (e.g., "furthermore," "however," "in addition") to connect ideas.
  • Clarity: Write simple, clear sentences. Avoid slang or overly complex words.
  • Proofread: Always check for grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors before submitting.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Topic – "The Importance of Education in Nigeria"
Introduction: "Education is the key to unlocking Nigeria's potential. From the streets of Lagos to the villages of Enugu, every child deserves access to quality learning. This essay argues that education empowers individuals and drives national development."
Body Paragraph 1: "Firstly, education reduces poverty. For instance, a student who learns vocational skills in Kano can start a small business, lifting their family out of hardship."
Conclusion: "In conclusion, education is not just a privilege but a necessity for Nigeria's growth. By investing in schools, we invest in our future."

Example 2: Topic – "The Role of Youth in Nation Building"
Introduction: "Nigeria's youth are its greatest asset. With over 60% of the population under 25, young people have the power to shape the country's destiny. This essay explores how youth can contribute through innovation and civic engagement."
Body Paragraph 2: "Additionally, youth can drive change through technology. A young coder in Abuja can develop apps that solve local problems, such as traffic management or healthcare access."
Conclusion: "To sum up, when youth are given opportunities, Nigeria thrives. It is time to harness this energy for a brighter tomorrow."

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that the longest essay ever written in English was over 100,000 words? It was about the history of the English language! But don't worry—your SSS1 essays only need to be about 350–500 words.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Write an introduction for an essay titled "The Benefits of Reading." Include a hook and a thesis statement.
  2. Question 2: List two linking words you could use to start a body paragraph in an essay about "Environmental Conservation."
  3. Question 3: What is the purpose of a conclusion in an essay? Give one example of a concluding sentence.

Answers

Answer 1: "Have you ever wondered how a single book can change your life? Reading opens doors to new worlds and ideas. This essay will argue that reading improves knowledge, empathy, and creativity."

Answer 2: "Firstly" and "Furthermore" (or "In addition," "Moreover").

Answer 3: A conclusion summarises the main points and restates the thesis. Example: "In conclusion, protecting our forests is essential for a sustainable future."
