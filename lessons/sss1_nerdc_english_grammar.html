🔍 Key Points to Remember

  • Subject — the person or thing doing the action (e.g., Chidi, the rain)
  • Verb — the action or state of being (e.g., runs, is, cooked)
  • Object — the person or thing that receives the action (e.g., the ball, rice)
  • In English, the normal order is Subject → Verb → Object (SVO).
  • A sentence can exist without an object (e.g., She sleeps.) but must have a subject and verb.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • ✅ Amaka bought fresh vegetables at the market.
  • ✅ The students completed their assignments before break.
🎉 Fun Fact! In Nigerian Pidgin English, the SVO order stays the same: "Di man chop yam" (The man ate yam). Even when languages mix, the grammar backbone remains strong!

✏️ Practice Questions

  1. Identify the subject, verb, and object in: "The taxi driver stopped the car suddenly."
  2. Rewrite this sentence in correct SVO order: "the oranges the girl peeled"
  3. Which part is missing in: "Bakes delicious bread." — subject, verb, or object?

✅ Answers

  1. Subject = The taxi driver, Verb = stopped, Object = the car
  2. "The girl peeled the oranges."
  3. Subject is missing (e.g., "Mama bakes delicious bread.")
