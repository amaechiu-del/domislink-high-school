Citizenship in Government (SSS1 - NERDC Syllabus)

Definition: Citizenship is the legal status of a person as a member of a particular country, conferring certain rights, duties, and privileges.

Key Concepts

  • Rights of Citizens: Right to vote, freedom of speech, right to life, and access to public services.
  • Duties of Citizens: Obeying the law, paying taxes, defending the country, and participating in community development.
  • Ways of Acquiring Citizenship:
    • Birth (Jus Soli – by being born in the country, or Jus Sanguinis – by blood/parentage)
    • Registration (for those with Nigerian ancestry, e.g., children of Nigerians born abroad)
    • Naturalization (for foreigners who meet residency and character requirements)
  • Loss of Citizenship: Voluntary renunciation, deprivation by government (e.g., for treason), or acquiring citizenship of another country (in some cases).

Nigerian Examples

  1. Example 1 – Citizenship by Birth: A child born in Lagos to Nigerian parents automatically becomes a Nigerian citizen by birth (Section 25 of the 1999 Constitution).
  2. Example 2 – Citizenship by Naturalization: A British businessman who has lived in Abuja for 15 years, speaks Hausa, and has contributed to the economy may apply for Nigerian citizenship by naturalization (Section 27 of the 1999 Constitution).
Fun Fact: Did you know? Nigeria’s 1999 Constitution allows dual citizenship for Nigerians by birth, but those who acquire citizenship by naturalization must renounce their previous nationality!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What are two rights guaranteed to Nigerian citizens under the 1999 Constitution?
  2. Question 2: Explain the difference between citizenship by birth (Jus Soli) and citizenship by registration.
  3. Question 3: List three duties that every Nigerian citizen must perform.

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Right to life (Section 33) and right to freedom of expression (Section 39).
  2. Answer 2: Citizenship by birth (Jus Soli) is acquired by being born in Nigeria, while citizenship by registration is for persons of Nigerian ancestry who are not citizens by birth (e.g., children of Nigerians born abroad).
  3. Answer 3: Obeying the laws of Nigeria, paying taxes as required, and defending the country when called upon (e.g., national service).
