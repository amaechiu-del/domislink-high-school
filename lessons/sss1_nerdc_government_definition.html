📖 Key Points to Understand

  • Government as an Institution: The set of structures (Legislature, Executive, Judiciary) that make and enforce laws.
  • Government as a Process: The continuous activities of making decisions, implementing policies, and resolving conflicts.
  • Government as a Group of People: The individuals (elected or appointed) who hold authority and run the state.
  • Purpose: To maintain order, provide public services, protect rights, and promote the welfare of citizens.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • Example 1 – Federal Government of Nigeria: The President, National Assembly (Senate & House of Representatives), and the Supreme Court together form the government that rules the country from Abuja.
  • Example 2 – Local Government in Oyo State: The Ibadan South-West Local Government Council, led by a Chairman and Councillors, provides services like waste collection and primary healthcare to the local community.
💡 Fun Fact: Nigeria operates a Presidential System of Government where the President is both the Head of State and Head of Government – similar to the United States, but uniquely adapted to Nigeria's federal structure of 36 states!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Define government as an institution in your own words.
  2. Name two arms of government found in Nigeria and state one function of each.
  3. Why is government considered a process rather than just a group of people?
📌 Answers
  1. Government as an institution refers to the permanent structures (like the legislature, executive, and judiciary) that are established to govern a state, regardless of who holds power at any time.
  2. Legislature (makes laws) and Executive (implements laws). In Nigeria, the National Assembly makes laws, while the President and ministers execute them.
  3. Government is a process because it involves ongoing activities such as debating, lawmaking, budgeting, and policy implementation – it is not static but a continuous cycle of decision-making and action.
