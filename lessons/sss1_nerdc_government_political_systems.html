Political Systems

Definition: A political system is the set of formal legal institutions that constitute a government. It includes the structures, processes, and activities by which a society makes and enforces its collective decisions.

Key Features of Political Systems

  • Democracy: Power is vested in the people, who rule either directly or through freely elected representatives. Key features include regular elections, multiparty competition, and protection of civil liberties.
  • Monarchy: A system where a single person (king, queen, emir) rules, often by hereditary right. In modern times, many monarchies are constitutional, meaning the monarch's powers are limited by law.
  • Dictatorship: A system where a single person or a small group holds absolute power, with no effective constitutional limits. Opposition is suppressed, and the military often supports the regime.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Nigeria's Democratic System
Since 1999, Nigeria has operated a federal presidential democratic republic. Citizens vote for the President, Governors, and legislators. The 2023 general elections saw over 87 million registered voters, demonstrating the practice of democracy.

Example 2: Traditional Monarchies in Nigeria
Nigeria has numerous traditional rulers (e.g., the Ooni of Ife, the Sultan of Sokoto, the Obi of Onitsha). While they hold cultural and ceremonial authority, their political power is limited by the Constitution, showing a constitutional monarchy-like arrangement within a democratic framework.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that the ancient Oyo Empire (1400s–1835) had a sophisticated political system with a checks-and-balances mechanism? The Oyo Mesi (council of chiefs) could advise the Alaafin (king) and even ask him to abdicate—similar to modern impeachment!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main difference between a democracy and a dictatorship?
  2. Name two features of a constitutional monarchy.
  3. Give one example of a traditional monarchy in Nigeria and explain its role in the modern political system.

Answers

  1. In a democracy, power belongs to the people and is exercised through free elections, while in a dictatorship, a single ruler or group holds absolute power without free elections or opposition.
  2. Features of constitutional monarchy: (i) The monarch's powers are limited by a constitution, (ii) the monarch serves as a ceremonial head of state while elected officials govern.
  3. Example: The Sultan of Sokoto. He serves as the spiritual leader of Nigerian Muslims and advises the government on cultural matters, but has no constitutional power to make laws or govern.
