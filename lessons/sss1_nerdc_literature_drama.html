What is Drama?

Drama is a form of literature written to be performed by actors on a stage before an audience. It tells a story through dialogue (spoken words), action (movement and gestures), and conflict (struggle between characters or ideas). Unlike novels or poems, drama comes alive only when it is acted out.

Key Elements of Drama

  • Plot – The sequence of events that make up the story (beginning, middle, end).
  • Characters – The people (or beings) who drive the story forward through their choices.
  • Dialogue – Conversations between characters that reveal their thoughts and move the plot.
  • Conflict – The central struggle (man vs man, man vs society, man vs self, man vs nature).
  • Setting – Where and when the story takes place (e.g., a village, a palace, modern city).
  • Theme – The big idea or message the playwright wants to share (e.g., love, betrayal, justice).
  • Stage Directions – Instructions in the script about lighting, movement, and tone (e.g., “enters left, weeping”).

Nigerian Examples of Drama

1. The Gods Are Not To Blame by Ola Rotimi
A classic Nigerian tragedy that adapts the Greek story of Oedipus into a Yoruba setting. It explores themes of fate, pride, and family loyalty. The play uses traditional Yoruba songs, proverbs, and drumming to bring the story to life.

2. The Trials of Brother Jero by Wole Soyinka
A comedy that satirizes religious hypocrisy in modern Nigeria. The play follows Brother Jero, a self-proclaimed prophet, as he tricks and manipulates his followers on a Lagos beach. It uses humour and irony to criticize corruption in the church.

🎭 Fun Fact

The longest-running play in Nigerian history is “King Emene” by Tunde Adegbola. It was performed for over 500 consecutive nights in Ibadan in the 1980s! Drama in Nigeria often blends English with local languages like Yoruba, Igbo, and Hausa to reach wider audiences.

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main difference between drama and a novel?
  2. In The Gods Are Not To Blame, what is the central conflict that drives the plot?
  3. Give two examples of stage directions a playwright might include in a script.

Answers

Answer 1: Drama is written to be performed on stage with actors, dialogue, and action, while a novel is meant to be read silently. Drama relies heavily on performance, whereas a novel uses description and narration.

Answer 2: The central conflict is the struggle between fate (the prophecy that Odewale will kill his father and marry his mother) and his attempts to avoid it. It is a man vs. fate conflict, showing that no one can escape destiny.

Answer 3: Examples include: “She exits stage left, sobbing” (movement/emotion) and “Lights dim to a single spotlight on the king” (lighting/atmosphere). Stage directions tell actors and directors how to perform the scene.
