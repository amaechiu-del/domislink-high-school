Understanding Prescribed Texts in Literature (Nerdc SSS1)

Key Concepts

  • Prescribed Texts are literary works officially selected by the NERDC curriculum for study in SSS1 Literature.
  • They are categorized into Prose (novels), Drama (plays), and Poetry.
  • Each text is analyzed for plot, character, setting, theme, and literary devices.
  • Students are expected to understand the historical and cultural context of each work.
  • Critical thinking is developed through comparing and contrasting different texts.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Lion and the Jewel by Wole Soyinka (Drama) – A play exploring tradition vs. modernity in a Yoruba village. Key themes: pride, love, and cultural conflict.

Example 2: The Joys of Motherhood by Buchi Emecheta (Prose) – A novel about a Nigerian woman’s struggles in a patriarchal society. Key themes: motherhood, sacrifice, and colonialism.

🎉 Fun Fact: Did you know that the first African Nobel Prize winner for Literature (1986) was Nigerian playwright Wole Soyinka? His play The Lion and the Jewel is one of the most studied prescribed texts in Nigerian secondary schools!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Name the three main genres of prescribed texts in the NERDC Literature curriculum.
    Answer: Prose, Drama, and Poetry.
  2. Question 2: In The Lion and the Jewel, what does the character Baroka represent?
    Answer: Baroka represents tradition, wisdom, and the old ways of the Yoruba village.
  3. Question 3: List two literary devices commonly analyzed in prescribed poetry texts.
    Answer: Rhyme and Imagery (or any two: meter, simile, metaphor, alliteration).
