Coordinate Geometry (SSS1 Mathematics - NERDC)

  • Coordinate plane is formed by two perpendicular number lines: the x-axis (horizontal) and the y-axis (vertical).
  • Point O (0,0) is called the origin.
  • Every point is written as (x, y) — first the x-coordinate (horizontal position), then the y-coordinate (vertical position).
  • Positive x is to the right, negative x to the left; positive y is upward, negative y downward.
  • The four regions are called quadrants (Q1: top-right, Q2: top-left, Q3: bottom-left, Q4: bottom-right).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A student plots the location of Abuja (FCT) on a map grid. If Abuja is 4 units east and 6 units north of the origin, what are its coordinates?
Answer: (4, 6). The x-coordinate is east (positive), y-coordinate is north (positive).

Example 2: A trader in Lagos marks his shop at (5, -3) on a city grid. Which quadrant is his shop in?
Answer: Quadrant IV (positive x, negative y).

🎉 Fun Fact: The coordinate system we use today was invented by French mathematician René Descartes in the 17th century. Legend says he came up with the idea while watching a fly crawl across his ceiling tiles!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What are the coordinates of the origin?
  2. In which quadrant does the point (-2, 5) lie?
  3. If a point has x = 0 and y = -4, where is it located on the coordinate plane?
Answers:
1. (0, 0)
2. Quadrant II (top-left)
3. On the negative y-axis (directly below the origin, 4 units down)
