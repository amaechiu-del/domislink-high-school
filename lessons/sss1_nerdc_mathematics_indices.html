Indices (Laws of Indices)

Indices (or exponents) are a shorthand way of writing repeated multiplication. For example, \(2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 8\). Understanding the laws of indices helps simplify expressions and solve equations.

Key Laws of Indices

  • Multiplication Law: \(a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}\)
    Example: \(2^3 \times 2^4 = 2^{3+4} = 2^7\)
  • Division Law: \(a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}\) (for \(a \neq 0\))
    Example: \(5^6 \div 5^2 = 5^{6-2} = 5^4\)
  • Power of a Power: \((a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}\)
    Example: \((3^2)^3 = 3^{2 \times 3} = 3^6\)
  • Zero Index: \(a^0 = 1\) (for \(a \neq 0\))
    Example: \(7^0 = 1\)
  • Negative Index: \(a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}\)
    Example: \(4^{-2} = \frac{1}{4^2} = \frac{1}{16}\)
  • Fractional Index: \(a^{\frac{1}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a}\)
    Example: \(8^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{8} = 2\)

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Naira and Kobo
If the number of naira in a bank account is \(2^5\) and the number of kobo is \(2^3\), find the total number of kobo (since 1 naira = 100 kobo).
Solution: Total kobo = \(2^5 \times 100 + 2^3\). But \(100 = 10^2 = (2 \times 5)^2 = 2^2 \times 5^2\). So total = \(2^5 \times 2^2 \times 5^2 + 2^3 = 2^{7} \times 25 + 8 = 128 \times 25 + 8 = 3200 + 8 = 3208\) kobo.
Using indices: \(2^5 \times 2^2 = 2^7 = 128\). Then multiply by 25.

Example 2: Population of Lagos
The population of a Lagos local government area is \(3^6\) people. If the population grows by a factor of \(3^2\) over 5 years, what is the new population?
Solution: New population = \(3^6 \times 3^2 = 3^{6+2} = 3^8 = 6561\). So the new population is 6561 people.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "index" comes from the Latin word meaning "pointer" or "indicator". In mathematics, indices were first used systematically by the French mathematician René Descartes in the 17th century. The ancient Egyptians also used a form of indices when dealing with geometric progressions!

Practice Questions

  1. Simplify: \(2^3 \times 2^5\) and write your answer as a single power of 2.
  2. Evaluate: \( \frac{5^7}{5^4} \)
  3. Find the value of: \( (4^2)^3 \)

Answers

  1. \(2^3 \times 2^5 = 2^{3+5} = 2^8\) (which equals 256)
  2. \(\frac{5^7}{5^4} = 5^{7-4} = 5^3 = 125\)
  3. \((4^2)^3 = 4^{2 \times 3} = 4^6 = 4096\)
