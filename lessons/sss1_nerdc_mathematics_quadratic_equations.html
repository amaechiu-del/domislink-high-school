Understanding Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation is any equation that can be written in the standard form:

ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0

  • Shape: The graph of a quadratic equation is a parabola — a U-shaped curve that opens upward (if a > 0) or downward (if a < 0).
  • Vertex: The highest or lowest point on the parabola is called the vertex.
  • Roots: The solutions to the equation (where the curve crosses the x-axis) are called roots or zeros.
  • Discriminant: The value b² - 4ac tells us how many roots exist:
    • If b² - 4ac > 0 → two distinct real roots
    • If b² - 4ac = 0 → one repeated real root
    • If b² - 4ac < 0 → no real roots

Nigerian Examples

🇳🇬 Example 1: Finding the cost of a bag of rice

The cost (in Naira) of producing x bags of rice is given by the quadratic equation: x² - 60x + 800 = 0. Find the number of bags produced.

Solution using factoring:

x² - 60x + 800 = 0
(x - 20)(x - 40) = 0
x = 20 or x = 40

✅ Answer: The company produced either 20 bags or 40 bags of rice.

🇳🇬 Example 2: A rectangular plot in Lagos

A rectangular plot of land has length 5 meters more than its width. Its area is 150 m². Find the dimensions of the plot.

Solution: Let width = w, then length = w + 5
Area = w(w + 5) = 150
w² + 5w - 150 = 0
(w + 15)(w - 10) = 0
w = -15 (reject) or w = 10

✅ Answer: Width = 10 m, Length = 15 m

💡 Fun Fact

Did you know? The word "quadratic" comes from the Latin word "quadratus", meaning "square". Ancient Nigerian mathematicians in the Mali Empire used geometric methods to solve quadratic problems related to land measurement and trade routes centuries before the modern formula was developed!

Practice Questions

  1. Solve for x: x² - 7x + 12 = 0
  2. A farmer in Kaduna has a rectangular field whose length is 8 meters more than its width. The area is 240 m². Find the width of the field.
  3. Use the quadratic formula to solve: 2x² + 5x - 3 = 0

📝 Answers

  1. x = 3 or x = 4
  2. Width = 12 m (Length = 20 m)
  3. x = ½ or x = -3
