Statistics: Introduction to Data Handling

Key Concepts

  • Data – Information collected for analysis (e.g., ages, test scores).
  • Frequency – How often a value occurs in a dataset.
  • Bar Chart – A diagram using bars to show frequency of categories.
  • Mean – Average of all values (sum ÷ number of values).
  • Median – Middle value when data is arranged in order.
  • Mode – Most frequently occurring value.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A shop in Lagos sells bags of rice: 5 bags on Monday, 8 on Tuesday, 12 on Wednesday, 6 on Thursday, 9 on Friday. What is the mean number of bags sold per day?

Solution: Mean = (5+8+12+6+9) ÷ 5 = 40 ÷ 5 = 8 bags.

Example 2: The ages of 7 students in a Kano secondary school are: 12, 14, 13, 15, 12, 16, 13. Find the mode.

Solution: Arrange: 12,12,13,13,14,15,16. The numbers 12 and 13 both appear twice. So the data has two modes: 12 and 13 (bimodal).

🎉 Fun Fact: The Nigerian National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) uses the same methods you are learning to track the country's population, inflation, and school enrollment rates!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: The heights (cm) of 5 students are: 150, 155, 160, 155, 170. Calculate the mean height.
    Answer: 158 cm
  2. Question 2: Scores from a class test in Abuja: 40, 55, 60, 55, 70, 80, 55, 90. What is the mode?
    Answer: 55
  3. Question 3: The number of yams sold by a farmer in 7 days: 20, 25, 30, 25, 35, 40, 25. Find the median.
    Answer: 25 (arranged: 20,25,25,25,30,35,40 → middle value is 25)
