Trigonometry: Introduction to Right-Angled Triangles

Key Concepts

  • Hypotenuse: The longest side, opposite the right angle.
  • Opposite: The side opposite the angle of interest (θ).
  • Adjacent: The side next to the angle of interest (θ), but not the hypotenuse.
  • Sine (sin) θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos) θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan) θ = Opposite / Adjacent

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A ladder leans against a wall in Lagos. The ladder (hypotenuse) is 5 m long, and it reaches 4 m up the wall (opposite side). Find the angle the ladder makes with the ground (θ).

Solution: sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 4/5 = 0.8. Using inverse sine: θ = sin⁻¹(0.8) ≈ 53.13°.

Example 2: From the top of a 10 m tall building in Abuja, the distance to a tree on the ground (adjacent side) is 24 m. Find the angle of depression from the top of the building to the tree.

Solution: tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent = 10/24 = 0.4167. Using inverse tan: θ = tan⁻¹(0.4167) ≈ 22.62°.

🌟 Fun Fact: The word "trigonometry" comes from Greek words "trigonon" (triangle) and "metron" (measure). Ancient Nigerian astronomers used similar triangle methods to track stars and seasons!

Practice Questions

  1. In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 6 cm and the hypotenuse is 10 cm. Find sin θ.
  2. A 12 m tall flagpole in Kaduna casts a shadow 9 m long. What is the angle of elevation of the sun?
  3. If cos θ = 0.6 and the hypotenuse is 15 cm, find the length of the adjacent side.

Answers

  • 1. sin θ = 6/10 = 0.6
  • 2. tan θ = 12/9 = 1.3333, so θ = tan⁻¹(1.3333) ≈ 53.13°
  • 3. Adjacent = cos θ × Hypotenuse = 0.6 × 15 = 9 cm
