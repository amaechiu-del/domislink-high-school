Key Concepts

  • Work (W) – Done when a force moves an object over a distance. W = F × d (unit: Joules, J)
  • Energy (E) – The ability to do work. Two main forms: Kinetic (movement) and Potential (stored).
  • Power (P) – The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. P = W / t (unit: Watts, W)
  • Law of Conservation of Energy – Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.
  • Efficiency – (Useful energy output / Total energy input) × 100%

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A student in Lagos pushes a 40 kg desk across a classroom floor with a force of 120 N over a distance of 5 m. How much work is done?
Solution: W = F × d = 120 N × 5 m = 600 J

Example 2: A generator in Abuja supplies 5000 W of power for 2 hours to run fans during a NEPA outage. How much energy is supplied?
Solution: Energy = Power × Time = 5000 W × (2 × 3600 s) = 5000 × 7200 = 36,000,000 J (36 MJ)

⚡ Fun Fact

Did you know? One serving of eba and egusi soup provides about 1,200 kJ of energy! That's enough energy to lift a 50 kg bag of rice over 2,400 meters into the air — or power a 60W light bulb for over 5 hours!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A boy uses a force of 80 N to push a wheelbarrow carrying sand through a distance of 12 m. Calculate the work done.
  2. Question 2: A 2 kg book falls from a shelf 1.5 m high. What is its potential energy before falling? (Take g = 10 m/s²)
  3. Question 3: A machine does 3600 J of work in 30 seconds. What is its power output?

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: W = 80 N × 12 m = 960 J
  2. Answer 2: PE = mgh = 2 kg × 10 m/s² × 1.5 m = 30 J
  3. Answer 3: P = W / t = 3600 J / 30 s = 120 W
