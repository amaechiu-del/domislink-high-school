Cell: The Basic Unit of Life

SSS1 Biology — WAEC Syllabus

Key Points about the Cell

  • Cell Membrane: A thin, semi-permeable layer that controls what enters and leaves the cell.
  • Nucleus: The "brain" of the cell — contains genetic material (DNA) and controls all cell activities.
  • Mitochondrion: The "powerhouse" — releases energy through respiration.
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Rough ER (with ribosomes) makes proteins; Smooth ER makes lipids.
  • Golgi Apparatus: Packages and transports proteins out of the cell.
  • Vacuole: Stores water, nutrients, and waste (in plant cells, it is large).
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance where chemical reactions occur.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Yam tuber cells: When you slice a yam, the starch grains you see are stored in the vacuoles of plant cells. This is why yam is a rich source of carbohydrates for many Nigerian meals like pounded yam.

2. Malaria parasite (Plasmodium): This single-celled organism invades the red blood cells of humans. In Nigeria, understanding its cell structure helps scientists develop drugs like artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs) to fight malaria.

🧠 Fun Fact!

The longest cell in the human body is the neuron (nerve cell). Some neurons in your leg can be up to 1 metre long! That's longer than a standard school ruler in Nigeria!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which organelle is known as the "powerhouse of the cell" and why?
    Answer: The mitochondrion. It releases energy (ATP) during cellular respiration.
  2. Question 2: Name one difference between a plant cell and an animal cell.
    Answer: Plant cells have a cell wall and chloroplasts, while animal cells do not. (Other valid: plant cells have a large central vacuole, animal cells have small vacuoles.)
  3. Question 3: What is the function of the cell membrane?
    Answer: It controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell (selectively permeable).
