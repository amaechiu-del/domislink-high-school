Evolution: The Unfolding Story of Life

Biology | SSS1 | WAEC Syllabus

Key Points

  • Evolution is the gradual change in living organisms over generations.
  • Natural selection is a main mechanism: organisms with traits better suited to their environment survive and reproduce more.
  • Variation exists within every population (e.g., different colours, sizes).
  • Over time, favourable traits become more common in the population.
  • Evidence for evolution includes fossils, comparative anatomy, and DNA similarities.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  • Cassava Varieties: Nigerian farmers have selected and bred cassava plants that are resistant to the cassava mosaic virus, leading to evolved, hardier varieties like TME 419.
  • Mango Trees in the Sahel: Mango trees in northern Nigeria have evolved deeper root systems and thicker bark to survive longer dry seasons compared to their southern relatives.

🎉 Fun Fact

The West African manatee (found in Nigerian rivers) shares a common ancestor with elephants! Their closest living relative is actually the elephant, not other marine mammals.

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1: What is the term for the process where organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring?

Answer: Natural selection.

Question 2: Name one piece of evidence that supports the theory of evolution.

Answer: Fossils (or comparative anatomy, DNA similarities, etc.).

Question 3: Why might a population of grasshoppers in a grassland area become mostly green over many generations?

Answer: Green grasshoppers are better camouflaged from predators, so they survive longer and reproduce more, passing on the green colour gene.
