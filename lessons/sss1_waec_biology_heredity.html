Heredity in Biology (SSS1 - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Heredity is the passing of traits (e.g., height, skin color) from parents to offspring through genes.
  • Genes are segments of DNA located on chromosomes; each parent contributes one allele per trait.
  • Dominant alleles (e.g., T for tallness) mask recessive alleles (e.g., t for shortness) in heterozygous individuals.
  • Genotype (e.g., TT, Tt, tt) determines phenotype (observable characteristics).
  • Mendel’s Law of Segregation states that alleles separate during gamete formation.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In Nigerian yam farming, some varieties are resistant to pests (dominant trait) while others are susceptible. Cross-breeding a resistant (RR) yam with a susceptible (rr) yam yields all resistant (Rr) offspring.

Example 2: Among the Fulani cattle, the gene for long horns (L) is dominant over short horns (l). A bull with Ll genotype mated with a cow with ll genotype will produce both long- and short-horned calves in a 1:1 ratio.

🎉 Fun Fact: The Nigerian dwarf goat can inherit a gene for "fainting" (myotonia congenita)! When startled, their muscles stiffen and they fall over—but they are not hurt. This is a recessive trait that can be passed down through heredity.

Practice Questions

  1. In pea plants, tall stem (T) is dominant over short stem (t). If a heterozygous tall plant (Tt) is crossed with a short plant (tt), what percentage of the offspring will be short?
  2. Define the term "genotype" and give an example using a trait of your choice.
  3. In Nigerian maize farming, purple kernels (P) are dominant over yellow kernels (p). If a homozygous purple plant (PP) is crossed with a yellow plant (pp), what will be the phenotype of the F1 generation?

Answers

Answer 1: 50% of the offspring will be short (tt). The cross Tt × tt gives 50% Tt (tall) and 50% tt (short).

Answer 2: Genotype refers to the genetic makeup of an organism, e.g., "Tt" for a heterozygous tall pea plant.

Answer 3: All F1 offspring will have purple kernels (Pp), since purple is dominant.
