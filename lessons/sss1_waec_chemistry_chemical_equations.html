Chemical Equations

Key Points

  • Chemical equation shows reactants (starting materials) on the left and products (resulting substances) on the right, separated by an arrow (→).
  • Law of Conservation of Mass requires the same number of each type of atom on both sides — this is called balancing the equation.
  • Subscripts (small numbers after element symbols) show the number of atoms in a molecule, e.g., H₂O means 2 hydrogen + 1 oxygen.
  • Coefficients (large numbers before formulas) indicate the number of molecules needed to balance the equation.
  • State symbols can be added: (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) aqueous solution.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Rusting of Iron (Common in Nigerian rain)

Iron reacts with oxygen and water to form hydrated iron(III) oxide (rust):

4Fe(s) + 3O₂(g) + 2H₂O(l) → 2Fe₂O₃·H₂O(s)

Note: This is why iron roofs in Nigeria need regular painting to prevent rust.

Example 2: Burning of Charcoal (Common in Nigerian cooking)

Carbon in charcoal burns in oxygen to produce carbon dioxide:

C(s) + O₂(g) → CO₂(g)

Note: Incomplete combustion produces poisonous carbon monoxide (CO) — always cook in well-ventilated areas!

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The ancient Egyptians used chemical reactions to make bread more than 5,000 years ago! When they mixed flour, water, and yeast, a chemical reaction produced carbon dioxide gas that made the dough rise. Today, the same reaction happens in every Nigerian bakery making Agege bread — it's all about balancing the ingredients!

Practice Questions

Q1. Balance the following chemical equation:
___H₂ + ___O₂ → ___H₂O

Show Answer

Answer: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Q2. In the reaction: Mg + HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂, how many HCl molecules are needed to balance the equation?

Show Answer

Answer: 2HCl molecules are needed. Balanced equation: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂

Q3. Write the balanced chemical equation for the thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate (limestone) used in Nigerian cement production. (Calcium carbonate → Calcium oxide + Carbon dioxide)

Show Answer

Answer: CaCO₃(s) → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)
