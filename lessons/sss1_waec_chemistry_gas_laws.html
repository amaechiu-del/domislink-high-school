Gas Laws (SSS1 Chemistry - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Boyle's Law: At constant temperature, the volume of a fixed mass of gas is inversely proportional to its pressure. P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
  • Charles' Law: At constant pressure, the volume of a fixed mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature (Kelvin). V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
  • Pressure Law (Gay-Lussac's Law): At constant volume, pressure is directly proportional to absolute temperature. P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
  • Standard Conditions: In Nigeria, we often use room temperature (25°C = 298 K) and atmospheric pressure (760 mmHg or 101.3 kPa).
  • Absolute Zero: -273°C or 0 K – the theoretical temperature at which gas volume becomes zero.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A mechanic in Lagos uses a bicycle pump to inflate a tyre. Initially, the air in the pump has a volume of 500 cm³ at 1 atm. If the pump compresses the air to 200 cm³ at the same temperature, what is the new pressure?

Solution: Using Boyle's Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ → (1 atm)(500 cm³) = P₂(200 cm³) → P₂ = 2.5 atm.

Example 2: A gas cylinder in a Kano laboratory contains 2.0 dm³ of gas at 27°C. If the cylinder is heated to 127°C at constant pressure, what will be the new volume?

Solution: Convert to Kelvin: T₁ = 27 + 273 = 300 K, T₂ = 127 + 273 = 400 K. Charles' Law: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ → 2.0/300 = V₂/400 → V₂ = 2.67 dm³.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that the tyres of cars in northern Nigeria (e.g., Kano) are often inflated to slightly different pressures than in southern Nigeria (e.g., Port Harcourt)? This is because temperature affects gas pressure! A tyre inflated in hot weather may overexpand in cooler weather, and vice versa. Always check tyre pressure when the tyres are "cold" for accurate readings!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A sample of gas occupies 600 cm³ at 27°C. What volume will it occupy at 127°C, if pressure remains constant? (Answer: 800 cm³)
  2. Question 2: The pressure of a gas in a closed container is 120 kPa at 300 K. If the temperature rises to 450 K, what will be the new pressure? (Answer: 180 kPa)
  3. Question 3: A gas has a volume of 250 cm³ at 760 mmHg. If the pressure is increased to 950 mmHg at constant temperature, calculate the new volume. (Answer: 200 cm³)
