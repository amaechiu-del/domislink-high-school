Periodic Table: Foundation of Chemistry

SSS1 Chemistry – WAEC Syllabus

Key Concepts

  • Periods: Horizontal rows (1–7) showing increasing atomic number. Elements in the same period have the same number of electron shells.
  • Groups: Vertical columns (1–18) with elements sharing similar chemical properties due to the same number of valence electrons.
  • Metals, Non-metals & Metalloids: Metals (left) are shiny and conduct electricity; non-metals (right) are brittle; metalloids (e.g., Silicon) have mixed properties.
  • Atomic number: The number of protons in the nucleus, which determines the element's position.
  • Trend in reactivity: Group 1 metals become more reactive as you go down; Group 17 halogens become less reactive.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Aluminium (Al) – Nigeria is a major producer of aluminium through smelting in Ikot Abasi, Akwa Ibom. Aluminium is in Group 13, Period 3, and is a lightweight metal used for cooking pots and building materials.

Example 2: Limestone (Calcium carbonate, CaCO₃) – Abundant in Nigeria (e.g., in Ewekoro, Ogun State). Calcium (Ca) is a Group 2 alkaline earth metal. Limestone is used in cement production and agriculture to reduce soil acidity.

Fun Fact: The only letter not found in any element symbol on the periodic table is J. Also, the element Francium (Fr) is so rare that only about 30 grams exist on Earth at any given time!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which group of elements is known as the "noble gases" and why are they unreactive?
    Answer: Group 18 (He, Ne, Ar, etc.) – they have a full outer electron shell, making them chemically stable and unreactive.
  2. Question 2: Sodium (Na) is in Group 1, Period 3. How many electron shells does it have?
    Answer: 3 shells (since it is in Period 3). Its electron configuration is 2,8,1.
  3. Question 3: Name one element from the periodic table that is commonly used in Nigerian agriculture and state its group.
    Answer: Calcium (Ca) – Group 2. It is found in limestone used to neutralize acidic soils.
