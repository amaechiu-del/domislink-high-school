Key Concepts in Stoichiometry

  • Mole: The amount of substance containing 6.02 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number).
  • Molar mass: Mass of one mole of a substance (g/mol). For example, H₂O = 18 g/mol.
  • Mole ratio: The ratio of reactants/products in a balanced chemical equation.
  • Mass-mole conversion: Moles = Mass (g) ÷ Molar mass (g/mol).
  • Limiting reagent: The reactant that is completely used up first, limiting product yield.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Local Soap Production:
A soapmaker in Kano uses sodium hydroxide (NaOH, Molar mass = 40 g/mol) to make soap from palm oil. If 80 g of NaOH is used, how many moles is that?
Solution: Moles = 80 g ÷ 40 g/mol = 2 moles
Example 2 – Limestone in Cement (Lagos factory):
Calcium carbonate (CaCO₃, Molar mass = 100 g/mol) decomposes: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂. If 500 g of CaCO₃ is heated, what mass of CaO (56 g/mol) is produced?
Solution: Moles CaCO₃ = 500 ÷ 100 = 5 moles. 1:1 ratio, so 5 moles CaO. Mass = 5 × 56 = 280 g
🎉 Fun Fact: A single mole of water (18 g) contains more molecules than there are grains of sand on all the beaches in the world! In Nigeria, the Sahara Desert sand grains are about 10²⁰, but one mole is 6.02 × 10²³ — over 6,000 times more!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: How many moles are in 36 g of water (H₂O, Molar mass = 18 g/mol)?
    Answer: 36 ÷ 18 = 2 moles
  2. Question 2: In the reaction 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO, if 4 moles of Mg react completely, how many moles of MgO are produced?
    Answer: 2:2 ratio = 1:1, so 4 moles MgO
  3. Question 3: A student in Ibadan weighs 50 g of calcium (Ca, Molar mass = 40 g/mol). How many atoms of Ca does she have? (Avogadro's number = 6.02 × 10²³)
    Answer: Moles = 50 ÷ 40 = 1.25 moles. Atoms = 1.25 × 6.02 × 10²³ = 7.525 × 10²³ atoms
