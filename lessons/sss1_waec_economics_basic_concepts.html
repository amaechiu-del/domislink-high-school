Basic Concepts in Economics (SSS1 - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts Explained

  • Scarcity: The fundamental economic problem — unlimited human wants but limited resources (land, labour, capital, entrepreneurship).
  • Choice: Because of scarcity, individuals, firms, and governments must decide what goods and services to produce, how to produce them, and for whom.
  • Opportunity Cost: The value of the next best alternative foregone when a choice is made. It is not just money — it includes time, satisfaction, or other benefits.
  • Scale of Preference: A list of wants arranged in order of importance, helping to make rational choices.
  • Wants: Desires for goods and services. They are unlimited and recurring, while resources to satisfy them are finite.

Nigerian Examples

🇳🇬 Example 1: A Student in Lagos
Chidi has ₦500 and wants to buy either a textbook for Economics or a new shirt. He chooses the textbook. The opportunity cost is the shirt he did not buy — and the satisfaction he would have gotten from wearing it.

🇳🇬 Example 2: A Farmer in Kaduna
A farmer has a plot of land that can be used to grow either maize or yam. He decides to plant maize. The opportunity cost is the yam harvest he gave up. This illustrates scarcity (limited land) and choice (what to produce).

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know? In ancient Nigeria, cowrie shells were used as money. Because they were scarce in some regions, they had high value — a perfect real-world example of scarcity driving value!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define scarcity and explain why it is considered the basic economic problem.
  2. Question 2: Aisha has ₦1,000 and must choose between buying a novel or a geometry set. She buys the geometry set. What is the opportunity cost? Explain.
  3. Question 3: Give one Nigerian example of opportunity cost in a household decision.

📝 Answers:

Answer 1: Scarcity is the situation where human wants are unlimited but the resources to satisfy them are limited. It is the basic economic problem because all other economic issues (choice, opportunity cost, resource allocation) arise from scarcity.

Answer 2: The opportunity cost is the novel Aisha did not buy. Opportunity cost is the next best alternative foregone — in this case, the enjoyment and knowledge she would have gained from reading the novel.

Answer 3: Example: A family in Enugu has ₦20,000 and must choose between buying a new cooking pot or paying for electricity repairs. They choose the pot. The opportunity cost is the repaired electricity supply (lighting and appliance use).
