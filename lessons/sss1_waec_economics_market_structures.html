Market Structures in Economics

SSS1 Economics – WAEC Syllabus

Key Features of Market Structures

  • Perfect Competition: Many buyers and sellers; identical products (e.g., agricultural produce); free entry/exit; no single firm can influence price.
  • Monopolistic Competition: Many sellers; products are similar but differentiated (e.g., soap brands); non-price competition (advertising, branding).
  • Oligopoly: Few large firms dominate the market; high barriers to entry; firms are interdependent (e.g., telecom networks).
  • Monopoly: Single seller dominates; unique product with no close substitutes; high barriers to entry; firm can set price.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Perfect Competition: The Onion Market in Kano – hundreds of traders sell nearly identical onions, and prices are determined by supply and demand. No single trader can set the price.

2. Monopoly: The Nigerian Postal Service (NIPOST) – for many years, NIPOST was the only provider of letter delivery services in Nigeria, with no close substitute.

💡 Fun Fact

Did you know? In a monopoly, the firm can be a price maker – it sets the price because consumers have no alternative. However, in Nigeria, the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) regulates telecom monopolies to protect consumers from unfair pricing!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: In which market structure do firms produce identical products and have no control over price?
    Answer: Perfect Competition.
  2. Question 2: Give one Nigerian example of an oligopoly market.
    Answer: The mobile telecommunications industry (e.g., MTN, Glo, Airtel, 9mobile) – only a few large firms dominate.
  3. Question 3: What is the main difference between monopolistic competition and monopoly?
    Answer: In monopolistic competition, there are many sellers with differentiated products; in a monopoly, there is only one seller with a unique product and no close substitutes.
