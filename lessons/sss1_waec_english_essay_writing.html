Essay Writing – WAEC English for SSS1

Essay writing is a key skill in the WAEC English syllabus. You will be required to write clear, well-structured essays on various topics. The basic structure is: Introduction → Body → Conclusion.

Key Points to Remember

  • Plan before you write: Spend 5 minutes brainstorming and outlining your ideas.
  • Use paragraphs: Each paragraph should focus on one main idea.
  • Support your points: Use examples, facts, or personal experiences.
  • Use simple, clear English: Avoid overly complex words; clarity is better than confusion.
  • Revise and edit: Check for grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors.

Two Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Argumentative Essay
Topic: "Should the use of mobile phones be banned in secondary schools?"
Introduction: "Mobile phones have become a common sight in Nigerian schools. While they can be useful, many teachers argue that they distract students. This essay argues that mobile phones should be banned during school hours to improve concentration and academic performance."
Body: "Firstly, phones distract students during lessons. For instance, a study in Lagos showed that 70% of students check social media during class. Secondly, phones can be used for cheating during exams. In 2023, WAEC reported several cases of malpractice involving phones. Finally, banning phones encourages face-to-face interaction, which is important for developing social skills."
Conclusion: "In conclusion, the negative effects of mobile phones in schools outweigh their benefits. A ban during school hours will help students focus, reduce cheating, and build better relationships."
Example 2: Narrative Essay
Topic: "Write about a day you will never forget."
Introduction: "It was a hot Saturday in Kano when my life changed forever. I had just finished my JSS3 exams and my father took me to the village to meet my grandmother for the first time."
Body: "The journey was long and dusty. When we arrived, I saw my grandmother weaving a traditional basket. She taught me how to make one, and as we worked, she told me stories of our ancestors. I learned about the importance of family and tradition. That evening, we ate tuwo shinkafa together under the stars."
Conclusion: "That day taught me that our roots are precious. I will never forget the warmth of my grandmother's smile or the taste of her cooking. It was truly a day I will cherish forever."
Fun Fact: Did you know that the longest English essay ever written was over 1,000 pages? It was by a Nigerian student in 1985, but the teacher gave it back saying, "Next time, stick to the word limit!" 😄

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Write an argumentative essay on the topic: "Should the Nigerian government ban the use of plastic bags?" Outline your introduction, two body paragraphs, and conclusion.
  2. Question 2: Write a narrative essay titled "My First Day in Senior Secondary School." Describe your feelings, what you saw, and one memorable event.
  3. Question 3: Write an expository essay explaining the importance of reading books. Give at least three reasons with examples.

Answers (Sample outlines)

Answer 1 (Outline):
Introduction: Plastic bags cause pollution in Nigeria. This essay argues for a ban to protect the environment.
Body 1: They block drainage systems and cause flooding (e.g., Lagos floods).
Body 2: They harm animals that eat them (e.g., goats in markets).
Conclusion: A ban will reduce waste and save lives.

Answer 2 (Outline):
Introduction: I was nervous and excited on my first day at Government Secondary School, Abuja.
Body: I got lost looking for my classroom, but a kind senior student helped me. I made a friend named Chidi during break.
Conclusion: It was a day of new beginnings and I felt ready to learn.

Answer 3 (Outline):
Introduction: Reading books is essential for success in school and life.
Reason 1: Improves vocabulary and writing skills (e.g., reading novels helps with essays).
Reason 2: Boosts knowledge and critical thinking (e.g., reading history books).
Reason 3: Reduces stress and increases empathy (e.g., reading stories about other cultures).
Conclusion: Everyone should make reading a daily habit.
