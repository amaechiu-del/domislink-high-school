Lexis & Structure: Word Classes and Sentence Patterns

Key Concepts

  • Lexis refers to the vocabulary of a language—the set of words and their meanings. It includes synonyms, antonyms, homonyms, and word formation (e.g., affixes).
  • Structure deals with the arrangement of words to form correct sentences. This covers parts of speech, tenses, subject-verb agreement, and clause types (independent and dependent).
  • Sentence Patterns are the basic frameworks for English sentences, such as Subject-Verb-Object (SVO), Subject-Verb-Complement (SVC), and Subject-Verb-Adverbial (SVA).
  • Mastering Lexis & Structure helps you express ideas clearly, avoid ambiguity, and perform well in WAEC comprehension and essay sections.

Nigerian Examples in Context

Example 1 (Lexis): The word “gist” is widely used in Nigerian English to mean “a chat or interesting news.”
“After the meeting, we shared gist about the upcoming school festival.”

Example 2 (Structure): In Nigerian spoken English, the correct subject-verb agreement is often tested.
Correct: “The committee has submitted its report.” (Not: “The committee have submitted…”)

🎉 Fun Fact!

Practice Questions (WAEC Style)

  1. Choose the correct option to complete the sentence: “Neither the teacher nor the students ______ satisfied with the result.”
    A. is   B. are   C. was   D. has been
  2. Identify the word that is a synonym for “benevolent”:
    A. cruel   B. kind   C. lazy   D. proud
  3. Which sentence follows the Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) pattern?
    A. The boy ran quickly.
    B. She gave a gift.
    C. The dog is happy.
    D. They are in the garden.

Answers:

1. B. are (subject-verb agreement with “neither…nor” follows the nearest subject “students” – plural)

2. B. kind (benevolent means generous and kind)

3. B. She gave a gift. (Subject = She, Verb = gave, Object = a gift)
