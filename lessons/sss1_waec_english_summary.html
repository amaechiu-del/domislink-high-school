What is a Summary?

A summary is a brief, concise restatement of the main points of a passage in your own words. It should be shorter than the original and capture only the essential ideas, leaving out examples, repetitions, and minor details.

Key Rules for Summary Writing

  • Read carefully – Understand the passage before you begin
  • Identify the main idea – Ask: "What is the author's central point?"
  • Select only key points – Omit illustrations, statistics, and digressions
  • Use your own words – Do not copy sentences verbatim
  • Be concise – Target about ⅓ of the original length
  • Maintain logical flow – Your summary should read smoothly
  • Do not add opinions – Stick to the author's ideas only
  • Check word count – Stay within the specified limit

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (WAEC-style passage):
Original: "Agriculture remains the backbone of Nigeria's economy, employing over 35% of the workforce. Crops like cassava, yam, and cocoa are widely cultivated. However, poor storage facilities and lack of modern equipment reduce farmers' yields significantly."

Summary: "Agriculture, which employs over a third of Nigeria's workforce, faces challenges of poor storage and outdated equipment that lower crop yields."
Example 2 (Nigerian context):
Original: "The Aso Rock Villa in Abuja serves as the official residence and workplace of the Nigerian President. It hosts important state meetings, foreign dignitaries, and national ceremonies. The complex is heavily guarded and not open to the public."

Summary: "Aso Rock Villa in Abuja is the President's official residence where state meetings and ceremonies occur, and it is closed to the public."

⭐ Fun Fact

Did you know? The word "summary" comes from the Latin summa, meaning "total" or "gist." In Nigerian WAEC English exams, summary questions test your ability to condense a 150-word passage into just 60 words – that's a reduction of 60%!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Read the following passage and write a summary in not more than 40 words.
    "The harmattan season in Northern Nigeria brings dry, dusty winds from the Sahara Desert. Temperatures drop sharply at night, and visibility is often reduced. Despite the discomfort, many locals enjoy the cool weather and use the season for harvest festivals."

    Answer: The harmattan in Northern Nigeria brings dry winds and cold nights, reducing visibility, but locals appreciate the cool weather and harvest festivals.

  2. Question 2: Identify the three main points in this passage:
    "Lagos is Nigeria's largest city and economic hub. Its traffic congestion is notorious, yet the city's nightlife and entertainment scene attract many tourists. The government is investing in new rail lines to ease transport."

    Answer: (i) Lagos is Nigeria's largest economic city. (ii) It has heavy traffic but vibrant nightlife. (iii) The government is building rail lines to improve transport.

  3. Question 3: Which of these is NOT a rule of summary writing?
    A) Use your own words
    B) Include all examples from the original
    C) Keep it brief
    D) Maintain the original meaning

    Answer: B (Include all examples from the original)
