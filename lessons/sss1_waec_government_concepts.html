Concepts in Government (SSS1 - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Power – The ability to influence or control the behaviour of others, even against their will. It can be coercive (force) or persuasive.
  • Authority – The legitimate or accepted right to exercise power. It is based on consent, tradition, or law (e.g., a president's constitutional authority).
  • Legitimacy – The acceptance by the people that a government's rule is lawful and rightful. It is earned through free elections, good governance, and rule of law.
  • Sovereignty – The absolute and supreme power of a state to govern itself without external interference. It includes internal (over citizens) and external (independence from other states) dimensions.

Nigerian Examples

1. Power & Authority in the Nigerian Presidency: The President of Nigeria derives authority from the 1999 Constitution and the mandate of the electorate. However, when the military seized power in coups (e.g., 1966, 1983), they had power (through force) but lacked legitimacy until later transitions.

2. Sovereignty and the Biafran War (1967–1970): The Federal Government of Nigeria asserted its sovereignty over the Eastern Region when it declared secession as Biafra. The war ended with Nigeria maintaining its territorial integrity, reinforcing the principle that sovereign states cannot be broken up unilaterally.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "sovereignty" comes from the Old French word soverain, meaning "supreme ruler." In Nigeria, the 1999 Constitution states that sovereignty belongs to the people – meaning no individual or group can claim absolute power without the people's consent.

Practice Questions

  1. Define the concept of legitimacy in government. Why is it important for a government to be seen as legitimate?
  2. Differentiate between power and authority, using an example from Nigerian politics.
  3. Explain how the concept of sovereignty applies to the relationship between the Federal Government of Nigeria and state governments.

Answers (for self-check)

Answer 1: Legitimacy is the acceptance by citizens that a government's rule is lawful and rightful. It is important because without legitimacy, a government may face resistance, instability, or rebellion. Example: The 2015 Nigerian election was seen as legitimate because it was peaceful and widely accepted.

Answer 2: Power is the ability to compel obedience, even through force; authority is the right to command based on law or consent. Example: A police officer has authority to arrest (given by law), but a mob using force has power without authority. In Nigeria, the President has both power and authority under the Constitution.

Answer 3: Sovereignty means the Federal Government has supreme authority over the entire country. State governments exercise delegated powers but cannot override federal laws on matters like defence, currency, or foreign affairs. The 1999 Constitution divides powers, but ultimate sovereignty rests with the Federal Republic of Nigeria.
