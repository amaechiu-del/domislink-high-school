Forms of Government

SSS1 Government – WAEC Syllabus

Key Forms of Government

  • Democracy: Power rests with the people, exercised through voting and representation. Emphasizes individual rights and majority rule.
  • Monarchy: A single ruler (monarch) holds power, often by hereditary succession. Can be absolute (full control) or constitutional (limited by law).
  • Military Government: Armed forces take control, usually via a coup. Often suspends the constitution and civil liberties.
  • Oligarchy: Rule by a small group of elites (wealthy, military, or political).
  • Theocracy: Religious leaders govern in the name of a deity.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Democracy (1999–present): Nigeria operates a federal presidential constitutional republic. Citizens elect the President and National Assembly. The Fourth Republic began in 1999 after decades of military rule.
  2. Military Rule (1966–1999, intermittently): Nigeria experienced several military coups, e.g., General Ibrahim Babangida (1985–1993) and General Sani Abacha (1993–1998). These regimes suspended the constitution and ruled by decree.
Fun Fact: Nigeria is often called the "Giant of Africa" and has the largest population of any African country. Its journey from military rule to democracy in 1999 inspired many other African nations to pursue democratic reforms!

Practice Questions

  1. Define democracy and list two features of a democratic government.
  2. What is the difference between an absolute monarchy and a constitutional monarchy?
  3. Name one military ruler in Nigeria's history and the period they ruled.
Answers:
  1. Democracy is a system where citizens hold power through voting. Features: free and fair elections, rule of law, protection of human rights.
  2. Absolute monarchy: the monarch has unlimited power (e.g., Saudi Arabia). Constitutional monarchy: the monarch's powers are limited by a constitution (e.g., United Kingdom).
  3. General Sani Abacha (1993–1998).
