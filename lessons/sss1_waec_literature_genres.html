📚 Genres in Literature (SSS1 – WAEC Syllabus)

🔍 What are Genres?

  • Genre means a category or type of literature based on style, form, and content.
  • Drama is written to be performed on stage. It uses dialogue, acts, and scenes. Examples: tragedy, comedy, melodrama.
  • Prose is written in ordinary language (not verse). It includes novels, short stories, and novellas. It often has chapters and paragraphs.
  • Poetry uses rhythm, rhyme, and imagery. It is written in lines and stanzas. Common forms: sonnet, haiku, epic, lyric.
  • Each genre has its own conventions and purposes, helping writers express ideas in different ways.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

📘 Prose Example: Things Fall Apart by Chinua Achebe – A novel about pre-colonial Igbo society and the impact of colonialism.

🎭 Drama Example: The Lion and the Jewel by Wole Soyinka – A play that uses comedy and drama to explore tradition versus modernity in a Yoruba village.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The oldest known genre is poetry – the Epic of Gilgamesh was written over 4,000 years ago! In Nigeria, oral poetry like Ijala (Yoruba hunting poetry) has been passed down for centuries.

✍️ Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Which genre of literature is primarily meant to be performed on stage?
    Answer: Drama.
  2. Question 2: Name one Nigerian novel and its author (from the WAEC syllabus).
    Answer: Things Fall Apart by Chinua Achebe (or The Joys of Motherhood by Buchi Emecheta, etc.).
  3. Question 3: What is the main difference between prose and poetry?
    Answer: Prose uses ordinary language and paragraphs, while poetry uses rhythm, rhyme, and stanzas.
