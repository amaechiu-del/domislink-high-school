Literary Appreciation (SSS1 Literature - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Elements of Literary Appreciation

  • Theme & Message: The central idea or underlying meaning of a literary work. What lesson or insight does the author want to convey?
  • Character & Plot: The people (or beings) in the story and the sequence of events. Appreciating how characters develop and how the plot unfolds is essential.
  • Language & Style: The choice of words, imagery, figurative language (similes, metaphors, personification), and sentence structure used by the author to create effect.
  • Mood & Tone: The atmosphere or feeling the reader experiences (mood) and the author's attitude toward the subject (tone).
  • Point of View: The perspective from which the story is told (first person, third person, omniscient).

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Lion and the Jewel by Wole Soyinka
Appreciate Soyinka's use of dramatic irony and poetic language to explore the clash between tradition (Baroka) and modernity (Lakunle). The theme of female agency is embodied by Sidi, whose choice reveals deeper social commentary.

Example 2: Things Fall Apart by Chinua Achebe
Achebe's proverbial style and third-person narrative immerse the reader in Igbo culture. Appreciate how the tragic hero, Okonkwo, is shaped by his fear of weakness, and how the novel's mood shifts from communal harmony to despair as colonialism disrupts Umuofia.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The word "appreciation" comes from Latin appretiare, meaning "to set a price or value." In literature, appreciation means not just reading, but valuing the artistry—like a detective uncovering hidden treasures in words!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: In Achebe's Things Fall Apart, what literary device does the author use when Okonkwo is described as "a man who ruled his household with a heavy hand"? (Answer: Metaphor / Imagery)
  2. Question 2: Identify two elements a reader should examine to appreciate the mood of a poem by Niyi Osundare. (Answer: Word choice/diction and sound devices like alliteration or rhythm)
  3. Question 3: How does Soyinka use character contrast in The Lion and the Jewel to highlight the theme of tradition vs. modernity? (Answer: Baroka (tradition) vs. Lakunle (modernity) — Sidi's choice between them reveals the value of cultural roots.)

Answers:
1. Metaphor/Imagery (describing his rule as "heavy hand" creates a vivid picture).
2. Diction (words like "gloom," "shadow") and sound devices (e.g., repetition, rhyme) to set atmosphere.
3. Baroka represents traditional wisdom and cunning; Lakunle represents shallow modernity. Sidi's eventual choice of Baroka shows the triumph of deep-rooted culture over superficial change.
