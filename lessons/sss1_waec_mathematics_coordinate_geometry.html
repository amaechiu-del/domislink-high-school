Key Concepts in Coordinate Geometry

  • Cartesian Plane: Two perpendicular axes (x-axis horizontal, y-axis vertical) intersect at the origin (0,0).
  • Coordinates: Points are written as (x, y) — first the horizontal position, then the vertical position.
  • Quadrants: The axes divide the plane into four quadrants (I: x>0, y>0; II: x<0, y>0; III: x<0, y<0; IV: x>0, y<0).
  • Distance Formula: Distance between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]
  • Midpoint Formula: Midpoint = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)
  • Gradient (Slope): m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁). Positive slope = line rises; negative slope = line falls.

Nigerian Example 1: Distance Between Two Cities

On a map of Nigeria, Lagos is at point L(2, 3) and Abuja is at point A(7, 15). Find the straight-line distance between them (units in km).

Solution: Distance = √[(7 - 2)² + (15 - 3)²] = √[5² + 12²] = √(25 + 144) = √169 = 13 km

Nigerian Example 2: Midpoint of a Road

A straight road connects Ibadan at I(1, 4) and Kano at K(9, 10). A petrol station is built exactly halfway. Find its coordinates.

Solution: Midpoint = ((1 + 9)/2, (4 + 10)/2) = (10/2, 14/2) = (5, 7)

💡 Fun Fact: The coordinate system is named after French mathematician René Descartes, who supposedly thought of it while watching a fly crawl across his ceiling tiles! In Nigeria, coordinate geometry helps surveyors map land boundaries and plan new roads.

Practice Questions

  1. Find the gradient of the line passing through points P(2, 5) and Q(6, 13).
  2. Calculate the distance between points A(-3, 4) and B(5, -2).
  3. If the midpoint of a segment joining X(4, y) and Y(10, 8) is (7, 6), find the value of y.

Answers

  1. m = (13 - 5)/(6 - 2) = 8/4 = 2
  2. Distance = √[(5 - (-3))² + (-2 - 4)²] = √[8² + (-6)²] = √(64 + 36) = √100 = 10 units
  3. Midpoint y-coordinate: (y + 8)/2 = 6 → y + 8 = 12 → y = 4
