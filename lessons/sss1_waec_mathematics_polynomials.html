Polynomials (SSS1 Mathematics – WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Polynomial – an algebraic expression with one or more terms, e.g., 3x² + 2x – 5.
  • Degree – the highest power of the variable. Example: 4x³ – x + 7 has degree 3.
  • Leading coefficient – the coefficient of the term with the highest degree.
  • Constant term – the term without a variable (e.g., -5 in 3x² + 2x – 5).
  • Monomial, Binomial, Trinomial – one, two, or three terms respectively.
  • Like terms – terms with same variable and exponent (e.g., 2x² and -5x²).

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (WAEC-style): Simplify 3x² + 5x – 2x² + 4x – 7.

Solution: Group like terms: (3x² – 2x²) + (5x + 4x) – 7 = x² + 9x – 7.

Example 2 (Everyday context): A farmer in Oyo State has a rectangular plot. Length = (2x + 3) m, width = (x – 1) m. Find the perimeter as a polynomial.

Solution: Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2[(2x+3) + (x–1)] = 2(3x + 2) = 6x + 4 metres.

🧠 Fun Fact: The word "polynomial" comes from Greek poly (many) and Latin nomen (name). In ancient Nigeria, the Yoruba people used polynomial-like patterns in Ishan art to represent growth and balance!

Practice Questions

  1. Identify the degree and leading coefficient of 7 – 4x³ + 2x² – x.
  2. Add the polynomials: (5x² – 3x + 2) + (x² + 4x – 6).
  3. A taxi driver in Lagos charges a base fare of ₦200 plus ₦50 per km. Write a polynomial for the fare for x km.

Answers:

  1. Degree = 3, Leading coefficient = -4.
  2. 6x² + x – 4.
  3. Fare = 50x + 200.
