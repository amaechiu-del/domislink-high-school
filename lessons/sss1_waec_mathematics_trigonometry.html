Trigonometry: Introduction to Sine, Cosine & Tangent

Key Definitions

  • Hypotenuse – the longest side, opposite the right angle.
  • Opposite side – the side across from the angle θ.
  • Adjacent side – the side next to angle θ (not the hypotenuse).
  • Sine (sin) θ = Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos) θ = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan) θ = Opposite ÷ Adjacent
  • Remember: SOH CAH TOA – a simple way to recall the ratios!

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A ladder leans against a wall in Lagos. The ladder reaches 8 m up the wall and its base is 6 m from the wall. Find the angle the ladder makes with the ground.

Solution: Opposite = 8 m, Adjacent = 6 m. Tan θ = 8/6 = 1.3333. θ = tan⁻¹(1.3333) ≈ 53.1°

Example 2: A flagpole in Abuja casts a shadow 12 m long when the angle of elevation of the sun is 60°. How tall is the flagpole?

Solution: Tan 60° = Height / 12. Height = 12 × tan 60° = 12 × 1.732 = 20.78 m

🎉 Fun Fact: The word "trigonometry" comes from Greek words trigonon (triangle) and metron (measure). Ancient Nigerian astronomers used similar angle calculations to track the movement of stars across the savannah!

Practice Questions

  1. In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite angle A is 5 cm and the hypotenuse is 13 cm. Find sin A.
  2. A tree casts a shadow 15 m long. The angle of elevation to the top of the tree is 45°. How tall is the tree?
  3. If cos θ = 0.6 and the adjacent side is 9 cm, find the length of the hypotenuse.

Answers

  1. sin A = 5/13 ≈ 0.3846
  2. Height = 15 × tan 45° = 15 × 1 = 15 m
  3. Hypotenuse = 9 ÷ 0.6 = 15 cm
