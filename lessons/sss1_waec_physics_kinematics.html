Kinematics: The Study of Motion

Definition: Kinematics is the branch of physics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion.

Key Concepts in Kinematics

  • Distance (s): Total length of path travelled (scalar). SI unit: metre (m).
  • Displacement (s): Shortest straight-line distance from start to finish (vector).
  • Speed (v): Rate of change of distance. Speed = Distance / Time
  • Velocity (v): Rate of change of displacement (vector). Velocity = Displacement / Time
  • Acceleration (a): Rate of change of velocity. Acceleration = (v - u) / t

Nigerian Examples

🇳🇬 Example 1: Lagos to Ibadan Road Trip
A car travels from Lagos to Ibadan (150 km) in 2 hours. What is its average speed?
Solution: Speed = 150 km / 2 h = 75 km/h. If the car returns to Lagos, displacement is 0 km, so average velocity = 0 km/h.

🇳🇬 Example 2: Abuja Market Runner
A boy runs from one end of a market to the other, 200 m in 40 seconds. He then turns and runs back 100 m in 20 seconds. What is his average velocity?
Solution: Net displacement = 200 m - 100 m = 100 m. Total time = 40 s + 20 s = 60 s. Velocity = 100 m / 60 s = 1.67 m/s.

💡 Fun Fact: The fastest land animal in Nigeria is the cheetah, but did you know a peregrine falcon can reach speeds of over 320 km/h when diving? That's faster than many cars on the Lagos-Ibadan expressway!

Practice Questions

  1. A student walks 30 m north, then 40 m east. What is the total distance travelled and the magnitude of the displacement?
  2. A car accelerates from rest at 4 m/s². What is its velocity after 5 seconds?
  3. A bus travels 240 km from Enugu to Onitsha in 3 hours. What is its average speed in km/h and m/s?

Answers

  1. Distance: 30 m + 40 m = 70 m. Displacement: √(30² + 40²) = √(900 + 1600) = √2500 = 50 m north-east.
  2. v = u + at = 0 + (4 m/s² × 5 s) = 20 m/s.
  3. Average speed: 240 km / 3 h = 80 km/h. In m/s: 80 × (1000/3600) = 22.22 m/s.
