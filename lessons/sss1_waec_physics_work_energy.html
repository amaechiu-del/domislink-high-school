Work and Energy (SSS1 Physics – WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Work is done when a force moves an object through a distance. Formula: Work = Force × Distance (Joules, J).
  • Energy is the ability to do work. It exists in forms like kinetic, potential, thermal, and chemical.
  • Kinetic Energy = ½ × mass × velocity² (J). Energy of motion.
  • Potential Energy = mass × gravity × height (J). Stored energy due to position.
  • Principle of Conservation of Energy: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A farmer in Kano pushes a wheelbarrow loaded with 50 kg of groundnuts over a distance of 10 m using a force of 200 N. Work done = 200 N × 10 m = 2000 J.

Example 2: A student in Lagos lifts a 5 kg physics textbook from the floor to a table 0.8 m high. Potential energy gained = 5 kg × 10 m/s² × 0.8 m = 40 J.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "energy" comes from the Greek word energeia meaning "activity" or "operation". In Nigeria, the energy from the sun (solar) is increasingly used to power homes and schools in rural areas!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A boy applies a force of 40 N to push a box across a floor over a distance of 5 m. How much work does he do?
  2. Question 2: A ball of mass 2 kg is dropped from a height of 10 m. What is its potential energy just before release? (Take g = 10 m/s²)
  3. Question 3: State the Principle of Conservation of Energy and give one example from daily life in Nigeria.

✅ Answers

  • Answer 1: Work = 40 N × 5 m = 200 J.
  • Answer 2: Potential energy = 2 kg × 10 m/s² × 10 m = 200 J.
  • Answer 3: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed. Example: A generator converts chemical energy (fuel) into electrical energy for home use in Nigeria.
