Growth in Biology (SSS2 - NERDC Syllabus)

Definition: Growth is the irreversible increase in the size, mass, and complexity of an organism, usually accompanied by an increase in the number of cells (cell division) or cell enlargement. It is a fundamental characteristic of all living things.

Key Concepts of Growth

  • Irreversible: Growth is permanent; a plant or animal cannot shrink back to its earlier size (except temporary wilting or weight loss).
  • Measurable parameters: Growth is measured by increase in height, weight, volume, and number of cells.
  • Phases of growth: In plants, growth passes through the lag phase (slow initial growth), log phase (rapid exponential growth), and stationary phase (growth slows due to limiting factors).
  • Types of growth:
    • Primary growth – increase in length (apical meristems in plants).
    • Secondary growth – increase in girth (lateral meristems in woody plants).
  • Factors affecting growth: Light, water, nutrients, temperature, hormones (e.g., auxins in plants, growth hormone in animals), and genetic factors.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Yam (Dioscorea spp.)
During the rainy season in Nigeria, yam vines grow rapidly due to abundant water and warm temperatures. The tubers enlarge through cell division and storage of starch, a clear case of primary and secondary growth.

Example 2 – The African Giant Snail (Archachatina marginata)
Common in southern Nigeria, this snail grows by adding new shell whorls and increasing body mass. Its growth rate slows during the dry season (harmattan) when food and moisture are scarce.

🐌 Fun Fact: Did you know? The Baobab tree (Adansonia digitata), found in northern Nigeria, can increase its trunk girth by several centimetres each year! Some baobabs are over 1,000 years old and still growing – their secondary growth never truly stops.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define growth in biology and state two measurable parameters used to study it.
    Answer: Growth is the irreversible increase in size, mass, and complexity of an organism. Measurable parameters include height (length) and weight (mass).
  2. Question 2: Differentiate between primary growth and secondary growth in plants. Give one Nigerian crop example for each.
    Answer: Primary growth is increase in length (e.g., maize stem elongating). Secondary growth is increase in girth (e.g., the thickening trunk of a mango tree).
  3. Question 3: List three environmental factors that affect the growth of yam in Nigeria and explain how each influences growth.
    Answer: (i) Water – needed for cell turgor and nutrient transport; (ii) Temperature – optimal 25-30°C for enzyme activity; (iii) Nutrients – nitrogen for leaf growth, potassium for tuber development.
