Electrochemistry: Redox Reactions & Electrochemical Cells

Key Concepts

  • Electrochemistry deals with the interconversion of chemical energy and electrical energy via redox reactions.
  • An electrochemical cell consists of two half-cells: anode (oxidation) and cathode (reduction).
  • The salt bridge completes the circuit by allowing ions to flow, maintaining electrical neutrality.
  • Electrons flow from anode to cathode through the external wire — this is the electric current.
  • In the Daniel cell: Zn (anode) oxidises: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻; Cu²⁺ (cathode) reduces: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In the Obajana Cement Factory (Kogi State), electrochemistry principles are used in the electrolytic refining of metals used in machinery parts, where impure copper is purified via electrolysis.
Example 2: Battery-powered inverters common in Nigerian homes (e.g., during power outages) rely on electrochemical cells — lead-acid batteries store chemical energy and convert it to electrical energy when needed.
⚡ Fun Fact: The world's first battery (the Voltaic Pile) was invented by Alessandro Volta in 1800. It used alternating discs of zinc and copper separated by cloth soaked in brine — just like the Daniel cell you just studied!

Practice Questions

  1. In the Daniel cell, which electrode is the anode and what happens there?
  2. What is the function of the salt bridge in an electrochemical cell?
  3. Write the overall redox reaction for the Zn-Cu cell and calculate the standard cell potential (E°cell) given: Zn²⁺/Zn = -0.76V, Cu²⁺/Cu = +0.34V.

Answers

1. The zinc electrode is the anode. Oxidation occurs: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻ (zinc loses electrons and dissolves into solution).

2. The salt bridge allows migration of ions (e.g., K⁺ and Cl⁻) between half-cells to maintain electrical neutrality and prevent charge buildup, thereby completing the circuit.

3. Overall reaction: Zn + Cu²⁺ → Zn²⁺ + Cu. Standard cell potential E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode = (+0.34 V) - (-0.76 V) = +1.10 V.
