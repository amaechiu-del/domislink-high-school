Understanding Development in Economics (SSS2 - NERDC Syllabus)

Key Concepts of Development

  • Economic Development is a broader concept than economic growth — it includes improvements in living standards, health, education, and reduction in poverty.
  • Indicators of Development: GDP per capita, Human Development Index (HDI), literacy rates, life expectancy, and access to clean water and electricity.
  • Stages of Development: Traditional (subsistence agriculture) → Transition (industrialization) → Modern (service/knowledge-based economy).
  • Structural Transformation: Movement of labor from agriculture to industry and services, accompanied by urbanization and technological change.
  • Sustainable Development: Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet theirs — includes economic, social, and environmental dimensions.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Lagos-Ibadan Railway Modernisation
This project, part of the Chinese-built standard gauge rail line, reduced travel time between Lagos and Ibadan from 3+ hours by road to about 2 hours by train. It boosted trade, created jobs, and improved connectivity — a clear example of infrastructure-driven development.

Example 2: M-KOPA Solar in Rural Nigeria
M-KOPA provides pay-as-you-go solar home systems to off-grid households in Nigeria. Over 200,000 Nigerian homes now have clean energy, enabling children to study at night, reducing kerosene use, and creating local jobs — illustrating inclusive development through technology.

💡
Fun Fact: Nigeria's Human Development Index (HDI) value for 2022 was 0.539, placing it in the "low human development" category. However, between 2000 and 2022, Nigeria's HDI increased by 48.3% — from 0.364 to 0.539 — showing significant progress in health, education, and income!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Explain the difference between economic growth and economic development, using one example from Nigeria.
  2. Question 2: List three indicators used to measure the level of development in a country. Why is the Human Development Index (HDI) considered a better measure than just GDP per capita?
  3. Question 3: Describe two challenges that Nigeria faces in achieving sustainable development.

Answers

Answer 1: Economic growth refers to an increase in a country's output of goods and services (GDP), while economic development includes improvements in living standards, health, education, and poverty reduction. Nigerian example: Nigeria's GDP grew due to oil exports (growth), but many rural communities still lack electricity and clean water (lack of development). The Lagos-Ibadan railway shows development because it improves mobility and access to markets.

Answer 2: Three indicators: (i) GDP per capita, (ii) Literacy rate, (iii) Life expectancy. HDI is better because it combines income, education, and health into a single index — a country could have high GDP but poor health and education (e.g., some oil-rich nations).

Answer 3: Two challenges: (i) Infrastructure deficit — poor roads, unstable electricity, and limited rail networks hinder industrial growth and access to markets. (ii) Environmental degradation — oil spills in the Niger Delta and deforestation from agriculture threaten long-term sustainability and livelihoods.
