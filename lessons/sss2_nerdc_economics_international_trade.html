International Trade – Economics (SSS2, NERDC Syllabus)

  • International Trade – The exchange of goods, services, and capital across national borders. Countries trade because no nation is self-sufficient.
  • Why trade? Differences in resources, climate, technology, and skills. It allows countries to specialize in what they produce best (comparative advantage).
  • Exports – Goods/services sold to other countries (e.g., Nigeria exports crude oil). Imports – Goods/services bought from other countries (e.g., Nigeria imports refined petroleum, machinery).
  • Balance of Trade – Difference between a country's exports and imports. Nigeria often has a trade surplus because of oil exports.
  • Benefits: Access to wider variety of goods, lower prices, technology transfer, and economic growth.
  • Challenges: Trade deficits, dumping, protectionism (tariffs, quotas), and currency fluctuations.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Nigeria exports crude oil to India and imports refined petroleum, cars, and electronics. This shows a classic trade pattern: raw materials for manufactured goods.

Example 2: Nigeria exports agricultural products like cocoa, cashew nuts, and sesame seeds to Europe and the USA, while importing machinery for its manufacturing sector from China and Germany.

💡 Fun Fact

Did you know? Nigeria is the largest producer of cassava in the world, yet it imports cassava starch and other derivatives! This highlights the need for value-added processing to boost trade earnings.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define the term "balance of trade" and state whether Nigeria generally has a surplus or deficit in its trade balance.
  2. Question 2: Explain one reason why countries like Nigeria trade with other nations instead of producing everything locally.
  3. Question 3: Name two goods Nigeria exports and two goods it imports.

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: Balance of trade is the difference between a country's exports and imports over a period. Nigeria generally has a trade surplus due to its large crude oil exports.
  2. Answer 2: Countries trade because of comparative advantage – e.g., Nigeria has abundant oil reserves (natural resource advantage), while China has advanced manufacturing technology. Both benefit by specializing and trading.
  3. Answer 3: Exports: Crude oil, cocoa. Imports: Refined petroleum, machinery/electronics.
