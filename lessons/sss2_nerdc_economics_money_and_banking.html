Key Concepts in Money & Banking

  • Money serves as a medium of exchange, store of value, and unit of account.
  • Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) is the apex bank responsible for monetary policy, issuing the Naira, and supervising all banks.
  • Commercial banks (e.g., Access Bank, UBA) accept deposits from customers and provide loans to individuals and businesses.
  • Credit creation occurs when banks lend out a portion of deposits, multiplying money in the economy.
  • Monetary policy tools include Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR), interest rates, and open market operations.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: CBN's Naira Redesign (2023)
The CBN redesigned the N200, N500, and N1,000 notes to control money supply, reduce counterfeit, and encourage digital banking. This affected cash hoarders and boosted e-payment platforms like Opay and PalmPay.
Example 2: Microfinance Banks in Rural Nigeria
Banks like LAPO Microfinance provide small loans to market women in Lagos and farmers in Kaduna, enabling them to buy inventory or equipment without collateral, thus driving grassroots economic growth.
💡 Fun Fact: The Naira gets its name from the word "Nigeria" and was introduced in 1973, replacing the Nigerian pound. The smallest Naira coin ever minted was the ½ kobo coin — now virtually extinct!

Practice Questions

  1. What are the three primary functions of money? Provide a brief explanation of each.
  2. Explain how the Central Bank of Nigeria can use the Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR) to control inflation.
  3. Why might a commercial bank refuse to give a loan to a small business owner in Nigeria? List two possible reasons.

Answers

  1. Medium of exchange (used to buy goods), store of value (holds purchasing power over time), unit of account (common measure of value for prices).
  2. If the CBN increases the CRR, commercial banks must keep more reserves at CBN, reducing their ability to lend. This decreases money supply, helping to lower inflation.
  3. Possible reasons: (a) The business lacks collateral or a credit history; (b) The bank considers the business too risky due to unstable cash flow or poor financial records.
