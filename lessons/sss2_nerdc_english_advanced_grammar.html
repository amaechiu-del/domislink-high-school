KEY CONCEPTS

  • Complex Sentence: Contains one main clause and at least one subordinate clause.
  • The subordinate clause cannot stand alone — it depends on the main clause for meaning.
  • Subordinating conjunctions (e.g., because, when, although) link the clauses.
  • Comma rules: If the subordinate clause begins the sentence, use a comma after it.

🇳🇬 NIGERIAN EXAMPLES

1. "Although the traffic was heavy, we arrived at the stadium on time."

2. "The students were excited because their team won the inter-house sports competition."

💡 FUN FACT

The longest English sentence ever written (from a novel by James Joyce) contains over 4,000 words and is essentially one complex sentence! In Nigerian English, complex sentences often reflect our rich oral storytelling tradition.

📝 PRACTICE QUESTIONS

  1. Identify the subordinate clause: "When the principal arrived, the students stood up."
    Answer: "When the principal arrived"
  2. Combine these sentences into one complex sentence: "The match was cancelled. It was raining heavily."
    Answer: "The match was cancelled because it was raining heavily."
  3. Add a comma if needed: "Although she was tired she continued reading."
    Answer: "Although she was tired, she continued reading."
