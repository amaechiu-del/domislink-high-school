Key Steps for Effective Comprehension

  • Preview – Scan headings, subheadings, images, and captions before reading to get an overview.
  • Predict – Make educated guesses about what the passage will discuss based on the preview.
  • Annotate – Underline key points, circle unfamiliar words, and jot down questions in the margins.
  • Infer – Read between the lines to understand implied meanings, tone, and the author’s purpose.
  • Summarise – After reading, restate the main idea and key supporting details in your own words.
  • Reflect – Connect the text to your own experience, other texts, or real-world issues.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A passage about “The Harmattan Season in Northern Nigeria” describes dry winds, dusty skies, and cracked skin. To comprehend it well, a student should preview the title, predict that it will discuss effects on daily life, and annotate phrases like “haze reduces visibility” and “farmers store grains early.” Inferring the author’s purpose helps the student understand why the season is both challenging and culturally significant.

Example 2: A comprehension text titled “The Importance of Palm Oil in Igbo Tradition” mentions its use in cooking, ceremonies, and medicine. A good reader will connect this to prior knowledge about Nigerian cuisine, ask why palm oil is sacred in some rituals, and summarise the economic and cultural roles of palm oil in the South-East.

🧠 Fun Fact

Did you know? The word “comprehension” comes from Latin “comprehendere” meaning “to grasp together.” Skilled readers actually use both sides of their brain — the left for decoding words and the right for visualising scenes and emotions described in the text!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the first step you should take before reading a comprehension passage?
  2. If a passage says “The market women packed their goods quickly as the sky darkened,” what can you infer is about to happen?
  3. Why is summarising an important post-reading activity?

Answers

  1. Preview the passage by scanning headings, subheadings, images, and captions to get an idea of the content.
  2. You can infer that a rainstorm or heavy downpour is about to occur, because the sky darkening often signals rain, and the women are hurrying to protect their goods.
  3. Summarising helps you check your understanding, identify the main idea and key details, and remember the text more clearly for discussion or exams.
