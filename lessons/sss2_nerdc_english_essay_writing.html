Essay Writing (SSS2 English - NERDC Syllabus)

Key Components of an Essay

  • Introduction: Grabs the reader's attention with a hook (e.g., a question, quote, or surprising fact). Provides background and ends with a clear thesis statement (your main argument).
  • Body Paragraphs: Each paragraph covers one main idea. Start with a topic sentence, support it with evidence (facts, examples, statistics), and explain how it relates to your thesis.
  • Conclusion: Restates the thesis in new words, summarizes key points, and leaves the reader with a lasting impression or call to action.
  • Cohesion: Use transition words like furthermore, however, consequently to connect ideas smoothly.
  • Clarity: Avoid slang and overly complex sentences. Write in clear, precise English.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: "The Importance of Agriculture in Nigeria's Economy"

Introduction: "Nigeria, once known as the 'Giant of Africa' for its agricultural exports, now struggles with food imports. This essay argues that reviving agriculture is essential for economic growth and job creation."

Body (Paragraph 2): "Firstly, agriculture employs over 35% of Nigeria's workforce, from cocoa farmers in Ondo to rice growers in Kebbi. By investing in modern farming equipment, the government can boost productivity and reduce unemployment."

Conclusion: "In conclusion, agriculture remains the backbone of Nigeria's economy. Through targeted policies and youth empowerment, the nation can reclaim its agricultural glory."

Example 2: "The Impact of Social Media on Nigerian Youths"

Introduction: "From Lagos to Port Harcourt, Nigerian youths spend hours on platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram. While social media connects them globally, it also poses risks to their academic focus and mental health."

Body (Paragraph 3): "Furthermore, social media has birthed new opportunities—many young Nigerians now earn a living as influencers or online traders. However, the pressure to portray a perfect life online often leads to anxiety."

Conclusion: "Therefore, while social media offers benefits, Nigerian youths must balance its use with real-world engagement and prioritize their education."

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Write an introduction paragraph for an essay titled: "The Role of Education in Combating Corruption in Nigeria." Include a hook and a thesis statement.
  2. Question 2: List three transition words you could use to begin body paragraphs in an essay about "The Benefits of Renewable Energy in Nigeria."
  3. Question 3: Identify the error in this conclusion: "In conclusion, I have talked about corruption, education, and poverty. So, that's it." Rewrite it properly.

Answers

Answer 1: "Imagine a Nigeria where public funds are used for schools, not stolen. Education is the key to building honest leaders and citizens. This essay will argue that quality education reduces corruption by promoting accountability and critical thinking."

Answer 2: "Firstly," "Additionally," "Moreover" (or "Furthermore," "In addition," "Consequently").

Answer 3: The error is that it is too informal and does not restate the thesis. Correct version: "In conclusion, corruption, education, and poverty are deeply connected. By investing in education, Nigeria can break this cycle and build a more just society."
