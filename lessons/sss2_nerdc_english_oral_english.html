Oral English: Intonation in Questions

Key Points

  • Rising intonation (↗) is used for Yes/No questions – the voice goes up at the end.
  • Falling intonation (↘) is used for Wh- questions (who, what, where, when, why, how) – the voice falls at the end.
  • Intonation affects meaning: a question with wrong intonation may sound rude or confusing.
  • Practice by listening to natural speech and mimicking the pitch changes.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A student asks the teacher: "Are we submitting our assignment tomorrow?" (Rising intonation – yes/no question)

Example 2: A market woman asks a customer: "How much did you pay for that yam?" (Falling intonation – Wh- question)

💡 Fun Fact: In Nigerian Pidgin English, intonation can change the meaning completely! For example, "You go?" (rising) means "Are you going?" while "You go" (falling) means "You will go." The same words, different tunes!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Does the question "Did you eat amala today?" use rising or falling intonation?
    Answer: Rising intonation (Yes/No question).
  2. Question 2: Which intonation pattern is correct for "Why did you leave early?"?
    Answer: Falling intonation (Wh- question).
  3. Question 3: Read this aloud: "Can she come to the party?" – Does your voice go up or down at the end?
    Answer: Up (rising intonation), because it is a Yes/No question.
