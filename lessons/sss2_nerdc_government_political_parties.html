Political Parties in Government (SSS2 - NERDC Syllabus)

What is a Political Party?

  • Definition: A group of people who share similar political ideas and work together to win elections and control government.
  • Key Functions:
    • Nominate candidates for public office.
    • Formulate policies (manifestos) to address national issues.
    • Provide a link between citizens and the government.
    • Organize opposition and hold the ruling party accountable.
  • Types:
    • Mass parties: Large membership, broad appeal (e.g., APC, PDP).
    • Elite parties: Small, influential membership.
    • Single-party system: Only one legal party (rare in democracies).
    • Multi-party system: Multiple parties compete freely (Nigeria’s system).
  • Features: A party must have a name, constitution, membership register, manifesto, and leadership structure.

Nigerian Examples

1. All Progressives Congress (APC) – Formed in 2013 from a merger of opposition parties. It won the presidency in 2015 (Muhammadu Buhari) and 2023 (Bola Tinubu). Its ideology is broadly progressive and social democratic.

2. People's Democratic Party (PDP) – Founded in 1998, it ruled Nigeria from 1999 to 2015. It is the main opposition party, with a focus on social liberalism and economic development. Notable presidents: Olusegun Obasanjo and Goodluck Jonathan.

💡 Fun Fact: Nigeria has over 90 registered political parties (as of 2024), but only a handful (like APC and PDP) have ever won a presidential election. The smallest party once fielded a candidate who won just 1,234 votes nationwide!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define a political party and list two of its major functions.
  2. Question 2: Differentiate between a single-party system and a multi-party system. Which one does Nigeria operate?
  3. Question 3: Name two major political parties in Nigeria and state the year one of them was formed.

Answers

  • Answer 1: A political party is an organized group of people with similar political views that seeks to control government through elections. Functions: (i) Contest elections, (ii) Formulate policies (manifesto).
  • Answer 2: In a single-party system, only one party is allowed to exist; in a multi-party system, multiple parties compete freely. Nigeria operates a multi-party system.
  • Answer 3: APC (formed 2013) and PDP (formed 1998).
