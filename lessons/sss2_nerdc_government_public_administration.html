Public Administration: The Engine of Government

Key Concepts

  • Public Administration refers to the implementation of government policies and the management of public programs.
  • It involves planning, organizing, staffing, directing, coordinating, reporting, and budgeting (POSDCORB) within government agencies.
  • While the Executive makes broad policies, Public Administration ensures those policies are executed efficiently and fairly.
  • It operates at all levels: Federal, State, and Local Government in Nigeria.
  • Core values include accountability, transparency, efficiency, and service to the public.

Nigerian Examples

✅ Example 1: National Identity Management Commission (NIMC)
NIMC administers the National Identification Number (NIN) system. It handles registration, data management, and issuance of IDs — a classic example of public administration delivering a service to millions of Nigerians.

✅ Example 2: Lagos State Waste Management Authority (LAWMA)
LAWMA is responsible for waste collection, disposal, and environmental sanitation across Lagos. It demonstrates how public administration works at the state level to maintain public health and order.

🎉 Fun Fact!

The term "Public Administration" was first used in 1855 by Woodrow Wilson (who later became U.S. President). He is called the "Father of Public Administration" and argued that administration should be separate from politics to run efficiently — an idea that shaped modern governments, including Nigeria's civil service!

Practice Questions

Q1: Define Public Administration and state two of its core values.

Answer: Public Administration is the implementation of government policies and management of public programs. Core values include accountability, transparency, efficiency, and service to the public (any two).

Q2: Give one Nigerian example of a public administration agency and explain its function.

Answer: The National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) administers the National Identification Number (NIN) system, handling registration, data management, and issuance of identity cards to citizens.

Q3: Differentiate between the roles of the Executive and Public Administration in government.

Answer: The Executive makes broad policies and decisions (e.g., passing a new law on education), while Public Administration implements those policies (e.g., building schools, hiring teachers, and managing the education budget).
