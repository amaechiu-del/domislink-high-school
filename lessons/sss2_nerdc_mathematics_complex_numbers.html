Key Concepts

  • Complex Number: A number of the form z = a + bi, where a and b are real numbers, and i = √(-1).
  • Real Part: Re(z) = a (the horizontal coordinate on the complex plane).
  • Imaginary Part: Im(z) = b (the vertical coordinate on the complex plane).
  • Complex Plane (Argand Diagram): A 2D graph where the x-axis represents real numbers and the y-axis represents imaginary numbers.
  • Conjugate: For z = a + bi, the conjugate is z̄ = a – bi (reflects across the real axis).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (Lagos Bus Fare):
A bus fare from Oshodi to Ikeja costs ₦300, but due to traffic, the journey takes 2 hours extra. Represent the total cost and time as a complex number where cost is the real part and extra time is the imaginary part.
Solution: z = 300 + 2i (₦300 real, 2 hours imaginary).
Example 2 (Abuja Temperature & Humidity):
In Abuja, the temperature is 35°C and the humidity index is 15. Write this as a complex number and find its conjugate.
Solution: z = 35 + 15i. Conjugate: z̄ = 35 – 15i.
⭐ Fun Fact!
The symbol i for √(-1) was introduced by Swiss mathematician Leonhard Euler in the 18th century. Complex numbers are essential in electrical engineering — Nigerian engineers use them to design power grids and communication networks!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. If z = 4 – 7i, what are Re(z) and Im(z)?
  2. Plot the complex number w = -3 + 2i on the Argand diagram. What is its conjugate?
  3. A student scores 85 in Mathematics (real) and 20 in Further Maths (imaginary). Express this as a complex number and find its modulus (distance from origin) to 1 decimal place.
✅ Answers:
1. Re(z) = 4, Im(z) = -7
2. Conjugate of w = -3 – 2i (point reflected across real axis)
3. z = 85 + 20i; Modulus = √(85² + 20²) = √(7225 + 400) = √7625 ≈ 87.3
