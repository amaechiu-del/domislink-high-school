Key Concepts

  • Differentiation finds the rate of change of a function at any given point — the slope of the tangent line to the curve.
  • If \( y = x^n \), then \( \frac{dy}{dx} = n x^{n-1} \) (the Power Rule).
  • The derivative \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) tells us how steep the curve is: positive = increasing, negative = decreasing, zero = flat (turning point).
  • Notation: \( f'(x) \) means the same as \( \frac{dy}{dx} \).
  • Differentiation is used in physics (velocity = derivative of displacement), economics (marginal cost), and engineering (optimization).

Nigerian Example 1: Fuel Consumption

The amount of fuel (in litres) used by a car travelling at speed \( v \) km/h is given by \( F(v) = 0.002v^2 + 0.5v + 10 \). Find the rate at which fuel consumption changes when the car is travelling at 60 km/h.

Solution:

\( F'(v) = 0.004v + 0.5 \)
At \( v = 60 \): \( F'(60) = 0.004(60) + 0.5 = 0.24 + 0.5 = 0.74 \) litres per km/h.

Nigerian Example 2: Cassava Yield

A farmer's cassava yield (in tonnes) after \( t \) months is modelled by \( Y(t) = 0.5t^3 - 3t^2 + 8t + 2 \). Determine the rate of change of yield after 4 months.

Solution:

\( Y'(t) = 1.5t^2 - 6t + 8 \)
At \( t = 4 \): \( Y'(4) = 1.5(16) - 6(4) + 8 = 24 - 24 + 8 = 8 \) tonnes per month.

🎉 Fun Fact: The word "derivative" comes from the Latin derivare, meaning "to draw off" — like drawing the slope off a curve! The modern notation \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) was introduced by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in 1675, and it's still used today in every calculus class across Nigeria and the world.

Practice Questions

  1. Differentiate \( y = 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 7x - 5 \).
  2. Find the gradient of the curve \( y = x^2 + 3x \) at the point where \( x = 2 \).
  3. If \( f(x) = 5x^4 - 3x^2 \), find \( f'(1) \).

Answers

  1. \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 12x^2 - 4x + 7 \)
  2. \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 2x + 3 \); at \( x=2 \), gradient = \( 2(2) + 3 = 7 \)
  3. \( f'(x) = 20x^3 - 6x \); \( f'(1) = 20(1) - 6(1) = 14 \)
