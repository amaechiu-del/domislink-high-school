What is Integration?

Integration is the reverse process of differentiation. While differentiation finds the rate of change, integration finds the accumulated total — often used to calculate the area under a curve between two points.

Key Concepts:

  • Indefinite Integral: ∫ f(x) dx = F(x) + C, where C is the constant of integration
  • Definite Integral: ∫ab f(x) dx = F(b) − F(a) — gives a numerical value (area)
  • Power Rule: ∫ xn dx = (xn+1)/(n+1) + C, for n ≠ −1
  • Integration is used in physics, economics, engineering, and probability

Nigerian Examples:

Example 1 — Oil Production: An oil company in Port Harcourt extracts oil at a rate given by R(t) = 50 + 10t barrels per day (t in days). Find the total oil extracted from day 2 to day 5.

Solution: ∫25 (50 + 10t) dt = [50t + 5t²]25 = (250 + 125) − (100 + 20) = 375 − 120 = 255 barrels

Example 2 — Cassava Yield: The growth rate of cassava plants in Oyo State is f(x) = 3x² + 2x kg per hectare (x = months). Find the total yield between month 1 and month 4.

Solution: ∫14 (3x² + 2x) dx = [x³ + x²]14 = (64 + 16) − (1 + 1) = 80 − 2 = 78 kg

🌟 Fun Fact: The integral symbol ∫ was introduced by German mathematician Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in the 17th century. It is derived from the Latin word summa (meaning "sum") — the long S shape represents summation of infinitely many small pieces!

Practice Questions:

  1. Evaluate ∫ (4x³ − 6x² + 5) dx
  2. Find the area under the curve y = 2x + 3 from x = 1 to x = 4
  3. A factory in Lagos produces goods at a rate P(t) = 100 + 20t − t² units per hour. How many units are produced from t = 0 to t = 6 hours?

Answers:

  1. x⁴ − 2x³ + 5x + C
  2. ∫14 (2x+3) dx = [x²+3x]14 = (16+12) − (1+3) = 28 − 4 = 24 square units
  3. ∫06 (100+20t−t²) dt = [100t+10t²−(t³/3)]06 = (600+360−72) − 0 = 888 units
