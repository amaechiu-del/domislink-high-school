What is a Matrix?

A matrix is a rectangular arrangement of numbers, symbols, or expressions, arranged in rows and columns. Matrices are used to solve systems of equations, transform shapes in computer graphics, and manage data in economics and engineering.

Key Features of Matrices

  • Order (or dimension): Written as rows × columns. For example, a 2×3 matrix has 2 rows and 3 columns.
  • Elements: Each number inside a matrix is called an element, identified by its row and column position (e.g., a₂₃ means row 2, column 3).
  • Square matrix: Same number of rows and columns (e.g., 3×3).
  • Zero matrix: All elements are zero.
  • Identity matrix: A square matrix with 1s on the main diagonal and 0s elsewhere.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Student Scores
The matrix below shows scores of three SSS2 students (Chidi, Amina, and Emeka) in Mathematics, English, and Physics.

          Math  Eng  Phy
  Chidi  [ 85   72   90 ]
  Amina  [ 78   88   84 ]
  Emeka  [ 92   76   81 ]

This is a 3×3 square matrix. The element at row 2, column 1 (Amina’s Math score) is 78.

Example 2: Market Prices
A trader in Lagos records prices (in ₦) of yam, rice, and beans at two different markets (Mile 12 and Ketu).

          Yam  Rice  Beans
  Mile12  [ 500  800   400 ]
  Ketu    [ 550  750   450 ]

This is a 2×3 matrix. The element at row 1, column 2 (rice price at Mile 12) is 800.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "matrix" comes from the Latin word for "womb" — because it was seen as a place where numbers are generated! In Nigeria, matrices are used by the National Bureau of Statistics to analyze census data and economic trends.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Given matrix \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 7 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 9 \end{bmatrix} \), what is its order? Identify the element \( b_{12} \).

    Answer

    Order = 2×3 (2 rows, 3 columns). \( b_{12} \) = 7 (row 1, column 2).

  2. Question 2: A 3×2 matrix has how many elements in total?

    Answer

    3 rows × 2 columns = 6 elements.

  3. Question 3: Write down the 2×2 identity matrix.

    Answer

    \( I_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)
