Key Concepts in Electric Circuits

  • Electric Current (I): The flow of electric charge through a conductor, measured in amperes (A).
  • Electromotive Force (E): The energy provided by a battery or source per unit charge, measured in volts (V).
  • Resistance (R): The opposition to current flow in a material, measured in ohms (Ω).
  • Ohm's Law: V = I × R — Voltage equals current multiplied by resistance.
  • Series Circuit: Components connected end-to-end; same current flows through all.
  • Parallel Circuit: Components connected across the same voltage; current divides.

Nigerian Examples

  • Example 1 (Lagos Power Supply): A 240V household supply in Surulere powers a 60W bulb. Using P = IV, the current drawn is I = 60/240 = 0.25A. If the bulb has resistance R = V/I = 240/0.25 = 960Ω, this explains why bulbs glow dimly during low voltage (brownouts).
  • Example 2 (PHCN Meter Reading): In Abuja, a prepaid meter shows 5A current flowing through a 220V line for 2 hours. Energy consumed = V × I × t = 220 × 5 × 2 = 2200 Wh = 2.2 kWh. At ₦24/kWh (Band A tariff), cost = 2.2 × 24 = ₦52.80.
⚡ Fun Fact: Nigeria's first hydroelectric dam, Kainji Dam (completed 1968), generates 760 MW of electricity — enough to power over 500,000 homes! The dam uses the flow of River Niger to spin turbines, converting kinetic energy into electrical energy.

Practice Questions

  1. Q1: A current of 3A flows through a resistor of 20Ω. Calculate the voltage across the resistor.
  2. Q2: In a series circuit with two bulbs of resistance 10Ω and 15Ω connected to a 12V battery, find the total current in the circuit.
  3. Q3: A Nigerian household uses a 2000W electric kettle on a 220V supply for 30 minutes. How much energy is consumed in kWh?
Answers:
A1: V = I × R = 3A × 20Ω = 60V
A2: Total R = 10Ω + 15Ω = 25Ω; I = V/R = 12V / 25Ω = 0.48A
A3: Energy = Power × Time = 2000W × 0.5h = 1000 Wh = 1.0 kWh
