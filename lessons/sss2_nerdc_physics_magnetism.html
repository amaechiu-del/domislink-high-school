Magnetism: The Invisible Force

Key Concepts

  • Magnetic poles: Every magnet has a North (N) and South (S) pole — opposite poles attract, like poles repel.
  • Magnetic field lines: Invisible lines that flow from N to S outside the magnet and from S to N inside. They never cross.
  • Ferromagnetic materials: Iron, cobalt, and nickel are strongly attracted to magnets. Examples include steel and magnetite.
  • Temporary vs. permanent magnets: Soft iron can be magnetised temporarily, while steel retains magnetism for longer.
  • Earth's magnetism: The Earth behaves like a giant magnet with its magnetic south pole near the geographic north pole.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Loudspeakers in Lagos markets: Many market traders use loudspeakers which contain a permanent magnet and a coil of wire. When electric current flows, the coil moves, producing sound — a practical application of electromagnetism common in places like Balogun Market.

2. Magnetic separation in mining: In Jos, Plateau State, iron ore is separated from other minerals using powerful magnets. This process helps extract valuable metals from crushed rock.

⭐ Fun Fact

Did you know? The strongest magnets in the world are neodymium magnets, which are about 10 times stronger than ordinary fridge magnets. They are used in MRI machines in Nigerian hospitals like the Lagos University Teaching Hospital (LUTH) to create detailed images of the inside of the body!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A student brings a magnet near a pile of iron filings. Some filings stick to the ends of the magnet but not the middle. Explain why.
  2. Question 2: If you break a bar magnet into two pieces, how many poles will each piece have? Explain.
  3. Question 3: A compass needle points north in Lagos. What does this tell you about the Earth's magnetic field?

✅ Answers

Answer 1: The magnetic force is strongest at the poles (ends) of the magnet and weakest at the centre (neutral region). Iron filings are attracted only where the magnetic field is strong enough.

Answer 2: Each piece will have both a north and a south pole. Magnetic poles always occur in pairs — it is impossible to isolate a single pole (this is called the law of poles).

Answer 3: The Earth has a magnetic field that behaves as if a giant bar magnet is inside it. The compass needle aligns with this field, with its north pole pointing toward the Earth's magnetic south pole (near the geographic north).
