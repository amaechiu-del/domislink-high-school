Key Concepts in Modern Physics

  • Quantum Theory: Energy exists in discrete packets called quanta (photons for light). An electron can only occupy specific energy levels in an atom.
  • Wave-Particle Duality: Light behaves as both a wave (interference) and a particle (photoelectric effect).
  • Photoelectric Effect: When light of sufficient frequency hits a metal surface, electrons are ejected. Kinetic energy of ejected electrons = hf - φ (work function).
  • Bohr Model: Electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed energy levels. Transition between levels emits or absorbs photons of specific frequencies.
  • Radioactive Decay: Unstable nuclei emit alpha, beta, or gamma radiation to become stable. Half-life is the time for half the nuclei to decay.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Cancer Treatment in Lagos: Teaching hospitals in Lagos use gamma radiation from cobalt-60 to treat cancer patients. The gamma rays target and destroy malignant cells while sparing healthy tissue, applying the principle of radioactive decay.
  2. Solar Power in Kano: Solar panels installed in Kano State use the photoelectric effect to convert sunlight into electricity. Photons from the sun knock electrons loose in silicon cells, generating current to power homes and schools.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "quantum" comes from the Latin quantus meaning "how much." In Nigeria, quantum physics helps design better LED bulbs used in many homes—they convert electricity into light with minimal heat, thanks to quantum mechanics of electron-hole recombination!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A metal has a work function of 2.0 eV. Light of frequency 6.0 × 1014 Hz shines on it. (Planck's constant h = 4.14 × 10-15 eV·s). Calculate the maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons.
  2. Question 2: A radioactive sample has a half-life of 8 days. If you start with 80 g of the sample, how much remains after 24 days?
  3. Question 3: An electron in a hydrogen atom drops from n=3 to n=2, emitting a photon of wavelength 656 nm. Calculate the energy of this photon in joules (h = 6.63 × 10-34 J·s, c = 3.0 × 108 m/s).

Answers

  1. Answer 1: E = hf = (4.14×10-15)(6.0×1014) = 2.484 eV. Kmax = E - φ = 2.484 - 2.0 = 0.484 eV
  2. Answer 2: 24 days = 3 half-lives. Remaining = 80 g × (½)^3 = 80 × ⅛ = 10 g
  3. Answer 3: E = hc/λ = (6.63×10-34)(3.0×108) / (656×10-9) = 1.989×10-25 / 6.56×10-7 = 3.03 × 10-19 J
