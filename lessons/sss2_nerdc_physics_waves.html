Key Concepts of Waves

  • Wave: A disturbance that transfers energy from one point to another without transferring matter.
  • Crest: The highest point of a wave above the rest position.
  • Trough: The lowest point of a wave below the rest position.
  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from rest position — determines wave energy.
  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between two successive crests or troughs.
  • Frequency (f): Number of complete waves passing a point per second (measured in Hertz, Hz).
  • Wave speed (v): v = f × λ

Nigerian Examples

  • Example 1 (Sound wave): When a talking drum (gangan) is played in a Yoruba ceremony, the skin of the drum vibrates, creating sound waves that travel through the air to listeners. Tightening the drum skin increases frequency (higher pitch).
  • Example 2 (Water wave): At Bar Beach, Lagos, ocean waves continuously crash onto the shore. These are mechanical waves where water particles move in circular orbits, transferring energy from the Atlantic Ocean to the coast.
⚡ Fun Fact: The Kainji Dam in Niger State uses the energy of flowing water (wave motion) to generate electricity. The dam’s turbines convert the kinetic energy of water waves into electrical energy — enough to power millions of homes!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A wave has a frequency of 5 Hz and a wavelength of 2 m. Calculate the wave speed.
    Answer: v = f × λ = 5 × 2 = 10 m/s
  2. Question 2: In the SVG diagram above, what is the distance between two consecutive crests called?
    Answer: Wavelength (λ)
  3. Question 3: A fisherman at Makoko floating school notices 10 wave crests pass his boat in 20 seconds. What is the frequency of the waves?
    Answer: f = number of waves / time = 10 / 20 = 0.5 Hz
