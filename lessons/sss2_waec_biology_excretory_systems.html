Excretory Systems – SSS2 Biology (WAEC Syllabus)

The excretory system is responsible for removing metabolic waste products from the body. In humans, this involves the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra. The process of osmoregulation and waste removal is vital for homeostasis.

Key Functions & Organs

  • Kidneys: Filter blood to remove urea, excess salts, and water. They produce urine.
  • Ureters: Muscular tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder using peristalsis.
  • Urinary Bladder: A hollow, muscular sac that stores urine until excretion.
  • Urethra: The tube through which urine is expelled from the body.
  • Nephrons: The functional units of the kidney where filtration, reabsorption, and secretion occur.
  • Homeostasis: The excretory system helps regulate blood pH, water balance, and removes toxic nitrogenous wastes.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Example 1: In rural farming communities in northern Nigeria, Ziziphus mauritiana (local name: Magarya) leaves are sometimes used in traditional remedies for urinary tract infections, demonstrating local knowledge of the excretory system.
  2. Example 2: The high rate of kidney disease in parts of southern Nigeria is linked to the consumption of locally brewed alcoholic drinks and the use of herbal concoctions containing nephrotoxic substances, highlighting the importance of kidney health education.

Fun Fact

The kidneys filter about 180 litres of blood every day, but only about 1.5 litres of urine is produced. The rest is reabsorbed! Without this process, you would need to drink 180 litres of water daily just to survive.

Practice Questions

  1. Question: Name the two major waste products removed by the human kidney and state one function of the urinary bladder.
    Show Answer

    Answer: Urea and excess water/salts. The urinary bladder stores urine temporarily until it is expelled from the body.

  2. Question: Explain how the nephron helps in the formation of urine. (Hint: mention filtration, reabsorption, and secretion).
    Show Answer

    Answer: Blood enters the glomerulus where filtration occurs (water, urea, glucose, salts pass into Bowman's capsule). Then, useful substances like glucose and most water are reabsorbed in the tubules. Finally, additional wastes (e.g., hydrogen ions) are secreted into the tubule, producing urine.

  3. Question: Why is the excretory system important for homeostasis in the human body? Give one Nigerian context example.
    Show Answer

    Answer: It regulates water and salt balance, removes toxic urea, and maintains blood pH. For example, in hot regions of northern Nigeria, the kidneys conserve water by producing more concentrated urine, preventing dehydration.
