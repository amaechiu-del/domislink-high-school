Nervous System (SSS2 Biology – WAEC Syllabus)

Key Points on the Nervous System

  • Neuron: The basic functional unit of the nervous system, specialized for transmitting nerve impulses.
  • Dendrites: Short, branched extensions that receive signals from other neurons or sensory receptors.
  • Cell Body (Soma): Contains the nucleus and organelles; integrates incoming signals.
  • Axon: Long fiber that conducts impulses away from the cell body toward other neurons or effectors.
  • Myelin Sheath: Fatty insulating layer (produced by Schwann cells) that speeds up impulse conduction (saltatory conduction).
  • Synapse: Junction between two neurons where neurotransmitters (e.g., acetylcholine) relay signals across the synaptic cleft.
  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Comprises the brain and spinal cord; processes and coordinates information.
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Nerves outside the CNS; connects the CNS to limbs and organs.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Yam Farming: When a Nigerian farmer touches a hot yam tuber while harvesting, sensory neurons in the skin send an impulse to the spinal cord (reflex arc). The spinal cord immediately signals motor neurons to pull the hand away—this happens before the brain even registers pain, protecting the farmer from burns.

Example 2 – Malaria Fever: In a malaria patient, the hypothalamus (part of the brain) detects toxins from Plasmodium parasites. It triggers the autonomic nervous system to raise body temperature (fever), causing shivering and sweating. This is the body's way of fighting the infection, common in many Nigerian households.

🧠 Fun Fact

The human brain contains about 86 billion neurons, and each neuron can form up to 10,000 connections with other neurons. That means the brain has more possible connections than there are stars in the Milky Way galaxy! In Nigeria, the saying "the brain is like a computer" is true—except it's far more powerful.

✏️ Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Name the three main parts of a neuron and state the function of each.
  2. Question 2: Explain how the myelin sheath increases the speed of nerve impulse conduction.
  3. Question 3: Describe the path of a nerve impulse in a simple reflex arc (e.g., touching a hot object).

📝 Answers

Answer 1:

  • Dendrites – receive signals from other neurons or sensory receptors.
  • Cell body (soma) – contains the nucleus and integrates incoming signals.
  • Axon – conducts nerve impulses away from the cell body to other neurons, muscles, or glands.

Answer 2: The myelin sheath acts as an electrical insulator, preventing leakage of the impulse. The impulse "jumps" from one node of Ranvier (gaps in the myelin) to the next—this is called saltatory conduction, which speeds up transmission compared to unmyelinated axons.

Answer 3: A simple reflex arc follows this path:
1. Receptor (skin) detects stimulus (heat) →
2. Sensory neuron carries impulse to spinal cord →
3. Relay neuron (in spinal cord) processes signal →
4. Motor neuron carries impulse to effector (muscle) →
5. Muscle contracts, pulling hand away. This bypasses the brain for a faster response.
