Environmental Chemistry – SSS2 (WAEC Syllabus)

  • Air Pollution: Release of harmful gases (CO₂, SO₂, NOₓ) from factories, vehicles, and burning of fossil fuels causes acid rain and respiratory problems.
  • Water Pollution: Industrial effluents, sewage, and agricultural runoff (fertilizers, pesticides) contaminate rivers and lakes, killing aquatic life.
  • Land/Soil Pollution: Dumping of non-biodegradable wastes (plastics, e-waste) and oil spills degrade soil fertility and harm ecosystems.
  • Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming: Increased CO₂ and methane trap heat, leading to rising temperatures, melting ice caps, and climate change.
  • Biodegradable vs. Non-biodegradable: Organic matter (food scraps) decomposes naturally; plastics and metals persist for hundreds of years.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Oil Spills in the Niger Delta: Frequent spills from oil pipelines (e.g., Shell, Chevron) have polluted farmlands, rivers, and creeks, destroying fisheries and causing health issues for local communities.

2. E-waste Dumping in Lagos (Alaba Market): Huge quantities of discarded electronics (computers, phones) are burned or dismantled, releasing toxic lead and mercury into the soil and air, affecting traders and residents.

💡 Fun Fact!

Did you know? The Ogoni region in Nigeria has one of the highest levels of soil hydrocarbon contamination in the world — some areas have over 10,000 mg/kg of crude oil, making the land unfit for farming for decades.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. State two major sources of air pollution in Nigerian cities.
  2. Explain how acid rain is formed from factory emissions.
  3. Give one reason why plastic waste is a serious environmental problem in Nigeria.

✅ Answers

  1. Vehicle exhaust fumes and burning of fossil fuels (e.g., generators, industrial boilers).
  2. Sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) from factories react with water vapour in the atmosphere to form sulfuric and nitric acids, which fall as acid rain.
  3. Plastics are non-biodegradable — they clog drainage systems, cause flooding, and when burned release toxic fumes (dioxins).
