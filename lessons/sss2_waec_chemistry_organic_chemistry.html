Organic Chemistry – SSS2 (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts in Organic Chemistry

  • Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds, including their structure, properties, and reactions.
  • Carbon forms covalent bonds with other atoms, especially hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, and halogens.
  • Hydrocarbons are compounds made of only carbon and hydrogen (e.g., alkanes, alkenes, alkynes).
  • Functional groups (e.g., –OH, –COOH, –NH₂) determine the chemical behavior of organic molecules.
  • Homologous series are families of organic compounds with similar chemical properties and a general formula (e.g., alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂).
  • Isomerism occurs when compounds have the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Palm oil contains organic compounds called triglycerides (esters of glycerol and fatty acids). These are used in cooking and soap making in Nigeria.

Example 2: Cassava (Manihot esculenta) contains starch, a polysaccharide made of glucose units. Starch is a key organic compound in Nigerian diets and industrial production of ethanol.

🎉 Fun Fact!

Did you know? The longest known organic molecule is DNA, which can contain billions of atoms! In Nigeria, organic chemistry is vital for developing local pharmaceuticals from plants like neem and bitter leaf.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What is the general formula for the alkane homologous series? Give an example of an alkane with 5 carbon atoms.
  2. Question 2: Name the functional group present in ethanol (C₂H₅OH) and state one use of ethanol in Nigeria.
  3. Question 3: Draw the structural formula of propene (C₃H₆) and identify the type of bond between the first two carbon atoms.

🔑 Answers

  1. Answer 1: General formula for alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂. Example: Pentane (C₅H₁₂).
  2. Answer 2: Functional group: hydroxyl group (–OH). Uses in Nigeria: as a solvent in pharmaceuticals, fuel for stoves, or in the production of alcoholic beverages.
  3. Answer 3: Propene structure: H₂C=CH–CH₃. The bond between the first two carbon atoms is a double bond (one sigma and one pi bond).
