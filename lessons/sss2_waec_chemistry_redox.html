🔬 Key Concepts: Redox Reactions

  • Redox = Reduction + Oxidation (two simultaneous processes).
  • Oxidation is the loss of electrons (or gain of oxygen).
  • Reduction is the gain of electrons (or loss of oxygen).
  • Use the mnemonic: OIL RIG – Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain.
  • An oxidising agent causes oxidation (gets reduced itself). A reducing agent causes reduction (gets oxidised itself).
  • In the diagram above: Mg is the reducing agent (loses e⁻), Cu²⁺ is the oxidising agent (gains e⁻).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Rusting of Iron in Lagos
Iron (Fe) reacts with oxygen and water to form hydrated iron(III) oxide (rust). Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻ (oxidation) — iron loses electrons. O₂ + 2H₂O + 4e⁻ → 4OH⁻ (reduction) — oxygen gains electrons. Rusting is accelerated by the humid coastal climate of Lagos.
Example 2: Bleaching of Starch by Chlorine (Water Treatment in Kaduna)
Chlorine (Cl₂) is used to disinfect water. It oxidises iodide ions (I⁻) to iodine (I₂), which turns starch blue-black. Cl₂ + 2e⁻ → 2Cl⁻ (reduction of chlorine). 2I⁻ → I₂ + 2e⁻ (oxidation of iodide). This test is used in Kaduna’s water treatment plants to check for residual chlorine.
💡
Fun Fact: The word “redox” was coined in 1928. In the human body, redox reactions power cellular respiration — the process that gives you energy to run in a football match in Surulere!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Q1: In the reaction Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu, identify the substance that is reduced and the substance that is oxidised.
  2. Q2: Give the oxidation number of chlorine in Cl₂ (g) and in Cl⁻ (aq). Which change represents reduction?
  3. Q3: Why is the rusting of iron considered a redox reaction? Name the reducing agent and the oxidising agent.
✅ Answers:
A1: Cu²⁺ is reduced (gains 2e⁻ → Cu). Zn is oxidised (loses 2e⁻ → Zn²⁺).
A2: In Cl₂, oxidation number = 0. In Cl⁻, oxidation number = -1. Going from 0 to -1 is a gain of electrons → reduction.
A3: Iron loses electrons (Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻) = oxidation. Oxygen gains electrons (O₂ + 4e⁻ → 2O²⁻) = reduction. Reducing agent = Fe (iron). Oxidising agent = O₂ (oxygen).
