Economic Growth

Key Points on Economic Growth

  • Definition: Economic growth is the sustained increase in a country's production of goods and services over time, measured by the rise in real Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
  • Measurement: It is calculated as the percentage increase in real GDP per capita, accounting for population changes.
  • Factors: Key drivers include natural resources, human capital, capital formation, technological innovation, and institutional stability.
  • Indicators: Rising GDP, improved infrastructure, higher employment rates, and increased industrial output signal growth.
  • Importance: Growth leads to higher living standards, poverty reduction, and increased government revenue for public services.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Agricultural Sector Growth (2019-2022): Nigeria's agricultural sector grew by 2.3% annually due to government policies like the Anchor Borrowers' Programme, which boosted rice and cassava production. States like Kebbi and Ogun saw increased yields, contributing to national GDP.

2. Telecom Boom (2001-2021): The liberalization of Nigeria's telecommunications sector led to massive growth. From less than 1 million lines in 2001, mobile subscriptions rose to over 222 million by 2021, driving GDP growth through services, employment, and innovation.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know? Nigeria's economy grew by over 7% annually between 2000 and 2014, making it one of the fastest-growing economies in Africa during that period! This was largely driven by oil revenues and reforms in banking and telecommunications.

📝 Answers

Answer 1: Economic growth is the sustained increase in a country's real GDP over time. Two factors: (i) Investment in human capital (education/training) improves productivity. (ii) Technological innovation (e.g., mobile banking) boosts efficiency across sectors.

Answer 2: The curve slopes upward because real GDP increases over time due to factors like capital accumulation, technology, and labor growth. The distance Y₁ to Y₄ represents the total increase in real GDP over the period shown.

Answer 3: Economic growth is a quantitative increase in output (e.g., Nigeria's 7% GDP growth in 2014). Economic development is qualitative improvement in living standards (e.g., Nigeria's increase in literacy rate from 57% in 2000 to 77% in 2021). Growth can occur without development if benefits are not widely shared.
