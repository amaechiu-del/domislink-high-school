Cloze Test Mastery (SSS2 WAEC Focus)

What is a Cloze Test?

  • A passage with missing words (gaps) that you must fill using context clues.
  • Tests your vocabulary, grammar, and reading comprehension.
  • Each gap usually has four options (A, B, C, D) — choose the best fit.
  • Read the entire sentence before and after the gap to understand meaning.
  • Look for signal words (e.g., however, because, although) that show relationships.
  • Check tense, plural/singular, and part of speech needed.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: "The durbar festival in Kano attracts many ______ from across the country."
Options: A. spectators, B. textbooks, C. bicycles, D. oranges
Answer: A (spectators – people who watch the event)

Example 2: "Despite the heavy harmattan, the students were ______ in their studies."
Options: A. lazy, B. diligent, C. noisy, D. tired
Answer: B (diligent – hardworking, despite the weather)

🎉 Fun Fact!

The word "cloze" comes from "closure" – the psychological tendency to complete a pattern. Your brain naturally wants to fill gaps!

📝 Practice Questions (WAEC Style)

Read the passage and choose the best option for each gap.

"The new principal was known for his ______ (1) discipline. He believed that a well-ordered school leads to academic success. However, some students found his methods too ______ (2)."

Question 1: A. strict   B. weak   C. careless   D. funny
Question 2: A. lenient   B. harsh   C. playful   D. simple

"During the Sallah celebration, families ______ (3) together to share meals and exchange gifts."

Question 3: A. scatter   B. gather   C. fight   D. sleep

✅ Answers:

1. A (strict) – discipline that is firm and consistent.
2. B (harsh) – opposite of lenient; too severe.
3. B (gather) – come together as a family.
