Letter Writing (SSS2 – WAEC Syllabus)

Key Points for WAEC Letter Writing

  • Formal Letters – Use for official requests, complaints, or applications. Address: "Dear Sir/Madam," and end with "Yours faithfully,"
  • Informal Letters – Use for friends/family. Address: "Dear [Name]," and end with "Yours sincerely," or "Your friend,"
  • Address & Date – Always write your address and the date in the top right corner. Recipient’s address goes on the left.
  • Paragraphing – Use at least three paragraphs: introduction, main body, and conclusion.
  • Tone & Clarity – Be polite, clear, and avoid slang. Formal letters require a respectful tone.
  • Signature – Sign your full name below the closing.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Formal Letter (Complaint to School Principal)

12, Unity Road,
Ikeja, Lagos.
15th March 2025.

The Principal,
St. John’s Secondary School,
Ikeja, Lagos.

Dear Sir,

Complaint About Poor Sanitary Conditions

I write to respectfully draw your attention to the poor state of our school toilets. The facilities are often dirty and lack water. This poses a health risk to students. I kindly request that urgent repairs and regular cleaning be arranged. Thank you for your prompt attention.

Yours faithfully,
Chidi Okonkwo
SS2 Student

Example 2 – Informal Letter (To a Cousin)

House 7, Bida Road,
Minna, Niger State.
10th April 2025.

Dear Aisha,

I hope this letter meets you well. I was so happy to hear that you passed your JAMB exam with flying colours! We must celebrate when you visit next month. I am currently busy preparing for my own WAEC exams, but I make time for football every Saturday. Please send me your new phone number so we can chat more. I miss you!

Your loving cousin,
Fatima

🎉 Fun Fact: Did you know that the longest letter ever written was over 1.4 million words? It was written by a Nigerian author, Chinua Achebe, in his draft of Things Fall Apart – though he later edited it down! In WAEC, however, your letter should be between 250–400 words.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Write a formal letter to the Chairman of your Local Government Area complaining about the lack of electricity in your community. Include your address, date, and a clear body.
  2. Question 2: Write an informal letter to your best friend inviting him/her to your upcoming birthday party. Mention the date, time, and venue.
  3. Question 3: What are the differences between a formal and an informal letter? List at least three.

Answers

Answer 1 (Sample): Address: 5, Market Street, Abeokuta. Date: 20th April 2025. Recipient: The Chairman, Abeokuta North LGA. Salutation: Dear Sir, Body: Complain about darkness, request action. Closing: Yours faithfully, Your Name.

Answer 2 (Sample): Address: 22, Peace Avenue, Enugu. Date: 1st May 2025. Salutation: Dear Chidi, Body: Invite to party on 15th May at 3pm, No. 10 Garden Road. Closing: Your friend, Emeka.

Answer 3: (i) Formal letters use "Dear Sir/Madam" and "Yours faithfully"; informal use "Dear [Name]" and "Yours sincerely". (ii) Formal letters have a subject line; informal do not. (iii) Formal letters avoid slang and contractions; informal letters can use them.
