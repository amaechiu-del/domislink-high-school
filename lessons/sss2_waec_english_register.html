Understanding Register in English (SSS2 WAEC Syllabus)

Key Points about Register

  • Register refers to the variety of language used in a particular social setting, profession, or field of activity.
  • It is determined by who is speaking, to whom, about what, and where.
  • Different registers have specialized vocabulary, grammar, and tone – e.g., legal, medical, religious, journalistic.
  • Register is not the same as dialect or accent; it is about context-appropriate language.
  • In formal registers (e.g., academic writing), contractions and slang are avoided; in informal registers, they are common.
  • Mastering register helps you communicate effectively and appropriately in different situations.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Courtroom Register (Legal):
"My Lord, I humbly submit that the defendant is not guilty of the charge of armed robbery as stated in Count One of the information."
(Notice the formal address "My Lord", legal terms like "defendant", "charge", "information".)

Example 2 – Hospital Setting (Medical):
"Doctor, the patient presented with acute malaria, exhibiting pyrexia and severe cephalalgia. I have prescribed an antimalarial regimen."
(Notice "pyrexia" for fever, "cephalalgia" for headache, "regimen" for treatment plan.)

🎉 Fun Fact!

In Nigeria, the Pidgin English register is widely used in markets and informal conversations, but in a WAEC English exam, you must switch to the formal register – no "How far?" or "Wahala" allowed!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Identify the register in this sentence: "The judge adjourned the case sine die."
    Answer: Legal register (sine die = without a fixed date).
  2. Question 2: Rewrite this informal statement in a formal medical register: "The patient has a bad headache and high fever."
    Answer: "The patient presents with severe cephalalgia and pyrexia."
  3. Question 3: What register would a journalist use in a newspaper report about a political rally?
    Answer: Journalistic register (formal, objective, with terms like "allegedly", "according to sources", "the gathering").
