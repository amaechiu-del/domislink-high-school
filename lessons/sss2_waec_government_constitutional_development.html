Constitutional Development in Nigeria (SSS2 - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Features of Constitutional Development

  • 1922 Clifford Constitution: Introduced elective principle (Nigerian Legislative Council) but limited franchise to Lagos and Calabar.
  • 1946 Richards Constitution: Created regional councils (North, West, East) to promote unity, but was criticized for lack of consultation.
  • 1951 Macpherson Constitution: Established a central legislature and regional houses of assembly; first to allow Nigerian ministers.
  • 1954 Lyttleton Constitution: Introduced federalism with three regions, each with its own premier and cabinet.
  • 1960 Independence Constitution: Made Nigeria a sovereign state with a parliamentary system and a Governor-General representing the Queen.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The 1954 Lyttleton Constitution established Nigeria as a federation, allowing the Northern, Western, and Eastern regions to control their own budgets and civil services. This laid the foundation for the current federal structure.

Example 2: The 1960 Independence Constitution retained the Queen as Head of State but introduced a Prime Minister (Sir Abubakar Tafawa Balewa) as head of government, reflecting Nigeria's transition from colonial rule to self-governance.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The 1951 Macpherson Constitution was the first to be drafted after widespread consultation with Nigerians, including village meetings. However, it was rejected by many because the central government still had too much power!

Practice Questions

  1. Which constitution introduced the elective principle in Nigeria?
    Answer: The 1922 Clifford Constitution.
  2. Name one key feature of the 1954 Lyttleton Constitution.
    Answer: It established federalism with three regions (North, West, East) and gave them self-government.
  3. What was a major criticism of the 1946 Richards Constitution?
    Answer: It was imposed without consulting Nigerians, leading to widespread opposition.
