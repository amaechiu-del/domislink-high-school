Foreign Policy: A Guide for SSS2 Government Students

Definition: Foreign policy is the set of principles, strategies, and actions a sovereign state uses to guide its interactions with other states and international actors. It aims to protect national interests, security, and economic prosperity.

Key Components of Foreign Policy

  • National Interest: The primary goal—protecting territorial integrity, economic welfare, and cultural values.
  • Diplomacy: The peaceful conduct of relations through negotiation, treaties, and international organizations.
  • Economic Cooperation: Trade agreements, foreign aid, and investment partnerships (e.g., OPEC, ECOWAS).
  • Security & Defense: Military alliances, peacekeeping, and counter-terrorism efforts.
  • Sovereignty: The right to self-determination and non-interference in internal affairs.

Nigerian Examples

  • Example 1: Nigeria’s Role in ECOWAS — Nigeria has used foreign policy to promote regional stability in West Africa. For instance, Nigeria led ECOMOG (Economic Community of West African States Monitoring Group) peacekeeping missions in Liberia and Sierra Leone in the 1990s to restore order and protect democracy.
  • Example 2: Nigeria’s Afrocentric Policy — Under President Olusegun Obasanjo (1999–2007), Nigeria pursued a foreign policy centered on Africa. This included debt relief negotiations with the Paris Club and championing the New Partnership for Africa’s Development (NEPAD) to boost continental integration.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know? Nigeria’s first Prime Minister, Sir Abubakar Tafawa Balewa, once famously said, “Nigeria has no enemies.” This phrase became the cornerstone of Nigeria’s early foreign policy of “friendship with all nations.”

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define foreign policy and state two of its main objectives for a sovereign state.
  2. Question 2: Explain how Nigeria’s participation in ECOWAS reflects its foreign policy objectives.
  3. Question 3: What is the significance of the Afrocentric approach in Nigeria’s foreign policy under President Obasanjo?

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Foreign policy is a state’s strategy for dealing with other countries. Two main objectives are (a) protecting national security and territorial integrity, and (b) promoting economic prosperity through trade and investment.
  2. Answer 2: Nigeria’s leadership in ECOWAS (e.g., ECOMOG peacekeeping in Liberia) shows its commitment to regional stability, collective security, and peaceful conflict resolution—core foreign policy goals.
  3. Answer 3: The Afrocentric approach prioritized African unity, economic development (e.g., NEPAD), and debt relief. It strengthened Nigeria’s influence in Africa and promoted continental self-reliance.
