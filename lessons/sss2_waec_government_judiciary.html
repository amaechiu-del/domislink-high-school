Judiciary in Government (SSS2 WAEC Syllabus)

Key Functions of the Judiciary

  • Interpretation of Laws: Courts explain the meaning of statutes and the constitution when disputes arise.
  • Adjudication: Judges settle conflicts between individuals, groups, or the state using legal principles.
  • Constitutional Review: The judiciary can declare laws or executive actions unconstitutional (judicial review).
  • Protection of Rights: Courts safeguard citizens' fundamental human rights through enforcement of legal remedies.
  • Judicial Precedent: Higher court decisions bind lower courts, ensuring consistency in legal interpretation.

🇳🇬 Example 1: Supreme Court & Gubernatorial Disputes

In Ladoja v. INEC (2007), the Supreme Court asserted its authority to interpret the constitution regarding the tenure of a governor, reinforcing the rule of law in Nigeria's federal system.

🇳🇬 Example 2: Fundamental Rights Enforcement

The High Court in Registered Trustees of SERAP v. Nigeria (2014) declared that the failure to investigate human rights abuses violated the constitution, showing the judiciary's role in protecting citizens.

⚡ Fun Fact: Nigeria's Supreme Court has the final say on all civil and criminal matters. Its decisions are binding on all lower courts, and no appeal lies from its judgments—making it the highest judicial authority in the land!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What is the primary role of the judiciary in a democratic government?
  2. Name two ways the Nigerian judiciary ensures its independence from the executive.
  3. Explain the concept of "judicial review" and give one example from Nigeria.

✅ Answers

1. The judiciary interprets laws, settles disputes, and ensures that the actions of the executive and legislature comply with the constitution.

2. (i) Security of tenure – judges cannot be removed arbitrarily. (ii) Financial autonomy – the judiciary controls its budget to prevent executive influence.

3. Judicial review is the power of courts to declare laws or executive actions unconstitutional. Example: In Ladoja v. INEC, the Supreme Court reviewed and nullified an improper removal of a governor.
