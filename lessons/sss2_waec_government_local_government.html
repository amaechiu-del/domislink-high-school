Local Government: The Third Tier of Government in Nigeria

Key Features of Local Government

  • Definition: The third and lowest tier of government in Nigeria, established to bring governance closer to the people at the grassroots level.
  • Constitutional Backing: Established under Section 7 of the 1999 Constitution of Nigeria (as amended).
  • Structure: Comprises an elected Chairman and elected Councillors representing wards within the Local Government Area (LGA).
  • Functions: Primary education, primary healthcare, rural roads, waste management, market development, and registration of births, deaths, and marriages.
  • Revenue Sources: Statutory allocations from the Federation Account, internally generated revenue (taxes, fees, licenses), and grants from state governments.
  • Autonomy: While constitutionally recognized, LGAs in Nigeria often face limited financial and administrative autonomy due to state government control.

Two Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Surulere Local Government Area (Lagos State)
One of the 20 LGAs in Lagos State. It is responsible for primary education (running local primary schools), waste collection services, and maintaining local roads like Adeniran Ogunsanya Street. It generates internal revenue through market fees and shop licenses in areas like Aguda and Itire.

Example 2: Nsukka Local Government Area (Enugu State)
Located in Enugu State, this LGA oversees primary healthcare centers in rural communities, local agricultural extension services, and the maintenance of rural roads. It also manages the Nsukka Main Market, collecting levies from traders to fund local development projects.

💡 Fun Fact: Did you know that Nigeria has 774 Local Government Areas (as of 2024)? The Federal Capital Territory (Abuja) has 6 Area Councils that function similarly to LGAs. The largest LGA by land area is Monguno in Borno State, while the most populous is Alimosho in Lagos State, with over 1.2 million residents!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Identify and explain two major sources of revenue for Local Governments in Nigeria.
  2. Question 2: State three specific functions performed by Local Government Authorities in the area of primary healthcare delivery.
  3. Question 3: Why is Local Government often described as the "grassroots" level of government? Give one reason.

Answers

Answer 1: (i) Statutory Allocation – Funds from the Federation Account shared monthly by the Federal Government to all LGAs based on population, equality, and land mass. (ii) Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) – Money collected within the LGA through market fees, tenement rates, marriage registration fees, and business permits.

Answer 2: (i) Establishment and management of primary health centers (PHCs) in rural areas. (ii) Immunization programs for children against diseases like polio and measles. (iii) Environmental sanitation and health education campaigns to prevent cholera and malaria.

Answer 3: Local Government is called the "grassroots" level because it is the closest tier of government to the people in villages, towns, and communities. It directly addresses local needs like village roads, local markets, and primary schools, ensuring that ordinary citizens can participate in governance and access basic services without traveling far.
