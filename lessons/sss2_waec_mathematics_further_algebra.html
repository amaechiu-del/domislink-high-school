Further Algebra: Sequences and Series (SSS2 WAEC)

  • Arithmetic Progression (AP): A sequence where the difference between consecutive terms is constant. The nth term = a + (n-1)d, where a is the first term and d is the common difference.
  • Sum of AP: Sn = n/2 [2a + (n-1)d] or Sn = n/2 (first + last term).
  • Geometric Progression (GP): A sequence where each term is multiplied by a constant ratio r. The nth term = arn-1.
  • Sum of GP (finite): Sn = a(rn - 1)/(r - 1) for r ≠ 1.
  • Sum to infinity (GP): S∞ = a/(1 - r) when |r| < 1.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Example 1: Market Savings

A trader in Lagos saves ₦500 in the first week, ₦550 in the second week, ₦600 in the third week, and so on in arithmetic progression. How much will she save in the 12th week?

Solution: a = 500, d = 50, n = 12.
T12 = a + (12-1)d = 500 + 11(50) = 500 + 550 = ₦1,050.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Example 2: Cassava Farming

A farmer in Oyo State plants cassava stems. The number of stems doubles every week. If he starts with 5 stems, how many stems will he have after 6 weeks? (Assume no harvesting in between)

Solution: This is a GP: a = 5, r = 2, n = 6.
T6 = ar5 = 5 × 25 = 5 × 32 = 160 stems.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The ancient Nigerian game Mancala (or Ayo) uses geometric progression! The number of seeds in each pit often follows a doubling pattern, just like a GP. This helped players calculate moves quickly without paper!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: The 5th term of an AP is 18 and the 9th term is 30. Find the first term and common difference.
  2. Question 2: A GP has first term 3 and common ratio 2. Find the sum of the first 6 terms.
  3. Question 3: The sum to infinity of a GP is 20 and the first term is 8. Find the common ratio.

✅ Answers

1. a = 6, d = 3 (since T5 = a+4d = 18, T9 = a+8d = 30, subtract: 4d=12 → d=3, then a=6).
2. S6 = 3(26 - 1)/(2-1) = 3(64-1) = 3×63 = 189.
3. S∞ = a/(1-r) → 20 = 8/(1-r) → 20(1-r) = 8 → 20 - 20r = 8 → 20r = 12 → r = 12/20 = 0.6.
