Mensuration: Surface Area & Volume of Solids

SSS2 Mathematics (WAEC Syllabus)

  • Mensuration deals with the calculation of lengths, areas, and volumes of geometric shapes.
  • Volume is the amount of space occupied by a solid (measured in cubic units).
  • Surface Area is the total area of all faces or surfaces of a solid.
  • Key formulas for common solids:
    • Cube: V = l³, SA = 6l²
    • Cuboid: V = l × w × h, SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)
    • Cylinder: V = πr²h, SA = 2πr² + 2πrh
    • Cone: V = ⅓πr²h, SA = πr² + πrl (l = slant height)
    • Sphere: V = ⁴⁄₃πr³, SA = 4πr²
  • Units: Always ensure consistent units (e.g., cm, m). Convert if necessary.

📘 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A cylindrical water tank in Lagos has a radius of 1.4 m and a height of 3 m. Calculate its volume. (Take π = ²²⁄₇)

Solution: V = πr²h = (22/7) × (1.4)² × 3 = (22/7) × 1.96 × 3 = (22/7) × 5.88 = 22 × 0.84 = 18.48 m³

Example 2: A rectangular fish pond in Enugu measures 5 m long, 3 m wide, and 1.5 m deep. Find the total surface area of the interior (including the bottom and sides).

Solution: SA = 2(lw + lh + wh) = 2(5×3 + 5×1.5 + 3×1.5) = 2(15 + 7.5 + 4.5) = 2 × 27 = 54 m²

💡 Fun Fact: The Great Pyramid of Giza has a base area of about 53,000 m²! Ancient Egyptian builders used mensuration principles thousands of years ago to ensure perfect proportions. In Nigeria, the Zuma Rock near Abuja is a natural monolith with approximate volume of 2.5 million m³ — imagine calculating its surface area!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. A cone has a base radius of 7 cm and a height of 24 cm. Find its volume. (Take π = ²²⁄₇)
  2. A sphere has a radius of 6 cm. Calculate its surface area. (Take π = 3.142)
  3. A cuboid measures 8 m by 5 m by 2 m. What is its volume in cubic metres?

✅ Answers:

  1. V = ⅓ × (22/7) × 7² × 24 = ⅓ × (22/7) × 49 × 24 = ⅓ × 22 × 7 × 24 = ⅓ × 3696 = 1232 cm³
  2. SA = 4 × 3.142 × 6² = 4 × 3.142 × 36 = 4 × 113.112 = 452.448 cm²
  3. V = 8 × 5 × 2 = 80 m³
