Plane Geometry – Parallel Lines and Transversals

  • Parallel lines are lines in a plane that never meet (e.g., l₁ and l₂ above).
  • A transversal is a line that cuts across two or more parallel lines.
  • Corresponding angles are equal (e.g., the green θ and orange θ in the animation).
  • Alternate interior angles are equal – they lie on opposite sides of the transversal.
  • Co-interior (allied) angles sum to 180° – they lie inside the parallel lines on the same side.
  • Vertically opposite angles formed by intersecting lines are always equal.
🇳🇬 Example 1 (Lagos Road Junction): At a major intersection in Ikeja, two parallel roads (Allen Avenue and Opebi Road) are crossed by a transversal road (Ikorodu Road). If one angle on the transversal is 110°, find the corresponding angle on the other parallel road.
Solution: Corresponding angles are equal → the other angle is also 110°.
🇳🇬 Example 2 (Abuja City Planning): A transversal road crosses two parallel streets in Wuse. One co-interior angle is 75°. What is the other co-interior angle?
Solution: Co-interior angles sum to 180°. 180° – 75° = 105°.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "geometry" comes from Greek geo (earth) and metron (measure). Ancient Egyptians used plane geometry over 4,000 years ago to rebuild land boundaries after the Nile flooded!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One alternate interior angle is 48°. Find the other alternate interior angle.
  2. In a plane, a transversal creates a pair of corresponding angles: (2x + 10)° and 70°. Find the value of x.
  3. If two co-interior angles are (3y)° and (2y + 20)°, what is the value of y?
✅ Answers:
1. 48° (alternate interior angles are equal)
2. 2x + 10 = 70 → 2x = 60 → x = 30
3. 3y + (2y + 20) = 180 → 5y + 20 = 180 → 5y = 160 → y = 32
