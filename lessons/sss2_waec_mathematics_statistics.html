Statistics: Measures of Central Tendency

  • Mean = sum of all values ÷ number of values. It is the average.
  • Median = the middle value when data is arranged in order.
  • Mode = the value that appears most frequently.
  • For grouped data, use class marks and frequencies to estimate the mean.
  • Measures of central tendency help summarise large data sets into one representative value.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A farmer in Kano recorded the following yam harvests (in kg) over 5 days: 40, 55, 60, 55, 70. Find the mean and mode.

Solution: Mean = (40+55+60+55+70)/5 = 280/5 = 56 kg. Mode = 55 kg (appears twice).

Example 2: The ages of 7 students in a Lagos secondary school are: 14, 15, 16, 14, 17, 15, 18. Find the median age.

Solution: Arrange ages: 14, 14, 15, 15, 16, 17, 18. Median (4th value) = 15 years.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? The term "statistics" comes from the Latin word status meaning "state". In ancient Nigeria, the Oyo Empire used census data to organise its army and tax collection — an early form of statistics!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Find the mean of: 12, 15, 18, 21, 24.
  2. What is the median of: 3, 7, 2, 9, 5, 6?
  3. In a class of 10 students, the scores out of 10 are: 6, 7, 8, 6, 9, 7, 7, 10, 6, 8. What is the mode?

✅ Answers

  1. Mean = (12+15+18+21+24)/5 = 90/5 = 18
  2. Arrange: 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 9 → Median = (5+6)/2 = 5.5
  3. Mode = 7 (appears 3 times)
