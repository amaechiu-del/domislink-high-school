Atomic Physics – SSS2 (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Atomic Structure: Atoms consist of a dense nucleus (protons + neutrons) surrounded by electrons in specific energy levels (shells).
  • Energy Levels: Electrons occupy discrete orbits; they can jump to higher levels by absorbing energy (excitation) or fall to lower levels by emitting energy (radiation).
  • Radioactivity: Unstable nuclei decay by emitting alpha (α), beta (β), or gamma (γ) radiation – a key part of atomic physics.
  • Isotopes: Atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons (e.g., Carbon-12 and Carbon-14).
  • Nuclear Reactions: Fusion (joining light nuclei) and fission (splitting heavy nuclei) release huge amounts of energy.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Gamma Irradiation of Cassava: The Nigerian Atomic Energy Commission (NAEC) uses gamma rays from cobalt-60 to irradiate cassava and yam tubers. This kills pests and extends shelf life, helping farmers reduce post-harvest losses.

Example 2 – Oil Well Logging: In the Niger Delta, oil companies use neutron and gamma ray sources to probe rock formations. This atomic physics technique helps locate crude oil and natural gas deposits deep underground.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "atom" comes from the Greek word atomos meaning "indivisible." But we now know atoms can be split! In fact, the first artificial nuclear reactor was built in Chicago in 1942 – and today, Nigeria is working towards building its own research reactor for medicine and agriculture.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define an isotope. Give one example of an isotope used in medicine or agriculture in Nigeria.
  2. Question 2: In Bohr's model, what happens to an electron when it absorbs a photon of light? Explain the energy change.
  3. Question 3: A radioactive sample has a half-life of 8 days. If you start with 80 g, how much remains after 24 days?

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Isotopes are atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons. Example: Cobalt-60 is used in Nigeria for cancer treatment and food irradiation.
  2. Answer 2: The electron absorbs energy and jumps from a lower energy level (ground state) to a higher energy level (excited state). The energy of the photon must exactly match the difference between the two levels.
  3. Answer 3: After 24 days (3 half-lives): 80 g → 40 g → 20 g → 10 g remaining.
