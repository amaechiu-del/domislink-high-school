Electromagnetic Waves (SSS2 Physics - WAEC Syllabus)

Definition: Electromagnetic waves are transverse waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, propagating through space at the speed of light (c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s). They require no medium for transmission.

Key Properties

  • Transverse nature: Electric (E) and magnetic (B) fields oscillate perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation.
  • Speed in vacuum: All electromagnetic waves travel at c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s.
  • No medium required: They can travel through vacuum (unlike sound waves).
  • Wavelength (λ) and Frequency (f): Related by c = fλ.
  • Energy: Higher frequency means higher energy (E = hf, where h is Planck's constant).

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Radio broadcasting (NTA & FRCN): Radio stations in Nigeria use radio waves (a type of EM wave) to transmit audio signals from the transmitter in Abuja or Lagos to your radio receiver at home. These waves travel through the air and even through walls.

Example 2: Microwave communication (MTN & Glo networks): Mobile network operators in Nigeria use microwaves to transmit data between cell towers and to satellite dishes. Microwaves have higher frequencies than radio waves and can carry large amounts of data for calls and internet.

💡 Fun Fact: The Sun emits a broad spectrum of electromagnetic waves, including visible light, ultraviolet, and infrared. In Nigeria, we experience strong UV radiation, which is why sunscreen is important even on cloudy days!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the speed of all electromagnetic waves in a vacuum?
    Answer: 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s (speed of light).
  2. State two differences between electromagnetic waves and sound waves.
    Answer: (i) EM waves are transverse; sound waves are longitudinal. (ii) EM waves can travel through vacuum; sound waves require a medium.
  3. A radio station in Lagos broadcasts at a frequency of 100 MHz (100 × 10⁶ Hz). Calculate the wavelength of the radio waves. (Use c = 3.0 × 10⁸ m/s)
    Answer: λ = c/f = (3.0 × 10⁸) / (100 × 10⁶) = 3.0 m.
