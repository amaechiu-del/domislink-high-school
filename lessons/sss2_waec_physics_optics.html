Optics: Refraction of Light

SSS2 Physics (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Points

  • Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one medium to another due to a change in speed.
  • The incident ray, refracted ray, and the normal all lie in the same plane.
  • When light travels from a less dense medium (air) to a denser medium (water), it bends towards the normal.
  • The angle of incidence (θ₁) and angle of refraction (θ₂) are related by Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂.
  • The refractive index (n) of a medium measures how much it slows down light.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A fisherman in Lagos Lagoon sees a fish below the water surface. Due to refraction, the fish appears closer to the surface than it actually is. This is why spearing fish requires aiming below the apparent position.
Example 2: During harmattan in Northern Nigeria, mirages occur on hot roads. Light from the sky refracts through layers of hot air near the ground, creating an illusion of water on the road.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "refraction" comes from Latin refringere meaning "to break apart". The bending of light also causes rainbows — when sunlight refracts through raindrops, it splits into seven colours!

Practice Questions

  1. Light travels from air (n = 1.00) into water (n = 1.33). If the angle of incidence is 40°, calculate the angle of refraction. (sin 40° = 0.643)
  2. Explain why a pool of water appears shallower than its actual depth.
  3. A ray of light passes from glass (n = 1.50) into air at an angle of incidence of 30°. Will the ray bend towards or away from the normal? Calculate the angle of refraction. (sin 30° = 0.500)

Answers

1. Using Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂ → 1.00 × 0.643 = 1.33 × sin θ₂ → sin θ₂ = 0.643/1.33 = 0.483 → θ₂ = sin⁻¹(0.483) ≈ 28.9°.

2. Light from the bottom of the pool refracts away from the normal when leaving water into air. The brain traces the ray back in a straight line, making the image appear at a shallower depth.

3. Light travels from denser (glass) to less dense (air), so it bends away from the normal. n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂ → 1.50 × 0.500 = 1.00 × sin θ₂ → sin θ₂ = 0.750 → θ₂ = sin⁻¹(0.750) ≈ 48.6°.
