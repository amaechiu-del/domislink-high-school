Conservation in Biology (SSS3 - NERDC Syllabus)

Key Concepts of Conservation

  • Conservation is the sustainable management of natural resources (plants, animals, water, soil) to prevent depletion and extinction.
  • Biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms in an ecosystem; conservation protects this variety.
  • Methods include in-situ (e.g., national parks, game reserves) and ex-situ (e.g., zoos, seed banks) conservation.
  • Overexploitation, habitat destruction, pollution, and climate change are major threats to conservation.
  • Legislation (like Nigeria's National Parks Service Act) and community-based programs help enforce conservation.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Yankari National Park (Bauchi State) – Nigeria's largest wildlife park, home to elephants, baboons, and antelopes. It is a protected area that promotes eco-tourism and anti-poaching efforts.

2. Omo Forest Reserve (Ogun State) – A tropical rainforest reserve that conserves timber species, rare plants, and wildlife like the endangered white-throated monkey. It also supports sustainable logging and community livelihoods.

✨ Fun Fact ✨

The Cross River National Park (Nigeria) is one of the last remaining habitats of the Cross River gorilla – the world's rarest great ape, with fewer than 300 individuals left in the wild!

📝 Practice Questions

Q1. Define conservation and state two reasons why it is important in Nigeria.

Show Answer

Conservation is the protection and sustainable use of natural resources. Importance: (i) Prevents extinction of species like the Cross River gorilla. (ii) Maintains ecosystem services such as clean water and fertile soil for farming.

Q2. Differentiate between in-situ and ex-situ conservation, giving one Nigerian example of each.

Show Answer

In-situ conserves species in their natural habitat (e.g., Yankari National Park). Ex-situ conserves species outside their natural habitat (e.g., the National Botanic Garden in Ibadan).

Q3. List two human activities that threaten biodiversity in Nigeria and suggest one solution for each.

Show Answer

Threats: (i) Deforestation for agriculture – Solution: Enforce reforestation programs. (ii) Poaching of wildlife – Solution: Strengthen anti-poaching patrols and community education.
