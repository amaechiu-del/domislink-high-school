Chemical Industry: SSS3 Chemistry (NERDC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Chemical Industry: The branch of industry that converts raw materials (e.g., crude oil, natural gas, limestone) into useful chemical products through physical and chemical processes.
  • Feedstock: Raw materials used as input in chemical reactions (e.g., naphtha from crude oil for producing plastics).
  • Unit Processes: Key chemical reactions in the industry, such as cracking (breaking large hydrocarbons into smaller ones), polymerization (forming plastics), and the Haber process (ammonia production).
  • Downstream & Upstream: Upstream involves extraction of raw materials; downstream involves refining and manufacturing end-user products like detergents, paints, and pharmaceuticals.
  • Environmental Impact: The chemical industry must manage waste, emissions, and byproducts to reduce pollution and comply with regulations.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Indorama Eleme Petrochemicals (Port Harcourt): Produces polypropylene and polyethylene from natural gas and naphtha. These are used to make plastic containers, bags, and packaging materials widely used across Nigeria.
  2. Dangote Fertilizer Plant (Lekki): Produces urea fertilizer from natural gas via the Haber-Bosch process. This supports Nigerian agriculture by providing affordable nitrogen-based fertilizers for crops like maize, rice, and cassava.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? Nigeria's petrochemical industry produces over 1.5 million metric tons of polymers (plastics) annually! This is enough to produce billions of water sachets, which are a common source of drinking water across the country. The chemical industry actually helps provide clean drinking water to millions of Nigerians through plastic packaging.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: What is the primary raw material used in Nigeria's petrochemical industry, and name one major product derived from it.
  2. Question 2: Describe the purpose of the "cracking" process in the chemical industry.
  3. Question 3: Why is the chemical industry important to Nigeria's economy? Give two reasons.

Answers

  1. Answer 1: The primary raw material is crude oil (or natural gas). A major product is polypropylene (used for plastics), or gasoline (used as fuel).
  2. Answer 2: Cracking is the process of breaking down large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller, more useful ones (e.g., converting heavy oil into petrol and diesel).
  3. Answer 3: The chemical industry contributes to Nigeria's economy by (i) providing employment in refineries and factories, and (ii) producing essential goods like fertilizers for agriculture and plastics for packaging, reducing the need for imports.
