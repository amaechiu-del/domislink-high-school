Polymers: Giant Molecules from Small Units

Key Concepts

  • Polymer: A large molecule made up of many repeating subunits called monomers.
  • Polymerization: The chemical process that joins monomers together to form a polymer chain.
  • Types:
    • Addition polymers – formed by adding monomers without losing any atom (e.g., polythene).
    • Condensation polymers – formed when monomers join with the loss of small molecules like water (e.g., nylon).
  • Properties: Polymers can be thermoplastic (soften on heating) or thermosetting (harden permanently).
  • Uses: Plastics, fibres, rubber, adhesives, and coatings.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  1. Polythene bags (nylon bags) – Commonly used for packaging kola nuts, garri, and other market goods across Nigeria. Made from addition polymerization of ethene.
  2. Rubber from Hevea brasiliensis – Natural rubber tapped from rubber plantations in Edo, Delta, and Akwa Ibom states. It is a polymer of isoprene monomers.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know that DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is a natural polymer? It is made of monomer units called nucleotides. The polymer chain of DNA carries the genetic code that makes every living thing unique – even the yam and cassava you eat!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. What is the name of the small repeating unit that makes up a polymer?
  2. Give one difference between addition and condensation polymerization.
  3. Name two Nigerian products that are made from polymers.

✅ Answers

  1. Monomer.
  2. Addition polymerization involves monomers adding together without losing any atoms, while condensation polymerization involves the loss of a small molecule (e.g., water) when monomers join.
  3. Examples: Polythene bags (used in markets) and natural rubber (from rubber plantations).
