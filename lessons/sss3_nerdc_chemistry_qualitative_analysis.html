Qualitative Analysis in Chemistry (SSS3 NERDC Syllabus)

Qualitative Analysis involves identifying the chemical components (cations, anions, and gases) in a given substance using simple tests like color, solubility, flame tests, and precipitation reactions. It helps chemists determine what is present, not how much.

Key Concepts

  • Cation tests: Flame test (e.g., Na⁺ gives yellow, K⁺ gives lilac); NaOH test (e.g., Fe²⁺ gives dirty green precipitate, Cu²⁺ gives blue precipitate).
  • Anion tests: Carbonate (CO₃²⁻) fizzes with dilute HCl, producing CO₂ gas that turns limewater milky. Sulfate (SO₄²⁻) gives a white precipitate with BaCl₂ solution.
  • Gas tests: H₂ (pops with a lighted splint), O₂ (relights a glowing splint), CO₂ (turns limewater milky), NH₃ (turns damp red litmus blue).
  • Solubility rules: All sodium, potassium, and ammonium salts are soluble. Most chlorides are soluble except AgCl and PbCl₂. Most sulfates are soluble except BaSO₄ and PbSO₄.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A student in Lagos tests a white solid suspected to be table salt (NaCl). Flame test gives a bright yellow color. Adding AgNO₃ solution gives a white precipitate soluble in dilute NH₃. Conclusion: The solid contains Na⁺ and Cl⁻ (sodium chloride).
Example 2: A sample from a stream in Port Harcourt is tested. It produces a gas that turns limewater milky when dilute HCl is added. The solution gives a white precipitate with BaCl₂. Conclusion: The sample contains CO₃²⁻ and SO₄²⁻ ions (carbonate and sulfate).
🧪 Fun Fact: The flame test for sodium is so sensitive that even a tiny speck of sodium from a fingerprint on the nichrome wire can produce a yellow flame! That's why chemists always clean their wire with concentrated HCl before testing.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A white salt gives a lilac flame test and produces a gas that turns limewater milky when dilute HCl is added. Identify the cation and anion present.
  2. Question 2: A solution gives a dirty green precipitate with NaOH, and the precipitate turns brown on standing. Which cation is present? What is the color change due to?
  3. Question 3: A student adds BaCl₂ solution to a sample and observes a white precipitate that does not dissolve in dilute HNO₃. Which anion is confirmed? Name one common Nigerian mineral containing this anion.

Answers

Answer 1: Cation = K⁺ (potassium, lilac flame). Anion = CO₃²⁻ (carbonate, because CO₂ turns limewater milky).

Answer 2: Cation = Fe²⁺ (iron(II)). The dirty green precipitate (Fe(OH)₂) oxidizes in air to brown Fe(OH)₃.

Answer 3: Anion = SO₄²⁻ (sulfate). Common Nigerian mineral: gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) found in parts of Borno and Taraba states.
