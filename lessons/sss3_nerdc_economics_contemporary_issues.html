Contemporary Issues in Economics (SSS3)

Contemporary issues in economics refer to current, real-world problems and debates that affect economies globally and locally. This lesson focuses on three key issues: inflation, unemployment, and economic growth vs. development, with a focus on Nigeria.

🔺 Inflation

  • A sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services.
  • Reduces purchasing power: the same amount of money buys fewer goods.
  • Can be caused by demand-pull (too much money chasing few goods) or cost-push (rising production costs).

🔵 Unemployment

  • When people who are willing and able to work cannot find jobs.
  • Types: frictional (short-term), structural (mismatch of skills), cyclical (due to economic downturn).
  • Leads to lost output, poverty, and social unrest.

🟢 Economic Growth vs. Development

  • Growth: increase in a country's output (GDP).
  • Development: broader improvement in living standards, education, health, and equality.
  • A country can grow (e.g., oil revenues) without developing (if wealth is not shared).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Inflation: In 2023, Nigeria's inflation rate hit over 28%, driven by fuel subsidy removal and naira devaluation. Prices of food, transport, and housing skyrocketed, hurting low-income households.

2. Unemployment: Nigeria's youth unemployment rate (ages 15–24) exceeded 42% in 2023. Many graduates lack skills matching available jobs, leading to underemployment and the "japa" (emigration) trend.

🎉 Fun Fact

Did you know? During Nigeria's oil boom in the 1970s, the economy grew rapidly (high GDP), but poverty and inequality also increased. This is a classic example of "growth without development" — the oil wealth didn't trickle down to improve schools, hospitals, or roads for most citizens.

📝 Practice Questions

Q1. Define inflation and state two ways it affects Nigerian households.

Q2. Explain the difference between economic growth and economic development. Give one Nigerian example.

Q3. List three types of unemployment and suggest one solution for each.

✅ Answers

A1. Inflation is a sustained rise in general price levels. Effects: (i) Reduces purchasing power — families can buy less food and fuel. (ii) Erodes savings — money saved loses value over time.

A2. Economic growth is an increase in GDP (e.g., Nigeria's GDP rising due to oil exports). Economic development is broader: improved health, education, and equality. Example: Nigeria's GDP grew in the 2000s, but many still lacked electricity and clean water (growth without development).

A3. (i) Frictional — solution: better job matching websites. (ii) Structural — solution: skills training programs (e.g., N-Power). (iii) Cyclical — solution: government stimulus spending during recessions.
