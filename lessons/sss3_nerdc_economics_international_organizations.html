Key Points

  • IMF (International Monetary Fund): Promotes global monetary cooperation, provides short-term loans to countries with balance-of-payments problems, and offers policy advice.
  • World Bank: Focuses on long-term economic development and poverty reduction through funding infrastructure, education, and health projects.
  • WTO (World Trade Organization): Oversees international trade rules, resolves trade disputes, and negotiates tariff reductions to promote free trade.
  • These organizations help stabilize the global economy, encourage trade, and support developing nations.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In 2020, Nigeria secured a $3.4 billion emergency loan from the IMF to combat the economic impact of COVID-19 and falling oil prices.
Example 2: Nigeria has been a WTO member since 1995, participating in trade policy reviews and benefiting from dispute resolution mechanisms to protect its exports.
💡 Fun Fact: The World Bank was originally created to help rebuild Europe after World War II. Today, it lends over $60 billion annually to developing countries — Nigeria has received over $30 billion in World Bank loans since 1960!

Practice Questions

  1. What is the main difference between the IMF and the World Bank in terms of their lending focus?
  2. Name one way the WTO has helped Nigeria's trade interests.
  3. Why might a country like Nigeria seek a loan from the IMF rather than from a commercial bank?

Answers

Answer 1: The IMF provides short-term loans to stabilize currencies and fix balance-of-payments crises, while the World Bank provides long-term loans for development projects like roads, schools, and power plants.

Answer 2: Through the WTO, Nigeria can challenge unfair trade barriers (e.g., when the EU restricted Nigerian cocoa exports) and participate in rule-making that affects its agricultural and oil exports.

Answer 3: IMF loans usually have lower interest rates and longer repayment periods than commercial banks, and they come with technical advice to help the country implement economic reforms.
