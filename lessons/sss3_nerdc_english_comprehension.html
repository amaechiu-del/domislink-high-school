Key Comprehension Skills (SSS3 NERDC)

  • Main Idea: Identify the central message or argument the author is making.
  • Supporting Details: Locate facts, examples, or reasons that back up the main idea.
  • Inference: Read between the lines – understand what is implied but not directly stated.
  • Vocabulary in Context: Use surrounding words to deduce the meaning of unfamiliar terms.
  • Author’s Purpose: Decide if the text aims to inform, persuade, entertain, or describe.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Passage:
“The harmattan wind swept through Kano, drying every leaf and cracking the soil. Farmers covered their yam barns with wet sacks to keep the tubers from shrivelling.”
Question: Why did farmers cover their yam barns with wet sacks?
Answer: To prevent the yams from drying out/shrivelling during the dry harmattan season.
Example 2 – Passage:
“Chief Nwosu’s obi was always full of visitors. Some came to seek his advice on land disputes, others to ask for school fees. His wife, Mama Ada, never complained, even when the pot of egusi soup ran low.”
Question: What can you infer about Chief Nwosu’s character?
Answer: He is a respected, generous community leader who people trust for help and advice.
💡 Fun Fact: The longest sentence ever found in a Nigerian English comprehension passage was 127 words long – it described the bustling Oshodi market in Lagos! Skimming and scanning skills help you break down such long sentences.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Passage: “Despite the heavy rain, the students of Government Secondary School, Enugu, marched proudly in the inter-house sports parade. The principal later commended their resilience.”
    What does the word “resilience” most likely mean?
    Answer: The ability to keep going despite difficulty / toughness.

  2. Passage: “The baobab tree stood like a giant in the middle of the village square. Under its shade, elders settled disputes, children played ludo, and traders displayed their wares.”
    What is the main idea of this passage?
    Answer: The baobab tree is a central and important gathering place for the village community.

  3. Passage: “Adaeze stared at her JAMB result slip. Her hands trembled. A single tear rolled down her cheek and splashed onto the paper.”
    What inference can you make about Adaeze’s result?
    Answer: She likely did not get the result she hoped for (the tear and trembling suggest disappointment, not joy).
