Revision in English Language (Nerdc Syllabus)

Definition: Revision is the process of reviewing, re-reading, and improving written work or previously learned material to reinforce understanding and correct errors.

Key Points to Remember

  • Purpose: Revision helps identify mistakes in grammar, spelling, punctuation, and logical flow of ideas.
  • Stages: Revise for content first (ideas and arguments), then structure (paragraphs and transitions), and finally mechanics (grammar and spelling).
  • Active Revision: Read aloud, use highlighters, create summaries, or ask a peer to review your work.
  • Common Areas: Focus on tenses, subject-verb agreement, pronoun consistency, and paragraph unity.
  • Tools: Use checklists, past questions, and teacher feedback to guide your revision.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Before revision: "The student them go to school yesterday."
After revision: "The students went to school yesterday." (Correction of plural form and tense)

Example 2: Before revision: "My mother she is a teacher and she teach in Lagos."
After revision: "My mother is a teacher, and she teaches in Lagos." (Removed pronoun repetition, corrected verb agreement)

🎉 Fun Fact: Did you know that the word "revision" comes from the Latin word revisere, meaning "to look at again"? In Nigerian English, we often say "I am going through my notes" — this is also a form of revision! Many WAEC and NECO examiners say that students who revise their essays at least twice score 15-20% higher than those who submit their first draft.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Revise the following sentence: "The boy don't know nothing about the accident." Write the corrected version.
  2. Question 2: Identify and correct two errors in this paragraph: "Nigeria is a beautiful country. It have many cultures. The people are friendly and they always help one another."
  3. Question 3: What is the most important thing to check first when revising an essay: spelling, content, or punctuation? Explain why.

Answers

  1. Answer 1: "The boy doesn't know anything about the accident." OR "The boy knows nothing about the accident." (Corrected double negative)
  2. Answer 2: Error 1: "It have" should be "It has" (subject-verb agreement). Error 2: "they always help" — the sentence is correct but could be improved to "they always help one another" (no change needed — the two errors are: "have" → "has" and add a comma after "cultures" for better flow). Corrected: "Nigeria is a beautiful country. It has many cultures, and the people are friendly. They always help one another."
  3. Answer 3: Content should be checked first. This is because if the ideas are unclear, incomplete, or off-topic, no amount of correct spelling or punctuation will make the essay effective. Good content provides the foundation for all other improvements.
