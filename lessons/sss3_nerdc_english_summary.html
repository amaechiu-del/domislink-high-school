📌 Key Rules for Writing a Summary

  • Read first — Understand the entire passage before writing.
  • Identify the main idea — Ask: What is the author’s central point?
  • Omit examples & repetitions — Keep only what is essential.
  • Use your own words — Do not copy phrases directly.
  • Stay objective — Do not add your opinion or interpretation.
  • Keep it short — Aim for about one-third of the original length.
  • Check for coherence — Your summary should flow as a complete paragraph.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (Literature):
Original: "In Chinua Achebe’s Things Fall Apart, Okonkwo is a respected leader in Umuofia who fears weakness because of his father Unoka’s laziness. He works hard to gain titles and wealth, but his fear of being seen as weak leads him to act harshly, even killing Ikemefuna, a boy who calls him father. Eventually, his rigid nature causes his downfall."

✅ Summary: "Okonkwo’s fear of appearing weak, stemming from his father’s failure, drives him to extreme actions that ultimately lead to his tragic downfall in Things Fall Apart."
Example 2 (Current Affairs):
Original: "The Nigerian government recently launched a new agricultural scheme aimed at boosting cassava production in the South-South region. Farmers will receive improved seedlings, training on modern farming techniques, and access to low-interest loans. The program is expected to create thousands of jobs and reduce the country’s dependence on imported food items."

✅ Summary: "A new government agricultural program in Nigeria’s South-South region aims to increase cassava production by providing farmers with seedlings, training, and loans, with the goal of creating jobs and reducing food imports."
⭐ Fun Fact: Did you know that the famous Nigerian writer Wole Soyinka once summarized a 300-page play manuscript into just two paragraphs for a newspaper review? He said, "A good summary is like a palm wine tap — you must extract only the sweetest drops without disturbing the tree!"

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1: Read the following passage and write a one-sentence summary:
"Nigeria’s education system faces many challenges, including inadequate funding, shortage of qualified teachers, and poor infrastructure. Despite these problems, some states have introduced technology in classrooms to improve learning outcomes. For example, Lagos State now uses tablets for students in selected schools."

Show Answer

✅ Despite challenges like poor funding and teacher shortages, some Nigerian states are using technology such as tablets to improve education.

Question 2: Which of the following should NOT appear in a good summary?
A) The main argument of the author
B) A direct quote from the passage
C) The central idea in your own words
D) The key supporting points

Show Answer

✅ B — Direct quotes should be avoided; a summary uses your own words.

Question 3: Summarize this Yoruba proverb explanation in one sentence:
"The Yoruba saying 'Ile la ti n ko eso oko' means that a child’s upbringing starts at home. It emphasizes that parents are the first teachers. If a child behaves badly in public, it reflects poor home training."

Show Answer

✅ The Yoruba proverb "Ile la ti n ko eso oko" teaches that a child’s behavior reflects the quality of training received at home from parents.
