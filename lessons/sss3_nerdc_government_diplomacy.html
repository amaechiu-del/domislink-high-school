Diplomacy in Government (SSS3 NERDC Syllabus)

🔍 Key Concepts of Diplomacy

  • Diplomacy is the art and practice of conducting negotiations between representatives of states or groups to manage international relations.
  • Bilateral diplomacy involves two countries working directly together (e.g., Nigeria and Ghana).
  • Multilateral diplomacy involves multiple countries through organizations like the United Nations or African Union.
  • Core tools: negotiation, dialogue, treaties, economic agreements, cultural exchanges, and summit meetings.
  • Diplomatic immunity protects diplomats from legal action in host countries to ensure free communication.
  • Modern diplomacy includes public diplomacy (engaging foreign publics) and economic diplomacy (trade & investment).

🇳🇬 Example 1: Nigeria & South Africa

In 2021, Nigeria and South Africa used diplomatic channels to resolve trade tensions and xenophobic attacks. The Nigeria-South Africa Bi-National Commission strengthened consular relations and protected citizens’ rights, showing how diplomacy de-escalates crises.

🇳🇬 Example 2: Nigeria & ECOWAS

Nigeria used multilateral diplomacy within ECOWAS to help restore peace in Liberia and Sierra Leone (1990s–2000s). Through negotiations, troop contributions, and sanctions, Nigeria demonstrated regional diplomatic leadership under the principle of collective security.

🎉 Fun Fact: The word “diplomacy” comes from the Greek diploma (folded document). In ancient Greece, envoys carried official folded documents as credentials. Today, diplomatic passports still serve that purpose!

  1. Define diplomacy and explain two types of diplomacy used by Nigeria in international relations.
  2. Describe one specific way Nigeria used diplomacy to resolve a conflict in West Africa.
  3. Why is diplomatic immunity important for international relations? Give a brief reason.

Answer 1: Diplomacy is the management of international relations through negotiation and dialogue. Two types: Bilateral diplomacy (e.g., Nigeria–Ghana relations) and Multilateral diplomacy (e.g., Nigeria in the United Nations or ECOWAS).

Answer 2: Nigeria used multilateral diplomacy through ECOWAS to mediate and deploy peacekeeping forces during the civil wars in Liberia and Sierra Leone, helping to restore stability through negotiations and regional cooperation.

Answer 3: Diplomatic immunity ensures diplomats can perform their duties without fear of harassment or legal intimidation by the host country, allowing free communication and negotiation between states.
