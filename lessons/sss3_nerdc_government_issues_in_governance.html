Issues in Governance

Key Governance Issues

  • Corruption & Fraud: Misuse of public office for private gain, including bribery, embezzlement, and nepotism. Undermines trust in government.
  • Lack of Accountability: Public officials not held responsible for their actions, leading to impunity and poor service delivery.
  • Weak Rule of Law: Inconsistent enforcement of laws, judicial inefficiency, and unequal treatment before the law.
  • Electoral Malpractice: Rigging, vote buying, violence during elections, and manipulation of electoral processes.
  • Inequality & Poverty: Uneven distribution of resources, poor social welfare, and lack of access to basic services for citizens.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: The Pension Scam (2010–2012)
Nigerian pension administrators stole over ₦100 billion in pension funds, exposing massive corruption in government agencies. The scandal led to the arrest of several officials but highlighted weak oversight and accountability.

Example 2: 2023 General Elections
Allegations of vote buying, intimidation, and technical glitches with the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) raised concerns about electoral integrity, though the election was largely peaceful compared to past cycles.

⭐ Fun Fact: Nigeria's Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) introduced the BVAS in 2023 to curb electoral fraud. The device uses biometric data to verify voters, making it one of Africa's most advanced electoral technologies!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define "accountability" in the context of governance. Why is it important for democracy?
    Answer: Accountability means public officials are answerable for their actions and decisions. It is important because it prevents abuse of power, ensures transparency, and builds citizen trust.
  2. Question 2: Identify two ways electoral malpractice can undermine a country's democracy.
    Answer: (i) It erodes public confidence in elections, leading to low voter turnout. (ii) It can result in illegitimate leaders who lack the mandate to govern effectively.
  3. Question 3: Give one example of a governance issue from Nigeria and suggest a solution.
    Answer: Example: Corruption in public procurement (e.g., inflated contracts). Solution: Strengthen anti-corruption agencies like the EFCC and ICPC, and enforce digital tracking of government spending.
