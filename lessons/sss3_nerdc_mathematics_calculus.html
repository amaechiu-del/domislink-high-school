Key Concepts in Calculus

  • Differentiation – measures the rate of change of a function. The derivative \( f'(x) \) gives the slope of the tangent line at any point.
  • Integration – finds the area under a curve (definite integral) or the original function from its derivative (indefinite integral).
  • Limit – the value a function approaches as the input gets closer to a point. The foundation of both differentiation and integration.
  • Notation: Derivative: \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) or \( f'(x) \). Integral: \( \int f(x) \, dx \).

Nigerian Example 1: Oil Production Rate

An oil company in Port Harcourt models its daily crude output (in thousands of barrels) as \( P(t) = 50 + 20t - t^2 \), where \( t \) is days after January 1. Find the rate of change of production after 5 days.

Solution: Differentiate: \( P'(t) = 20 - 2t \). At \( t = 5 \), \( P'(5) = 20 - 10 = 10 \) thousand barrels/day. This means production is increasing by 10,000 barrels per day on day 5.

Nigerian Example 2: Farm Plot Area

A farmer in Kaduna has a rectangular plot bounded by a river on one side. He uses 100 m of fencing for the other three sides. Find the maximum possible area of the plot.

Solution: Let width = \( x \), length = \( 100 - 2x \). Area \( A(x) = x(100 - 2x) = 100x - 2x^2 \). Differentiate: \( A'(x) = 100 - 4x \). Set to zero: \( 100 - 4x = 0 \Rightarrow x = 25 \). Maximum area = \( 25 \times 50 = 1250 \) m².

🧠 Fun Fact: The word "calculus" comes from the Latin word for "pebble" — ancient Romans used small stones for counting. The Nigerian mathematician Dr. Grace Alele-Williams was the first Nigerian woman to earn a PhD in mathematics (University of Chicago, 1963), and her work helped inspire calculus education across West Africa!

Practice Questions

  1. Differentiate \( f(x) = 3x^3 - 5x^2 + 2x - 7 \).
  2. Evaluate \( \int_{1}^{3} (2x + 4) \, dx \).
  3. Find the coordinates of the turning point of \( y = x^2 - 8x + 12 \), and state whether it is a maximum or minimum.

Answers

  1. \( f'(x) = 9x^2 - 10x + 2 \)
  2. \( \left[ x^2 + 4x \right]_{1}^{3} = (9 + 12) - (1 + 4) = 21 - 5 = 16 \)
  3. Derivative: \( 2x - 8 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 4 \). \( y = 16 - 32 + 12 = -4 \). Second derivative: \( 2 > 0 \), so it is a minimum at (4, -4).
