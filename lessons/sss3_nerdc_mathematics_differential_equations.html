Key Concepts

  • Differential Equation (DE): An equation involving derivatives of a function.
  • Order: The highest derivative present (e.g., first order if it contains dy/dx).
  • General Solution: Family of curves satisfying the DE, includes an arbitrary constant.
  • Particular Solution: Obtained by using an initial condition to find the constant.
  • Separation of Variables: Rearranging so each variable appears on one side.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (Population Growth in Lagos): The rate of population growth of a suburb is proportional to its current population. If the population doubles in 10 years, find the population after 20 years, given initial population \(P_0\).

Solution: \(\frac{dP}{dt}=kP \Rightarrow P=P_0 e^{kt}\). Using \(P(10)=2P_0\) gives \(k=\frac{\ln 2}{10}\). After 20 years: \(P=4P_0\).

Example 2 (Cooling of Akara Oil): A pot of oil cools from 200°C to 150°C in 5 minutes in a room at 30°C. Using Newton's law of cooling, find the temperature after 10 minutes.

Solution: \(\frac{dT}{dt}=-k(T-30) \Rightarrow T=30+170e^{-kt}\). Using \(T(5)=150\) gives \(k=\frac{1}{5}\ln\frac{170}{120}\). Then \(T(10)=30+170e^{-10k} \approx 30+170\left(\frac{120}{170}\right)^2 = 30+120\cdot\frac{120}{170} \approx 114.7^\circ\text{C}\).

💡 Fun Fact: Differential equations are used to model the spread of diseases like malaria in Nigeria — the SIR model (Susceptible, Infected, Recovered) is a system of ODEs!

Practice Questions

  1. Solve \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 + 2x\) given that \(y=5\) when \(x=0\).
  2. Find the general solution of \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y}{x}\) for \(x>0\).
  3. A radioactive substance decays at a rate proportional to its mass. If half of it decays in 100 years, what percentage remains after 200 years?

Answers:

  1. \(y = x^3 + x^2 + 5\)
  2. \(y = Cx\) (where \(C\) is constant)
  3. 25% remains (since half-life is 100 years, after two half-lives: \(\frac{1}{4}\))
