Vectors in Mathematics (SSS3 NERDC)

Key Concepts

  • Vector: A quantity with both magnitude (size) and direction. Examples: displacement, velocity, force.
  • Scalar: A quantity with only magnitude (no direction). Examples: mass, time, temperature.
  • Notation: Vectors are often written as a, b, or in column form: [34]
  • Addition: Add components: a + b = (ax+bx, ay+by). The SVG above shows the tip-to-tail method.
  • Magnitude: |a| = √(ax² + ay²)
  • Direction: Given by angle θ = tan⁻¹(ay/ax) relative to horizontal.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (Lagos to Ibadan): A bus travels 120 km East from Lagos, then 90 km North. What is its resultant displacement?

Solution: Vector a = (120, 0), Vector b = (0, 90). Resultant = (120, 90). Magnitude = √(120² + 90²) = √(14400 + 8100) = √22500 = 150 km. Direction = tan⁻¹(90/120) = tan⁻¹(0.75) ≈ 36.87° North of East.

Example 2 (River Niger Crossing): A boat heads directly across the River Niger at 5 m/s North, while the current flows East at 3 m/s. Find the boat's actual velocity.

Solution: Velocity vector = (3, 5). Magnitude = √(3² + 5²) = √34 ≈ 5.83 m/s. Direction = tan⁻¹(5/3) ≈ 59.04° North of East.

🎉 Fun Fact: The word "vector" comes from Latin meaning "carrier". In Nigeria, vector analysis is used by engineers designing the Third Mainland Bridge in Lagos to calculate forces and ensure stability!

Practice Questions

  1. A hunter in Enugu walks 8 km East, then 6 km South. Calculate the magnitude and direction of his displacement from the starting point.
  2. Given vector p = (4, -2) and q = (-1, 3), find p + q and |p + q|.
  3. A car moves with velocity (40, 30) km/h. Find its speed and the angle it makes with the East direction.

Answers

  1. Magnitude = 10 km, direction = 36.87° South of East (or 53.13° East of South)
  2. p + q = (3, 1); |p + q| = √10 ≈ 3.16
  3. Speed = 50 km/h, angle = 36.87° North of East
