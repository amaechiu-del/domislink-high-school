Matter: Structure & Properties (SSS3 Physics)

Key Concepts

  • Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. It is made of tiny particles (atoms/molecules).
  • Kinetic Theory: Particles are in constant random motion. Their energy determines the state (solid, liquid, gas).
  • Solid: Particles vibrate about fixed positions; strong intermolecular forces; definite shape and volume.
  • Liquid: Particles slide past each other; weaker forces; takes the shape of its container but has fixed volume.
  • Gas: Particles move freely at high speeds; negligible forces; fills any container and has no fixed shape/volume.
  • Changes of State: Melting (solid→liquid), boiling/evaporation (liquid→gas), condensation (gas→liquid), freezing (liquid→solid), sublimation (solid→gas).
  • Brownian Motion: Random movement of visible particles (e.g., smoke or pollen) caused by invisible molecules colliding with them.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

  1. Making Garri (Eba): When cassava is grated and fried, water evaporates (liquid → gas). The solid garri particles are left behind. Adding hot water to make eba shows how particles in the liquid (water) interact with solid garri to form a semi-solid paste.
  2. Melting of Palm Oil in the Sun: In northern Nigeria, palm oil stored in containers often melts from solid to liquid on hot afternoons. This illustrates how heat energy increases particle motion, overcoming intermolecular forces.

🤯 Fun Fact: In Nigeria, the Zuma Rock is a massive solid igneous rock. But did you know that even rocks slowly “flow” over millions of years? Under extreme pressure, solid matter in the Earth’s mantle behaves like a very thick liquid — a property called plastic deformation!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: A block of ice is left on a table in a warm room. Describe the changes in particle motion and arrangement as it melts into water.
  2. Question 2: Explain why a gas can be compressed easily, but a solid cannot. Use the kinetic model of matter in your answer.
  3. Question 3: In a Nigerian kitchen, a pot of soup is left uncovered on the stove. After some time, the volume of soup reduces. Use the concept of evaporation to explain this observation.

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: As heat is absorbed, ice particles gain kinetic energy and vibrate more vigorously. The rigid lattice breaks down; particles become free to slide past each other. The solid ice (fixed shape, close-packed) becomes liquid water (takes container shape, slightly more spaced).
  2. Answer 2: In a gas, particles are far apart with large empty spaces between them. Applying pressure pushes these particles closer together, reducing volume. In a solid, particles are already tightly packed in a fixed arrangement, leaving almost no empty space, so compression is negligible.
  3. Answer 3: When the soup is heated, water molecules at the surface gain enough kinetic energy to overcome intermolecular forces and escape into the air as vapour. This continuous loss of water molecules reduces the total volume of the soup. The process is faster because the pot is uncovered, allowing vapour to leave freely.
