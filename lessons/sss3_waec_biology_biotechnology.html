Biotechnology: Principles & Applications (SSS3 Biology)

Biotechnology is the use of living organisms or their products to modify human health and the human environment. It combines biology with technology to develop products and processes that improve life.

Key Concepts in Biotechnology

  • Genetic Engineering: Direct manipulation of an organism's DNA using biotechnology techniques (e.g., recombinant DNA technology).
  • Fermentation: Use of microorganisms (bacteria, yeast) to produce useful products like alcohol, bread, or yoghurt.
  • Bioremediation: Use of microbes to clean up pollutants and toxins from the environment (e.g., oil spills).
  • Cloning: Producing genetically identical copies of organisms or cells.
  • Stem Cell Technology: Using undifferentiated cells to repair or replace damaged tissues.

Nigerian Examples of Biotechnology

1. Cassava Mosaic Disease Resistance
Nigerian scientists at the National Root Crops Research Institute (NRCRI) Umudike have used genetic engineering to develop cassava varieties resistant to the Cassava Mosaic Virus (CMV). This has helped secure food security for millions of farmers who depend on cassava as a staple crop.
2. Local Production of Insulin from Bacteria
In collaboration with international biotech firms, Nigeria has begun pilot projects to produce recombinant human insulin using genetically modified E. coli bacteria. This reduces dependence on imported insulin and lowers costs for diabetic patients across the country.
🧬 Fun Fact: The first genetically modified organism (GMO) approved for human consumption was the Flavr Savr tomato in 1994. It was engineered to ripen more slowly, allowing it to stay fresh longer. Today, biotechnology helps produce enzymes used in Nigerian palm oil processing to improve yield!

Practice Questions

  1. Define biotechnology and state two of its applications in Nigerian agriculture.
  2. Explain how genetic engineering is used to produce human insulin. Name the organism commonly used.
  3. What is bioremediation? Give one example of how it could be applied in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria.
Answers:
1. Biotechnology is the use of living organisms to develop products for human benefit. In Nigeria, it is used to develop virus-resistant cassava and to improve palm oil processing through enzyme technology.

2. The human insulin gene is inserted into the DNA of E. coli bacteria. The bacteria then multiply and produce human insulin, which is harvested and purified for medical use. The organism used is Escherichia coli.

3. Bioremediation is the use of microorganisms to break down environmental pollutants. In the Niger Delta, oil-degrading bacteria could be introduced to clean up crude oil spills in creeks and mangrove swamps.
