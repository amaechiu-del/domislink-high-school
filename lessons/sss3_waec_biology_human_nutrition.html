Human Nutrition – SSS3 Biology (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts in Human Nutrition

  • Nutrition is the process by which the body takes in, digests, absorbs, and uses food for growth, energy, and repair.
  • Classes of food include carbohydrates (e.g., yam, rice), proteins (e.g., beans, fish), fats/oils (e.g., palm oil, groundnut), vitamins, minerals, and water.
  • Balanced diet contains all essential nutrients in correct proportions to maintain health.
  • Digestion begins in the mouth (mechanical and chemical), continues in the stomach (with gastric juices), and finishes in the small intestine (with bile and pancreatic enzymes).
  • Absorption of nutrients occurs mainly in the small intestine through villi into the bloodstream.
  • Assimilation is the use of absorbed nutrients by body cells for metabolism.
  • Egestion removes undigested waste via the large intestine and rectum.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 – Carbohydrate-rich meal: A plate of pounded yam with egusi soup provides energy (carbohydrates from yam) and proteins/fats from the melon seeds and palm oil in the soup. This combination supports both immediate energy needs and tissue repair.

Example 2 – Protein source: Groundnut (peanut) soup or kilishi (spiced dried beef) are common Nigerian protein-rich foods. They help build muscles and are often eaten with tuwo shinkafa (rice meal) for a balanced diet.

🎉 Fun Fact!

Did you know? The small intestine is about 6–7 metres long in adults — that’s longer than a car! Its large surface area (thanks to tiny finger-like projections called villi) helps absorb nutrients efficiently. In Nigeria, a traditional meal like eba and okro soup is digested in just a few hours, but the small intestine works hard to extract every bit of goodness!

📝 Practice Questions (WAEC-Style)

  1. Question 1: Name the part of the digestive system where most absorption of nutrients takes place.
    Answer: The small intestine (specifically the jejunum and ileum).
  2. Question 2: A student eats a meal of boiled yam and palm oil. Which two classes of food are mainly provided?
    Answer: Carbohydrates (from yam) and fats/oils (from palm oil).
  3. Question 3: What is the difference between digestion and assimilation?
    Answer: Digestion is the breakdown of food into smaller molecules (e.g., starch to glucose), while assimilation is the use of those absorbed molecules by body cells for energy, growth, or repair.
