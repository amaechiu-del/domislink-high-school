Reproductive Health

Topic: Reproductive Health – WAEC Biology (SSS3)

Key Concepts

  • Reproductive Health: A state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being in all matters relating to the reproductive system.
  • Family Planning: The ability of individuals and couples to decide freely the number and spacing of their children.
  • Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs): Infections spread through sexual contact, e.g., HIV, gonorrhea, syphilis.
  • Contraception: Methods used to prevent pregnancy, including condoms, pills, and intrauterine devices (IUDs).
  • Safe Motherhood: Ensuring women receive proper prenatal, delivery, and postnatal care to reduce maternal and infant mortality.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In rural northern Nigeria, community health workers use mobile clinics to provide family planning education and contraceptives, helping reduce high maternal mortality rates.

Example 2: The Nigerian government’s “Every Newborn Action Plan” focuses on improving reproductive health services in states like Kano and Lagos, training midwives and supplying essential medicines.

🎉 Fun Fact: Did you know? Nigeria has one of the highest rates of twin births in the world, especially among the Yoruba ethnic group, due to genetic factors and dietary intake of yams containing phytoestrogens.

Practice Questions

  1. Question: What is the primary purpose of family planning?
    Answer: To allow individuals and couples to decide the number and spacing of their children freely, promoting maternal and child health.
  2. Question: Name two sexually transmitted infections (STIs) common in Nigeria.
    Answer: HIV and gonorrhea (or syphilis, chlamydia).
  3. Question: Why is safe motherhood important in reproductive health?
    Answer: It reduces maternal and infant mortality by ensuring proper medical care during pregnancy, childbirth, and after delivery.
