Carboxylic Acids (WAEC SSS3 Chemistry)

  • General formula: R–COOH, where R is an alkyl or aryl group.
  • Functional group: The carboxyl group (–COOH) consists of a carbonyl (C=O) and a hydroxyl (–OH) on the same carbon.
  • Acidity: Carboxylic acids are weak acids that donate a proton (H⁺) from the –OH group.
  • Nomenclature: Replace the final "-e" of the parent alkane with "-oic acid" (e.g., ethanoic acid from ethane).
  • Physical properties: Higher boiling points than corresponding alcohols due to hydrogen bonding; soluble in water up to 4–5 carbons.
  • Key reactions: Esterification with alcohols, neutralization with bases, reduction to primary alcohols, and formation of acyl chlorides.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Palm Wine Souring: Fresh palm wine contains glucose. On exposure to air, bacteria oxidize ethanol to ethanoic acid (acetic acid), giving the sour taste.

2. Locust Bean (Ogiri) Fermentation: During fermentation of locust beans, butanoic acid (butyric acid) is produced, contributing to the characteristic pungent aroma of ogiri.

🎉 Fun Fact

The sting of red ants (common in Nigerian savannahs) contains methanoic acid (formic acid). That's why the bite feels like a sharp burn!

Practice Questions

  1. Q1: What is the functional group present in all carboxylic acids?
    Answer: The carboxyl group (–COOH).
  2. Q2: Write the structural formula of propanoic acid.
    Answer: CH₃–CH₂–COOH (or C₂H₅COOH).
  3. Q3: Name the product formed when ethanoic acid reacts with ethanol in the presence of concentrated H₂SO₄.
    Answer: Ethyl ethanoate (an ester) and water.
