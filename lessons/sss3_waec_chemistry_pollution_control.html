Pollution Control in Chemistry

SSS3 WAEC Chemistry Syllabus

Key Concepts in Pollution Control

  • Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances (pollutants) into the environment, causing damage to living organisms and ecosystems.
  • Pollution control involves methods to reduce or eliminate the release of pollutants, often using chemical, physical, or biological processes.
  • Scrubbers (as shown in the diagram) remove acidic gases like SO₂ from industrial exhaust using alkaline solutions (e.g., lime or NaOH).
  • Catalytic converters in vehicles convert toxic gases (CO, NOₓ, hydrocarbons) into less harmful substances (CO₂, N₂, H₂O).
  • Bioremediation uses microorganisms to break down pollutants, e.g., oil spills or heavy metals in soil.
  • Prevention is better than cure: using cleaner fuels, recycling, and proper waste disposal are key strategies.

Nigerian Examples

1. Oil Spill Cleanup in the Niger Delta: Chemical dispersants and bioremediation techniques are used to break down crude oil spills. For instance, the use of Pseudomonas bacteria to degrade hydrocarbons in affected soil and water.

2. Industrial Scrubbers in Lagos: Many factories in Lagos (e.g., cement plants and refineries) install wet scrubbers to remove particulate matter and sulfur dioxide from exhaust gases, reducing air pollution in densely populated areas.

⚡ Fun Fact: The Lagos State Environmental Protection Agency (LASEPA) once used a "pollution clock" that counts the number of days the air quality is hazardous. In 2023, they introduced a citizen science program where students use simple chemical test kits to measure pH of rainwater – detecting acid rain from industrial emissions!

Practice Questions (WAEC-Style)

  1. Question 1: A factory releases sulfur dioxide into the air. Which of the following is the most effective chemical method to remove it before release?
    A. Adding calcium carbonate to the exhaust
    B. Passing the gas through water
    C. Using a catalytic converter
    D. Burning the gas
  2. Question 2: Explain why the use of leaded petrol is banned in Nigeria as a pollution control measure. Give one chemical reason.
  3. Question 3: In the Niger Delta, oil-degrading bacteria are introduced to polluted water. What type of pollution control is this?
    A. Physical control
    B. Chemical control
    C. Bioremediation
    D. Mechanical control

Answers

Answer 1: A (Adding calcium carbonate – it reacts with SO₂ to form calcium sulfite, a solid that can be removed).

Answer 2: Leaded petrol contains tetraethyl lead, which when burned releases lead oxide – a toxic heavy metal that damages the brain and nervous system. It also poisons catalytic converters. The ban reduces air and soil pollution.

Answer 3: C (Bioremediation – using living organisms to break down pollutants).
