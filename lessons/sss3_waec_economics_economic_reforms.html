Economic Reforms

SSS3 Economics – WAEC Syllabus

Key Concepts

  • Economic Reforms – deliberate changes to government policies, laws, or institutions to improve economic performance.
  • Deregulation – removal of government controls over industries to increase competition and efficiency.
  • Privatisation – transfer of state-owned enterprises to private ownership to enhance productivity.
  • Liberalisation – opening up the economy to foreign trade and investment by reducing tariffs and quotas.
  • Fiscal Policy Reforms – changes in government spending and taxation to stabilise or stimulate the economy.
  • Monetary Policy Reforms – adjustments in interest rates, money supply, or exchange rate regimes (e.g., floating the naira).
  • Structural Adjustment Programmes (SAP) – comprehensive reforms often required by international lenders like the IMF.

Nigerian Examples

🇳🇬 Example 1: Privatisation of NITEL (2006)
The Nigerian Telecommunications Limited (NITEL) was sold to private investors to improve telecom services. While the process faced challenges, it paved the way for the mobile revolution (e.g., MTN, Glo) that transformed Nigeria's connectivity.

🇳🇬 Example 2: Fuel Subsidy Removal (2012 & 2023)
The government removed fuel subsidies to reduce fiscal deficits and redirect funds to infrastructure. Though initially controversial, it aimed to liberalise the downstream oil sector and attract private investment.

💡 Fun Fact: Nigeria's Economic Stabilisation Act of 2020 introduced the "Naira-for-Dollar" scheme to boost local production. It was part of post-COVID reforms to reduce import dependency and strengthen the naira.

Practice Questions

  1. Define economic reforms and list any three types of reforms commonly implemented in developing countries.
  2. Explain two reasons why the Nigerian government privatised state-owned enterprises like NITEL and the Power Holding Company of Nigeria (PHCN).
  3. Discuss the impact of fuel subsidy removal on Nigeria's fiscal balance and the welfare of citizens.

Answers

Answer 1: Economic reforms are deliberate policy changes to improve economic efficiency and growth. Types: deregulation, privatisation, liberalisation, fiscal reforms, monetary reforms.

Answer 2: (i) To reduce government expenditure on inefficient enterprises and redirect funds to essential services. (ii) To improve service delivery and attract private capital and expertise.

Answer 3: Positive impact: reduced fiscal deficit, freeing funds for infrastructure. Negative impact: increased transport costs and inflation, reduced disposable income for the poor. Long-term aim: encourage local refining and reduce import dependence.
