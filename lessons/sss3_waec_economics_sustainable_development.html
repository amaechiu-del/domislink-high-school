Sustainable Development in Economics (SSS3 WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Definition: Economic development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
  • Three Pillars:
    • Economic Sustainability – Profitable growth that provides jobs and income.
    • Social Sustainability – Fair access to resources, education, and healthcare for all.
    • Environmental Sustainability – Protecting natural resources and reducing pollution.
  • Indicators: GDP per capita, Human Development Index (HDI), carbon footprint, renewable energy use.
  • Importance: Prevents resource depletion, reduces inequality, ensures long-term economic stability.
  • Challenges: Short-term profit motives, poverty, lack of technology, corruption.

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Lagos Waste-to-Energy Project

The Lagos State Government partnered with private firms to convert organic waste from markets (e.g., Oshodi) into electricity. This reduces landfill pollution, provides clean energy, and creates jobs for waste collectors.

Example 2: National Social Investment Programme (NSIP)

Nigeria's NSIP includes the Conditional Cash Transfer (CCT) scheme and N-Power programme. These reduce poverty (social sustainability) while improving human capital for future economic growth.

💡 Fun Fact

Did you know? Nigeria loses about 400,000 hectares of forest each year due to deforestation. Sustainable development practices like reforestation and agroforestry can help restore this loss while boosting rural incomes!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Define sustainable development and state its three main pillars.
  2. Question 2: Explain how the Lagos Waste-to-Energy project contributes to environmental sustainability.
  3. Question 3: Why is social sustainability important for long-term economic growth in Nigeria?

Answers

  1. Answer 1: Sustainable development is development that meets present needs without harming future generations. Its three pillars are: economic sustainability (profit), social sustainability (people), and environmental sustainability (planet).
  2. Answer 2: The project reduces methane emissions from decomposing waste (which causes air pollution and climate change), generates clean electricity, and decreases reliance on fossil fuels. It also promotes recycling and reduces landfill usage.
  3. Answer 3: Social sustainability ensures access to education, healthcare, and equal opportunities. When people are healthy and educated, they are more productive, earn higher incomes, and contribute more to the economy. It also reduces social unrest, creating a stable environment for investment.
