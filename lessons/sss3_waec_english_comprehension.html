Comprehension (WAEC Syllabus – SSS3)

Comprehension is the ability to read, understand, and interpret a passage, then answer questions based on it. This lesson covers key skills: identifying main ideas, making inferences, understanding vocabulary in context, and recognizing the writer’s tone.

  • Main Idea: What the entire passage is about – usually found in the first or last paragraph.
  • Inference: “Reading between the lines” – using clues in the text to deduce what is not directly stated.
  • Vocabulary in Context: Look at surrounding words to guess the meaning of an unfamiliar word.
  • Writer’s Tone: The attitude (e.g., humorous, angry, formal, persuasive) – shown through word choice.
  • Purpose: Why the writer wrote the passage – to inform, entertain, persuade, or describe.

🇳🇬 Example 1: “The Harmattan Season”

“Every December, the harmattan wind sweeps across northern Nigeria, carrying fine dust that blurs the sunrise. Farmers prepare their fields, knowing the dry spell will soon give way to rain.”

Question: What does “blurs the sunrise” suggest?
Answer: The dust reduces visibility, making the sun look hazy.

🇳🇬 Example 2: “The Lagos Traffic”

“Danfo drivers navigate the chaotic streets with skill, their horns honking a familiar rhythm. Commuters learn patience or risk frustration.”

Inference: The writer’s tone is neutral/informative. The purpose is to describe the reality of Lagos traffic.

🎉 Fun Fact!

Did you know? In WAEC comprehension passages, the first question is often about the main idea. Skimming the first and last paragraphs can save you time!

📝 Practice Questions

Passage: “The baobab tree, often called the ‘tree of life,’ stores water in its massive trunk. During droughts, villagers in northern Nigeria tap this reservoir. Its fruit, ‘kuka,’ is used in soups and medicines.”

  1. Q1: Why is the baobab called the “tree of life”?
    Answer: Because it provides water, food (kuka), and medicine.
  2. Q2: What does “tap this reservoir” mean?
    Answer: To draw or collect water stored inside the trunk.
  3. Q3: What is the writer’s purpose?
    Answer: To inform the reader about the uses of the baobab tree.
