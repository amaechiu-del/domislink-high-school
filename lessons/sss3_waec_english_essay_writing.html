Essay Writing for SSS3 (WAEC Syllabus)

Master the art of structuring and expressing ideas effectively in the WAEC English exam.

Key Elements of a WAEC Essay

  • Introduction (1 paragraph): Hook the reader, state your thesis, and preview main points.
  • Body (3-4 paragraphs): Each paragraph covers one main idea with evidence, examples, and explanations.
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph): Summarize key points, restate thesis in new words, and leave a lasting impression.
  • Language: Use correct grammar, varied vocabulary, and appropriate tone (formal for argumentative/expository, descriptive for narratives).
  • Structure: Clear topic sentences, logical transitions (e.g., "Furthermore," "In contrast," "Therefore").

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Argumentative Essay
Topic: "Should the Nigerian government invest more in agriculture than oil?"
Introduction: "Nigeria, once the giant of Africa in agriculture, now relies heavily on oil. This essay argues that investing in agriculture is more sustainable for our future."
Body: Paragraph 2 discusses job creation (e.g., cassava farming in Ogun State), Paragraph 3 covers food security (reducing import bills), Paragraph 4 highlights export potential (cocoa in Ondo).
Conclusion: "Diversifying into agriculture will not only stabilize our economy but also empower rural communities."

Example 2: Descriptive Essay
Topic: "Describe a memorable festival in your community."
Introduction: "The Argungu Fishing Festival in Kebbi State is a vibrant spectacle that unites the North-West."
Body: Vivid details of the river, drummers, and fishermen diving with nets; the smell of fried fish and the cheers of the crowd.
Conclusion: "This festival is not just a competition—it is a celebration of our heritage and resilience."

🎉 Fun Fact: In the WAEC English exam, the essay section accounts for 50 marks out of 100! A well-structured essay with clear paragraphs and relevant examples can earn you up to 40+ marks. Also, examiners love essays that use Nigerian proverbs or local references—they show originality!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Write an argumentative essay on: "Should the use of social media be banned in Nigerian secondary schools?" Provide at least three reasons.
  2. Question 2: Describe your most memorable journey to a Nigerian city (e.g., Lagos, Abuja, or Enugu). Use sensory details.
  3. Question 3: Expository essay: "Explain the importance of the Youth Empowerment Programme in Nigeria."

Answers (Sample Outlines)

Answer 1 (Argumentative): Introduction – state your position (e.g., ban is necessary). Body – 1) Distraction from studies (e.g., students in Lagos using Instagram during lessons). 2) Cyberbullying cases (e.g., reports from Abuja schools). 3) Exposure to inappropriate content. Conclusion – balanced view: regulate, not ban completely, with parental guidance.

Answer 2 (Descriptive): Introduction – "My trip to Calabar during the Christmas carnival was unforgettable." Body – Describe the warm breeze, colorful costumes, the taste of fresh coconut, the sound of traditional music, and the crowd's energy. Conclusion – "That journey deepened my love for Nigeria's diversity."

Answer 3 (Expository): Introduction – define youth empowerment and its relevance. Body – 1) Reduces unemployment (e.g., N-Power program in Kano). 2) Builds skills (e.g., tech hubs in Yaba). 3) Promotes national development (e.g., young entrepreneurs in Anambra). Conclusion – investing in youth is investing in Nigeria's future.
