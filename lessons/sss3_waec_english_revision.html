🔁 Key Revision Strategies for WAEC English

  • Active Recall – Test yourself on comprehension passages, summary points, and grammar rules without looking at notes.
  • Spaced Repetition – Review past topics after 1 day, 1 week, and 1 month to strengthen long-term memory.
  • Past Questions Practice – Solve WAEC past questions under timed conditions to build speed and accuracy.
  • Peer Discussion – Explain concepts to classmates to identify gaps in understanding.
  • Use of Mnemonics – Create memory aids for literary devices, figures of speech, and spelling rules.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In a WAEC comprehension passage about “The New Yam Festival”, a student revises by first reading the questions, then skimming the passage for key phrases like “harvest rites” and “ancestral blessings”. This targeted approach saves time and improves accuracy.

Example 2: A student preparing for the Summary Writing section revises by rewriting a paragraph about “Traffic Congestion in Lagos” in 60 words, then comparing with a model summary to check for conciseness and clarity.

💡 Fun Fact: The WAEC English exam has been administered for over 60 years! The very first exam in 1955 had only essay and comprehension sections — no objective tests. Revision back then meant memorising entire passages by heart!

📝 Practice Questions (WAEC Style)

  1. Question 1: Identify the figure of speech in the sentence: “The wind whispered through the palm fronds.”
    Answer: Personification
  2. Question 2: In the sentence “Neither the teacher nor the students ___ satisfied with the result.”, fill the blank with the correct verb form.
    Answer: were
  3. Question 3: Which of the following is the correct spelling? (a) Occasion (b) Ocassion (c) Occassion (d) Okasion
    Answer: (a) Occasion
