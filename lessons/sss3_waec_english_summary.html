📚 Summary Writing (WAEC English Syllabus)

A summary is a shortened version of a longer text that captures only the essential ideas and main points, while omitting details, examples, and repetitions. It must be written in your own words.

🎯 Key Principles of a Good Summary

  • Read carefully – Understand the original passage fully before writing.
  • Identify the main idea – What is the author's central point?
  • Include only key supporting points – Leave out minor details, anecdotes, and statistics.
  • Use your own words – Do not copy sentences verbatim (paraphrase).
  • Be concise – Aim for about 1/3 the length of the original.
  • Maintain logical flow – Keep the original order of ideas.
  • Do not add opinions – Stick to what the author said, not your own views.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1:
Original (about the harmattan season): "In Nigeria, the harmattan season usually starts in December and ends in February. During this period, the weather becomes very dry and dusty. The harmattan winds blow from the Sahara Desert, bringing fine dust particles that reduce visibility. Many people experience dry skin and cracked lips. Farmers often find it difficult to work because of the cold mornings and dusty conditions."

✅ Summary: The harmattan season in Nigeria (December–February) brings dry, dusty winds from the Sahara, causing reduced visibility, dry skin, and challenges for farmers.

Example 2:
Original (about the Nigerian railway): "The Nigerian Railway Corporation operates train services connecting major cities such as Lagos, Ibadan, Kaduna, and Kano. The modernisation of the railway system began in 2015 with the introduction of standard gauge tracks. This has reduced travel time between Lagos and Ibadan to about two hours. However, the railway still faces challenges including inadequate funding and maintenance issues."

✅ Summary: The Nigerian Railway Corporation connects major cities; modernisation since 2015 has cut travel times, but funding and maintenance problems remain.

💡 Fun Fact

The word "summary" comes from the Latin summa, meaning "total" or "whole." In ancient Rome, a summarium was a brief overview of a longer legal document. Today, WAEC examiners award up to 5 marks for a well-written summary in the English Language paper!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Read the following passage and write a summary in one sentence:
    "Nigeria has over 250 ethnic groups, each with its own unique language and cultural practices. The three largest groups are Hausa, Igbo, and Yoruba. Despite these differences, Nigerians share a common national identity and pride in their country."
  2. Question 2: Identify the two main points in this paragraph and write them as a summary:
    "Regular exercise is important for good health. It helps to control weight, strengthens bones and muscles, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes. Additionally, exercise improves mental health by reducing anxiety and depression. Doctors recommend at least 30 minutes of moderate activity daily."
  3. Question 3: The following passage contains 50 words. Write a summary of not more than 15 words:
    "The harmattan wind in Nigeria blows from the Sahara Desert during the dry season. It carries fine dust that covers everything and makes the air hazy. People often wear warm clothing and apply cream to protect their skin from cracking."

🔑 Answers

  1. Answer 1: Nigeria has over 250 ethnic groups, including the Hausa, Igbo, and Yoruba, who share a common national pride despite their diverse cultures.
  2. Answer 2: Exercise improves physical health (weight control, stronger bones, disease prevention) and mental health (reduces anxiety and depression).
  3. Answer 3: The harmattan wind brings dust from the Sahara, causing hazy air and dry skin requiring protection.
