African Union (AU) – Government (SSS3 WAEC Syllabus)

Key Points about the African Union

  • Founded: 9 July 2002 (replacing the OAU) – Headquarters in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
  • Membership: 55 African countries, including Nigeria.
  • Aims: Promote unity, peace, democracy, and sustainable development across Africa.
  • Key Organs: Assembly of Heads of State (supreme), Executive Council, AU Commission, Peace & Security Council, Pan-African Parliament.
  • Flagship Projects: Agenda 2063 (long-term development plan), African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

1. Nigeria’s Role in AU Peacekeeping: Nigerian troops have served in AU missions in Somalia (AMISOM) and Mali, demonstrating Nigeria’s commitment to African peace and security.

2. Nigeria and AfCFTA: Nigeria ratified the African Continental Free Trade Area in 2020, boosting intra-African trade. Nigerian entrepreneurs now export goods like textiles and agricultural products with reduced tariffs across the continent.

✨ Fun Fact: The AU’s official motto is “A United and Strong Africa”. Its emblem features a gold map of Africa surrounded by 55 interlocking rings — one for each member state — and two palm leaves symbolising peace.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Which year was the African Union officially launched, and what organisation did it replace?
  2. Name two main organs of the AU and briefly state their functions.
  3. How has Nigeria contributed to the AU’s peace and security agenda? Give one example.

✅ Answers

  • 1. The AU was launched on 9 July 2002, replacing the Organisation of African Unity (OAU).
  • 2. (i) Assembly of Heads of State – supreme policy-making body. (ii) AU Commission – handles day-to-day administration and implementation of decisions.
  • 3. Nigeria has contributed troops to AU peacekeeping missions, e.g., in Somalia (AMISOM) and Mali, to help stabilise conflict zones.
