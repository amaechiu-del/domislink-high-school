ECOWAS: Economic Community of West African States

Topic: Regional Integration & International Organizations (WAEC Government Syllabus)

Key Facts about ECOWAS

  • Founded: 28 May 1975 (Treaty of Lagos). Revised Treaty signed in 1993.
  • Member States: 15 West African countries (including Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, etc.).
  • Main Objectives: Promote economic integration, free trade, free movement of people, and peace/security in the region.
  • Institutions: Authority of Heads of State, Council of Ministers, ECOWAS Commission, Community Court of Justice, and Parliament.
  • Currency: Plans to introduce a single currency (the Eco) — convergence criteria are still being met.
  • Peacekeeping: ECOMOG (ECOWAS Monitoring Group) has intervened in Liberia, Sierra Leone, Guinea-Bissau, and Mali.

Nigerian Examples

  1. Hosting the ECOWAS Headquarters: The ECOWAS Commission is based in Abuja, Nigeria. This gives Nigeria significant influence over regional policy and logistics.
  2. ECOMOG Peacekeeping Leadership: Nigeria contributed the largest troop contingent to ECOMOG during the Liberian Civil War (1990s) and led efforts to restore stability in Sierra Leone.

🌟 Fun Fact

Did you know? ECOWAS citizens can travel visa-free within member states for up to 90 days using only a passport or ECOWAS biometric ID card! Nigeria and Ghana are among the busiest travel corridors under this scheme.

Practice Questions (WAEC-Style)

  1. Q1: State three objectives of the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) as outlined in the 1975 Treaty of Lagos.
  2. Q2: Explain two ways in which Nigeria has benefited from its membership in ECOWAS.
  3. Q3: Identify two challenges facing the implementation of the ECOWAS single currency (the Eco).
📘 Click for Answers

A1: (i) Promote economic integration and cooperation among member states. (ii) Establish a common market for free movement of goods, services, capital, and people. (iii) Harmonize economic and industrial policies for regional development.

A2: (i) Nigeria hosts the ECOWAS headquarters in Abuja, enhancing its political influence and attracting international attention. (ii) Nigerian businesses and traders benefit from the ECOWAS Trade Liberalisation Scheme (ETLS), which allows duty-free movement of certain goods across the region.

A3: (i) Non-convergence of macroeconomic indicators (e.g., inflation, budget deficit) among member states. (ii) Dominance of the US dollar and CFA franc in some economies, making currency unification politically and economically complex.
