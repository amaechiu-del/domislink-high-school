United Nations (UN) – SSS3 Government (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Facts About the United Nations

  • Founded: 24 October 1945, after World War II, to promote peace and cooperation.
  • Headquarters: New York City, USA.
  • Membership: 193 member states (as of 2025).
  • Main Organs: General Assembly, Security Council, Secretariat, International Court of Justice, Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC), Trusteeship Council (inactive).
  • Core Purposes: Maintain international peace and security; promote human rights; foster social and economic development; protect the environment; provide humanitarian aid.
  • Security Council: 5 permanent members (USA, UK, France, Russia, China) with veto power; 10 non-permanent members elected for 2-year terms.
  • Secretary-General: Chief administrative officer (currently António Guterres, as of 2025).

Nigerian Examples

  • Peacekeeping Contributions: Nigeria has been one of the largest troop contributors to UN peacekeeping missions, including in Sierra Leone (UNAMSIL), Liberia (UNMIL), and Sudan (UNMIS). Nigerian soldiers have helped restore stability in conflict zones.
  • UN Security Council Seat: Nigeria has served several times as a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council (e.g., 2014–2015, 2020–2021), advocating for African interests and global security reforms.
🌟 Fun Fact: The UN's first peacekeeping mission was in 1948, and it was sent to monitor the ceasefire between Israel and its Arab neighbors. Nigeria joined the UN on October 7, 1960, just days after gaining independence!

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: Name any two of the five permanent members of the UN Security Council.
  2. Question 2: State two ways Nigeria has participated in UN activities.
  3. Question 3: What is the main function of the UN General Assembly?

Answers

  • Answer 1: United States, United Kingdom, France, Russia, or China (any two).
  • Answer 2: (i) Contributing troops to UN peacekeeping missions (e.g., in Sierra Leone, Liberia). (ii) Serving as a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council (e.g., 2014–2015, 2020–2021).
  • Answer 3: The General Assembly is the main deliberative body where all member states discuss and make recommendations on international issues, such as peace, security, and development.
