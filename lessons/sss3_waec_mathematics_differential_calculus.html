📐 Differential Calculus – The Mathematics of Change

Differential calculus studies how quantities change. The derivative measures the instantaneous rate of change — the slope of a curve at a single point.

🔑 Key Concepts

  • Derivative: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\) — the slope of the tangent line.
  • Power Rule: If \(y = x^n\), then \(\frac{dy}{dx} = n x^{n-1}\).
  • Constant Rule: Derivative of a constant is 0.
  • Sum/Difference Rule: Derivative of sum = sum of derivatives.
  • Notation: \(f'(x)\), \(\frac{dy}{dx}\), \(y'\) all mean derivative.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1 (WAEC Style): A particle moves along a line such that its displacement \(s(t) = 3t^2 - 6t + 4\) metres after \(t\) seconds. Find its velocity at \(t = 2\) seconds.

Solution: Velocity \(v(t) = s'(t) = 6t - 6\). At \(t=2\): \(v(2) = 6(2)-6 = 6\) m/s.
Example 2 (Everyday Context): The cost (in ₦) of producing \(x\) bags of garri is \(C(x) = 500 + 200x - 0.5x^2\). Find the marginal cost when 50 bags are produced.

Solution: Marginal cost = \(C'(x) = 200 - x\). At \(x=50\): \(C'(50) = 200 - 50 = ₦150\) per bag.
💡 Fun Fact: The word "calculus" comes from the Latin word for "small stone" — ancient Romans used pebbles for counting! Sir Isaac Newton called derivatives "fluxions" because they measure how quantities flow.

✏️ Practice Questions

  1. Differentiate \(y = 4x^3 - 7x^2 + 2x - 9\).
  2. If \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2}\), find \(f'(x)\).
  3. The height of a projectile is \(h(t) = 40t - 5t^2\) metres. Find the time when its velocity is zero.
✅ Answers:
1. \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 12x^2 - 14x + 2\)
2. \(f'(x) = -\frac{2}{x^3}\) (rewrite as \(x^{-2}\), then power rule)
3. \(v(t)=40-10t=0 \Rightarrow t=4\) seconds
