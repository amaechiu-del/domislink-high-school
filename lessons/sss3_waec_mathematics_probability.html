Probability (SSS3 Mathematics - WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Probability measures how likely an event is to occur, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
  • Formula: P(Event) = (Number of favourable outcomes) / (Total number of possible outcomes).
  • Sample Space (S) is the set of all possible outcomes. Example: For a die, S = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}.
  • Event (E) is a subset of the sample space. Example: Getting an even number = {2, 4, 6}.
  • Mutually Exclusive Events cannot happen at the same time. P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B).
  • Complementary Events: P(not E) = 1 - P(E).

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: In a bag, there are 5 red balls, 3 green balls, and 2 blue balls. If a ball is picked at random, what is the probability of picking a green ball?

Solution: Total balls = 5 + 3 + 2 = 10. Favourable (green) = 3. So P(green) = 3/10 = 0.3.

Example 2: In a WAEC examination, 60% of students passed Mathematics. If a student is selected at random, what is the probability that the student failed Mathematics?

Solution: P(pass) = 0.6. P(fail) = 1 - 0.6 = 0.4.

💡 Fun Fact: The word "probability" comes from the Latin probabilitas, meaning "to prove". In the 17th century, French mathematicians Blaise Pascal and Pierre de Fermat laid the foundation of probability theory while solving a gambling problem!

📝 Practice Questions

  1. A fair coin is tossed twice. What is the probability of getting at least one head?
    Answer: 3/4 (Sample space: HH, HT, TH, TT; favourable: HH, HT, TH).
  2. A bag contains 4 white, 5 black, and 1 red marble. If one marble is drawn at random, find the probability that it is not black.
    Answer: 5/10 = 1/2 (Total = 10; not black = white + red = 5).
  3. In a class of 40 students, 25 are girls. If a student is chosen at random, what is the probability that the student is a boy?
    Answer: 15/40 = 3/8 (Boys = 40 - 25 = 15).
