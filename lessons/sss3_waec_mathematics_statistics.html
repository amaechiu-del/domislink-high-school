Statistics: Measures of Central Tendency & Dispersion

SSS3 Mathematics (WAEC Syllabus)

Key Concepts

  • Mean – The average of all data points. Formula: \(\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n}\)
  • Median – The middle value when data is arranged in order.
  • Mode – The most frequently occurring value.
  • Range – Difference between highest and lowest values.
  • Variance & Standard Deviation – Measure how spread out the data is from the mean.

🇳🇬 Example 1: JAMB Scores

Five students scored: 45, 60, 75, 60, 80 in JAMB Mathematics.

Mean: (45+60+75+60+80)/5 = 320/5 = 64

Mode: 60 (appears twice)

Median: Arrange: 45, 60, 60, 75, 80 → Middle = 60

Range: 80 - 45 = 35

🇳🇬 Example 2: Monthly Market Sales (₦)

A trader in Lagos recorded weekly sales: 20,000; 25,000; 20,000; 30,000; 25,000

Mean: (20+25+20+30+25) thousand / 5 = 120,000/5 = ₦24,000

Mode: ₦20,000 and ₦25,000 (bimodal)

Median: 20, 20, 25, 25, 30 → ₦25,000

🎉 Fun Fact: Nigeria's National Bureau of Statistics uses these same measures to calculate inflation rates and unemployment figures! The mean is often used to report average wages, but the median is more accurate when there are very high or low outliers.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: The ages of 7 students are: 14, 16, 14, 17, 15, 14, 18. Find the mean, median, and mode.
  2. Question 2: A farmer in Kaduna harvested yam tubers (kg): 12, 15, 10, 12, 20, 15, 14. Calculate the range and the mean.
  3. Question 3: The scores of 10 students in a WAEC mock exam are: 55, 60, 65, 70, 75, 80, 85, 90, 95, 100. What is the median? What is the mean?

✅ Answers

  1. Answer 1: Mean = 108/7 ≈ 15.43; Median = 15; Mode = 14
  2. Answer 2: Range = 20 - 10 = 10 kg; Mean = 98/7 = 14 kg
  3. Answer 3: Median = (75+80)/2 = 77.5; Mean = 775/10 = 77.5
