AC Circuits (Physics - SSS3 WAEC Syllabus)

  • Alternating Current (AC) flows in a circuit with periodic reversal of direction, unlike Direct Current (DC).
  • The voltage and current vary sinusoidally with time: V(t) = V₀ sin(ωt) and I(t) = I₀ sin(ωt ± φ).
  • Phase difference (φ) occurs when the circuit contains reactive components (inductors or capacitors). For a pure resistor, φ = 0°.
  • Inductive reactance (XL = 2πfL) causes current to lag voltage by 90°; capacitive reactance (XC = 1/(2πfC)) causes current to lead voltage by 90°.
  • Impedance (Z) is the total opposition to AC flow: Z = √(R² + (XL - XC)²).
  • RMS values are used for power calculations: Vrms = V₀/√2, Irms = I₀/√2.

🇳🇬 Nigerian Examples

Example 1: A radio transmitter in Lagos uses an inductor of 2.5 mH and a capacitor of 100 µF in its tuning circuit. Calculate the resonant frequency (f₀) of the circuit. (Use f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC)))

Solution: L = 2.5 × 10⁻³ H, C = 100 × 10⁻⁶ F → √(LC) = √(2.5e-3 × 100e-6) = √(2.5e-7) = 5.0 × 10⁻⁴. f₀ = 1/(2π × 5.0e-4) ≈ 318.3 Hz.

Example 2: A household fan in Port Harcourt has a coil with resistance 20 Ω and inductive reactance 15 Ω. What is the impedance of the fan?

Solution: Z = √(R² + XL²) = √(20² + 15²) = √(400 + 225) = √625 = 25 Ω.

💡 Fun Fact: Nigeria's national grid operates at 50 Hz (frequency). This means the voltage changes direction 100 times every second! That's why your bulb flickers 100 times per second — too fast for your eyes to notice.

📝 Practice Questions

  1. An AC voltage source of 240 V (rms) is connected to a 60 Ω resistor. Calculate the peak current (I₀) through the resistor.
  2. A capacitor of 50 µF is connected to a 50 Hz AC supply. Determine its capacitive reactance (XC). (Take π = 3.14)
  3. In an R-L series circuit, R = 30 Ω and XL = 40 Ω. If the rms current is 2 A, find the rms voltage across the circuit.

Answers:

1. V₀ = 240 × √2 ≈ 339.4 V → I₀ = V₀ / R = 339.4 / 60 ≈ 5.66 A

2. XC = 1/(2πfC) = 1/(2 × 3.14 × 50 × 50 × 10⁻⁶) = 1/(0.0157) ≈ 63.7 Ω

3. Z = √(30² + 40²) = 50 Ω → Vrms = Irms × Z = 2 × 50 = 100 V
