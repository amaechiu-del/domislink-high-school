Quantum Physics – SSS3 WAEC Physics

k = hν – φ

Key Concepts

  • Quantum Theory: Energy is not continuous but exists in discrete packets called quanta (photons for light).
  • Photoelectric Effect: When light of sufficient frequency hits a metal surface, electrons are ejected.
  • Threshold Frequency (f₀): Minimum frequency needed to eject an electron; below this, no emission occurs regardless of intensity.
  • Work Function (φ): Minimum energy required to remove an electron from the metal surface (φ = hf₀).
  • Einstein's Equation: Kinetic energy of ejected electron = hf – φ, where h = Planck’s constant (6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ Js).
  • Wave-Particle Duality: Light behaves as both a wave and a particle (photon).

Nigerian Examples

Example 1: Solar Panels in Northern Nigeria
In states like Kano and Sokoto, solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using the photoelectric effect. Photons from the sun knock electrons free in silicon cells, generating current. The high sunlight intensity in Nigeria makes this ideal for rural electrification.

Example 2: Security Lights in Lagos
Automatic streetlights in Lagos use photoelectric sensors. When light intensity falls (at dusk), the sensor stops receiving photons, reducing electron flow and switching the light on. This quantum-based switch saves energy across the city.

🌟 Fun Fact

Albert Einstein won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for explaining the photoelectric effect, not for his theory of relativity! His work proved that light consists of particles (photons) and helped launch quantum mechanics.

Practice Questions

  1. Question 1: The work function of a metal is 2.0 eV. If light of frequency 6.0 × 10¹⁴ Hz is incident on it, will electrons be ejected? (h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ Js, 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J)

    Answer

    Energy of photon = hf = 6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 6.0×10¹⁴ = 3.978×10⁻¹⁹ J = 2.49 eV. Since 2.49 eV > 2.0 eV, electrons will be ejected.

  2. Question 2: What is the threshold frequency of a metal with a work function of 3.2 × 10⁻¹⁹ J? (h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ Js)

    Answer

    f₀ = φ / h = (3.2×10⁻¹⁹) / (6.63×10⁻³⁴) = 4.83 × 10¹⁴ Hz.

  3. Question 3: Explain why increasing the intensity of red light (low frequency) does not eject electrons from a metal, while even dim violet light (high frequency) does.

    Answer

    Intensity increases the number of photons, but each photon's energy depends on frequency (E = hf). Red light photons have energy below the work function, so no single photon can eject an electron. Violet photons have higher energy per photon, sufficient to overcome the work function even at low intensity.
